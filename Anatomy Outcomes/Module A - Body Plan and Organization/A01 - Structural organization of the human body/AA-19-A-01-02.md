# Examples of Each Level of Organization in the Human Body

This document lists at least three examples for each of the six levels of organization in the human body, moving from the simplest level to the most complex.

## Chemical Level

The chemical level consists of atoms and the molecules they form, which serve as the building blocks of all body structures.

- Water (H₂O) is the most abundant molecule in the body and provides the medium in which most chemical reactions occur.
- DNA is the molecule that stores the genetic information needed to build and operate the body.
- Glucose is a simple sugar that the body breaks down to release energy.
- Proteins such as hemoglobin and collagen carry out structural and functional roles throughout the body.

## Cellular Level

The cellular level consists of cells, which are the smallest units that can be considered alive.

- Red blood cells carry oxygen from the lungs to the rest of the body.
- Neurons transmit electrical signals that allow the body to sense and respond to its environment.
- Muscle cells, also called myocytes, contract to produce movement.
- White blood cells defend the body against infection and foreign invaders.

## Tissue Level

The tissue level consists of groups of similar cells working together, and it is traditionally divided into four primary types.

- Epithelial tissue covers and lines body surfaces, such as the skin and the lining of the gut.
- Connective tissue supports and binds other tissues, and it includes bone, blood, and cartilage.
- Muscle tissue produces movement and comes in cardiac, skeletal, and smooth varieties.
- Nervous tissue transmits signals and forms the brain, spinal cord, and nerves.

## Organ Level

The organ level consists of structures built from two or more tissue types that perform specific functions.

- The heart pumps blood throughout the body.
- The lungs exchange oxygen and carbon dioxide with the air.
- The liver processes nutrients and filters toxins from the blood.
- The brain coordinates the activity of the nervous system.

## Organ System Level

The organ system level consists of multiple organs cooperating toward a shared purpose, and there are eleven such systems in all.

- The cardiovascular system circulates blood to deliver oxygen and nutrients and to remove wastes.
- The respiratory system handles breathing and the exchange of gases between the body and the air.
- The digestive system breaks down food and absorbs the nutrients the body needs.
- The nervous system senses changes and coordinates the body's responses to them.

## Organismal Level

The organismal level consists of the complete living individual, so its examples are whole organisms rather than separate parts.

- Any individual human being qualifies as an example, since the level represents the body as a single integrated whole.
- Because this level captures the entire person, examples are simply different individuals, such as you, a patient, or any other complete human.
- In each such individual, all eleven organ systems function together to maintain life and homeostasis.
