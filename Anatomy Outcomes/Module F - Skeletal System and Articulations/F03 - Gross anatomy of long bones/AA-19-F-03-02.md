## Describe the bone components responsible for either lengthwise or circumferential (width) bone growth in long bones

# Lengthwise and Width Growth of Long Bones

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the bone components responsible for either lengthwise or circumferential (width) bone growth in long bones.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

Long bones grow in two directions, becoming longer and thicker, and each kind of growth is driven by a different component. Understanding which structure does which explains how bones lengthen in childhood and thicken throughout life. Today we describe the components responsible for lengthwise and width growth in long bones.

- By the end of this session you will be able to name the component responsible for lengthwise growth.
- You will be able to name the component responsible for width growth.
- You will be able to describe how each kind of growth occurs.
- You will be able to connect bone growth to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Two Growth Sites for Two Directions

The organising idea is that lengthwise growth happens at the growth plate near each end of the bone, while width growth happens at the periosteum around the shaft, so the two directions have separate, specialised sites. One lengthens, the other thickens. This division of growth sites frames the topic.

- Long bones grow both in length and in width.
- Lengthwise growth occurs at the growth plate, or epiphyseal plate.
- Width growth occurs at the periosteum on the outer surface.
- Each direction of growth has its own dedicated site.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Tower Raised From Within and Rendered From Outside

To keep the two growths concrete, picture a tower being raised in height by adding courses at a level near its top, while at the same time being thickened by rendering new layers onto its outer wall. The height comes from one internal site and the width from the outer surface. Long bones grow this way, and the image keeps lengthening and thickening separate.

- Adding courses near the top corresponds to lengthwise growth at the growth plate.
- Rendering new outer layers corresponds to width growth at the periosteum.
- The two happening together corresponds to bones lengthening and thickening at once.
- The analogy breaks down in that a tower is built by workers adding fixed materials, whereas bone growth is driven by living cells producing new bone tissue.

---

## Lengthwise Growth at the Growth Plate

Begin with lengthwise growth, driven by the growth plate near each end. It works by adding cartilage that becomes bone.

- The growth plate, or epiphyseal plate, is a layer of cartilage between the shaft and each end in a growing bone.
- Cartilage cells multiply on the side toward the end, pushing the end away and lengthening the bone.
- The older cartilage on the shaft side is replaced by bone, so the bone lengthens while the plate stays the same thickness.
- When growth ends, the plate is fully replaced by bone, forming the epiphyseal line, and lengthwise growth stops.

---

## Width Growth at the Periosteum

Next width growth, driven by the periosteum on the outer surface. It works by adding bone to the outside.

- The periosteum on the outer surface of the shaft adds new bone to widen the bone.
- Bone-forming cells in the periosteum lay down new bone on the outer surface.
- At the same time, cells lining the medullary cavity remove bone from the inside.
- This combination widens the bone while keeping its walls an efficient thickness.

---

## How the Two Growths Relate

Finally how the two kinds of growth relate over time, since they follow different timelines. One stops, the other continues.

- Lengthwise growth occurs during childhood and adolescence and stops when the growth plates close.
- Width growth can continue throughout life, allowing bones to thicken in response to load.
- Both processes are examples of bone being continually built and reshaped.
- Together they produce a bone that is both the right length and strong enough for its loads.

---

## Why Bone Growth Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

Understanding bone growth underlies your work with growing patients and with loading for bone strength. Each point below connects growth to practice.

- Growth plates are sites of injury in children and adolescents, and their damage can affect future growth.
- Because growth plates close at the end of adolescence, adult bones cannot lengthen, informing expectations after injury.
- Width growth in response to load is why weight-bearing exercise thickens and strengthens bone.
- The continual reshaping of bone underlies its adaptation to the loads you apply.
- Understanding growth grounds reasoning about paediatric injury and bone-strengthening exercise.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture of bone growth, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to the tower raised and rendered.

- It is a mistake to think bones lengthen at the periosteum, because lengthening occurs at the growth plate.
- It is a mistake to believe bones can lengthen after the growth plates close.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the medullary cavity is widened as the outside is thickened.
- It is a mistake to treat bone as unable to change in adulthood, because width growth and remodelling continue.

## Consolidation and Self-Check

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Naming each growth site and its process aloud is the strongest test.

- Name the component responsible for lengthwise growth from memory.
- Name the component responsible for width growth.
- Describe how the growth plate lengthens a bone.
- Describe how the periosteum widens a bone.
- Explain why adult bones cannot lengthen but can thicken.

---

## Key Takeaways

We have described how long bones grow in two directions. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- Long bones grow in length at the growth plate and in width at the periosteum.
- The growth plate adds cartilage that is replaced by bone, lengthening the bone until it closes.
- The periosteum adds bone to the outer surface while the inside is hollowed, widening the bone.
- Lengthwise growth stops in adolescence, while width growth can continue through life.
- Bone growth underlies paediatric injury and the strengthening of bone with load.
