## List and describe the different types of bone cells, with respect to location and function

# The Types of Bone Cells

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: List and describe the different types of bone cells, with respect to location and function.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

Bone is a living tissue maintained by a small team of specialised cells that build it, maintain it, and break it down. Understanding these cells explains how bone grows, heals, and remodels in response to load. Today we list and describe the types of bone cells with respect to location and function.

- By the end of this session you will be able to name the four types of bone cell.
- You will be able to state the location and function of each.
- You will be able to explain how the cells cooperate in remodelling.
- You will be able to connect the cells to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Team That Builds, Maintains, and Removes Bone

The organising idea is that bone is kept alive by a team of cells with complementary jobs, one making bone, one maintaining it, one removing it, and one held in reserve, so that bone can be continually rebuilt. Building and removing must be balanced. This idea of a coordinated building team frames the cells.

- Bone is maintained by four types of specialised cell.
- Osteogenic cells are reserve stem cells that become bone-builders.
- Osteoblasts build new bone, and osteocytes maintain it.
- Osteoclasts break bone down, balancing the building.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Building Maintenance Crew

To keep the cells concrete, picture a crew maintaining a building: apprentices in reserve who can be trained up, builders who lay new material, resident caretakers who monitor and maintain the finished structure, and a demolition team who remove old material so it can be replaced. Bone's cells are this crew, and the image keeps their complementary roles distinct.

- The reserve apprentices correspond to the osteogenic stem cells.
- The builders laying new material correspond to the osteoblasts.
- The resident caretakers correspond to the osteocytes maintaining bone.
- The demolition team correspond to the osteoclasts, though the analogy breaks down in that a maintenance crew works from plans while bone cells respond directly to mechanical and chemical signals.

---

## The Building Cells

Begin with the cells that make bone, the osteogenic cells and osteoblasts. These produce new bone.

- Osteogenic cells are unspecialised stem cells found in the periosteum and endosteum.
- They divide and develop into osteoblasts when new bone is needed.
- Osteoblasts are the bone-building cells that produce and deposit new bone matrix.
- As osteoblasts surround themselves with the matrix they make, they become osteocytes.

---

## The Maintenance Cells

Next the cells that maintain finished bone, the osteocytes. These are the most numerous.

- Osteocytes are mature bone cells trapped within the bone matrix they helped form.
- They occupy small spaces called lacunae within the bone.
- They maintain the bone matrix and sense mechanical stress on the bone.
- Their sensing of load helps signal when and where bone should be remodelled.

---

## The Removing Cells

Finally the cells that break bone down, the osteoclasts. These oppose the builders.

- Osteoclasts are large cells that break down and resorb bone matrix.
- They are found on bone surfaces undergoing remodelling.
- They release minerals from bone into the blood and remove old or damaged bone.
- Their activity is balanced against that of osteoblasts to keep bone healthy.

---

## Why Bone Cells Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The bone cells underlie remodelling, healing, and the response to load central to musculoskeletal practice. Each point below connects a cell to care.

- Osteocytes sense the load you apply, so mechanical loading signals bone to strengthen.
- The balance of osteoblasts and osteoclasts determines whether bone is gained or lost, central to osteoporosis.
- Osteoblasts drive the bone formation essential to fracture healing.
- Excess osteoclast activity underlies bone loss in disuse and disease.
- Understanding the cells grounds exercise prescription for bone health.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture of the bone cells, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to the building maintenance crew.

- It is a mistake to confuse osteoblasts and osteoclasts, because one builds bone and the other removes it.
- It is a mistake to think osteocytes are inactive, because they maintain bone and sense load.
- It is a mistake to treat bone as unchanging, because these cells continually rebuild it.
- It is a mistake to overlook osteogenic cells, the reserve that becomes bone-builders.

## Consolidation and Self-Check

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Naming each cell with its location and function aloud is the strongest test.

- Name the four types of bone cell from memory.
- State the location and function of each.
- Explain how an osteoblast becomes an osteocyte.
- Explain how osteoblasts and osteoclasts balance one another.
- Relate one bone cell to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

---

## Key Takeaways

We have listed the cells that keep bone alive. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- Bone is maintained by osteogenic cells, osteoblasts, osteocytes, and osteoclasts.
- Osteogenic cells are reserve stem cells that become osteoblasts.
- Osteoblasts build bone and become osteocytes, which maintain it and sense load.
- Osteoclasts break bone down, balancing the building.
- The balance of these cells underlies remodelling, healing, and the response to loading.
