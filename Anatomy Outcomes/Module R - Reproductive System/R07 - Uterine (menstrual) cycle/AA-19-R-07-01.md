## Name the phases of the uterine (menstrual) cycle, and describe the anatomical changes in the uterine wall that occur during each phase

# The Phases of the Uterine Cycle

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Name the phases of the uterine (menstrual) cycle, and describe the anatomical changes in the uterine wall that occur during each phase.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The lining of the uterus changes through a monthly cycle, shedding, rebuilding, and preparing to receive a pregnancy. Understanding these phases frames the menstrual cycle. Today we name the phases of the uterine cycle and describe the changes in the uterine wall during each.

- By the end of this session you will be able to name the phases of the uterine cycle.
- You will be able to describe the change in each phase.
- You will be able to explain what the changes prepare for.
- You will be able to connect the cycle to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Lining That Sheds, Rebuilds, and Prepares to Receive a Pregnancy

The organising idea is that the uterine lining goes through phases each month, shedding in the menstrual phase, rebuilding in the next phase, and preparing to receive a pregnancy in the last phase. Shed, rebuild, prepare. This idea of a rebuilding lining frames the topic.

- The uterine cycle has three phases.
- In the menstrual phase, the lining is shed.
- In the next phase, the lining rebuilds.
- In the last phase, the lining prepares to receive a pregnancy.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Room Cleared, Refurnished, and Made Ready for a Guest

To keep the phases concrete, picture a room that is cleared out, then refurnished, then stocked and warmed to be ready for a guest. The uterine lining is shed, rebuilt, and prepared to receive a pregnancy, and the image keeps the clearing, refurnishing, and readying in view.

- Clearing out the room corresponds to the shedding of the lining.
- Refurnishing corresponds to the rebuilding of the lining.
- Stocking and warming for a guest corresponds to preparing to receive a pregnancy.
- The analogy breaks down in that a room is prepared by people, whereas the lining changes are driven by hormones.

---

## The Menstrual Phase

Begin with the menstrual phase, when the lining is shed. This is the period.

- In the menstrual phase, the thickened lining is shed.
- This shedding is the menstrual period.
- It occurs because a pregnancy did not happen in the previous cycle.
- The lining is reduced to a thin base.

---

## The Rebuilding Phase

Next the rebuilding phase, when the lining regrows. It thickens again.

- After the period, the lining rebuilds.
- It thickens and its glands and vessels regrow.
- This rebuilding is driven by oestrogen from the growing follicle.
- The lining is prepared for the next phase.

---

## The Preparing Phase

Finally the preparing phase, when the lining is readied to receive a pregnancy. It becomes rich and secretory.

- After ovulation, the lining is prepared to receive a fertilised egg.
- Its glands secrete substances to nourish an early pregnancy.
- This is driven by progesterone from the hormone gland in the ovary.
- If pregnancy does not occur, the lining is shed and the cycle repeats.

---

## Why the Uterine Cycle Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The uterine cycle underlies the menstrual cycle relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The cycle's hormones affect the body throughout the month, relevant to symptoms across the cycle.
- The cycle is part of the female hormonal cycle that influences bone and tissue.
- Understanding the cycle clarifies the changes in the uterine lining.
- Menstrual symptoms are relevant to some patients you treat.
- Understanding it grounds your reasoning about the female reproductive system.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a room cleared, refurnished, and made ready for a guest.

- It is a mistake to muddle the order of the phases.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the lining rebuilds after the period.
- It is a mistake to forget that the last phase prepares the lining to receive a pregnancy.
- It is a mistake to think the changes occur without hormones.

---

## Consolidation and Self-Check

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Naming the phases and their changes aloud is the strongest test.

- Name the phases of the uterine cycle from memory.
- Describe the change in each phase.
- Explain what the changes prepare for.
- Explain which hormones drive the rebuilding and preparing phases.
- Relate the cycle to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

---

## Key Takeaways

We have described the phases of the uterine cycle. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The uterine cycle has a menstrual, a rebuilding, and a preparing phase.
- In the menstrual phase, the thickened lining is shed as the period.
- In the rebuilding phase, the lining thickens again, driven by oestrogen.
- In the preparing phase, the lining becomes rich and secretory, driven by progesterone.
- If pregnancy does not occur, the lining is shed and the cycle repeats.
