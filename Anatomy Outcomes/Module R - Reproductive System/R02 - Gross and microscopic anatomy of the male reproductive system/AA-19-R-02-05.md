## Identify and describe the structure and functions of the male external genitalia (e.g., scrotum, penis)

# The Male External Genitalia

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Identify and describe the structure and functions of the male external genitalia (e.g., scrotum, penis).

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The male external genitalia are the scrotum, which holds the testes at the right temperature, and the penis, which delivers the sperm. Understanding them frames the delivery of sperm. Today we identify and describe the structure and functions of the male external genitalia.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe the scrotum.
- You will be able to describe the penis.
- You will be able to state their functions.
- You will be able to connect the external genitalia to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Temperature-Regulating Sac and a Delivery Organ

The organising idea is that the scrotum holds the testes outside the body to keep them cooler, while the penis delivers the sperm and also carries urine. A cooling sac and a delivery organ. This idea of a cooling sac and delivery organ frames the topic.

- The scrotum is a sac that holds the testes.
- It keeps the testes slightly cooler than the body.
- The penis delivers the sperm.
- It also carries urine.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Cooling Housing and a Dispensing Nozzle

To keep the external genitalia concrete, picture a cooling housing that keeps sensitive units at the right temperature and a dispensing nozzle that delivers a product. The scrotum cools the testes and the penis delivers the sperm, and the image keeps the cooling housing and dispensing nozzle in view.

- The cooling housing corresponds to the scrotum.
- The dispensing nozzle corresponds to the penis.
- Keeping units at the right temperature corresponds to keeping the testes cooler.
- The analogy breaks down in that a housing and nozzle are machinery, whereas the external genitalia are living tissue.

---

## The Scrotum

Begin with the scrotum, which holds the testes. It regulates their temperature.

- The scrotum is a sac of skin and muscle hanging below the body.
- It holds the two testes.
- Its position keeps the testes slightly cooler than body temperature.
- Its muscle can raise or lower the testes to adjust their temperature.

---

## The Penis

Next the penis, which delivers the sperm. It contains erectile tissue.

- The penis is the organ that delivers the sperm.
- It contains columns of erectile tissue that fill with blood to make it firm.
- The urethra runs through it, carrying the sperm to the outside.
- The tip of the penis contains the external opening of the urethra.

---

## The Functions

Finally the functions, which combine cooling and delivery. The two organs serve reproduction.

- The scrotum keeps the testes at the temperature that sperm production needs.
- The penis delivers the sperm during reproduction.
- The penis also carries urine at other times.
- Together they serve the delivery of sperm and the passage of urine.

---

## Why the External Genitalia Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The external genitalia underlie reproduction and are relevant to pelvic-health practice. Each point below connects them to practice.

- The scrotal position of the testes keeps them cooler, essential to sperm production.
- The penis delivers sperm and carries urine, relevant to reproductive and urinary function.
- Understanding the external genitalia clarifies the delivery of sperm.
- Pelvic-health function is relevant to some patients you treat.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about the male reproductive system.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a cooling housing and a dispensing nozzle.

- It is a mistake to overlook that the scrotum regulates the testes' temperature.
- It is a mistake to think the penis carries only sperm, since it also carries urine.
- It is a mistake to forget that the penis contains erectile tissue.
- It is a mistake to place the testes inside the body cavity, since they lie in the scrotum.

## Consolidation and Self-Check

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Describing the structures and functions aloud is the strongest test.

- Describe the scrotum from memory.
- Describe the penis.
- State their functions.
- Explain how the scrotum regulates temperature.
- Relate the external genitalia to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

---

## Key Takeaways

We have described the male external genitalia. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The scrotum is a sac holding the testes below the body.
- Its position and muscle keep the testes slightly cooler, which sperm production needs.
- The penis contains erectile tissue that fills with blood and the urethra that carries sperm and urine.
- The penis delivers the sperm during reproduction.
- Together the scrotum and penis serve the cooling of the testes and the delivery of sperm.
