## Describe the production, composition, and functions of semen

# The Production, Composition, and Functions of Semen

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the production, composition, and functions of semen.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

Semen is the fluid that carries and supports the sperm, made from the sperm and the fluids of the accessory glands. Understanding it frames how the sperm are delivered and protected. Today we describe the production, composition, and functions of semen.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe how semen is produced.
- You will be able to describe its composition.
- You will be able to state its functions.
- You will be able to connect semen to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Sperm Mixed With Gland Fluids That Nourish, Protect, and Carry Them

The organising idea is that semen is made of sperm mixed with the fluids of the accessory glands, which nourish, protect, and carry the sperm. Sperm plus supporting fluids. This idea of sperm in supporting fluid frames the topic.

- Semen is a mixture of sperm and gland fluids.
- The sperm come from the testes.
- The fluids come from the accessory glands.
- The fluids nourish, protect, and carry the sperm.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Product Suspended in a Protective, Nourishing Medium

To keep semen concrete, picture a delicate product suspended in a medium that nourishes it, protects it from harm, and carries it to its destination. The sperm are suspended in the gland fluids that support them, and the image keeps the supporting medium in view.

- The delicate product corresponds to the sperm.
- The nourishing, protective medium corresponds to the gland fluids.
- Carrying it to its destination corresponds to carrying the sperm.
- The analogy breaks down in that a medium is inert, whereas semen is a living secretion.

---

## The Production of Semen

Begin with production, since semen is made from several sources. Sperm and fluids combine.

- The sperm are produced in the testes and matured in the epididymis.
- The accessory glands produce the fluids.
- As the sperm pass through the ducts, the gland fluids are added.
- The combined sperm and fluids form the semen.

---

## The Composition of Semen

Next the composition, which is mostly fluid. The sperm are a small part.

- Semen is mostly fluid from the accessory glands.
- The sperm make up only a small part of its volume.
- Most of the fluid comes from the seminal glands and the prostate.
- The fluid contains sugar for energy and substances that protect the sperm.

---

## The Functions of Semen

Finally the functions, which support the sperm. The fluid nourishes, protects, and carries them.

- The fluid provides energy for the sperm to move.
- It protects the sperm from the acidity of the female tract.
- It provides a medium that carries the sperm.
- It lubricates the passage of the sperm.

---

## Why Semen Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

Semen underlies reproduction relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- Semen carries and supports the sperm, essential to fertility.
- The gland fluids nourish and protect the sperm, relevant to reproductive function.
- Understanding semen clarifies how sperm are delivered and supported.
- Reproductive function is relevant to some patients you treat.
- Understanding semen grounds your reasoning about the male reproductive system.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a product suspended in a protective, nourishing medium.

- It is a mistake to think semen is mostly sperm, since it is mostly gland fluid.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the fluid nourishes and protects the sperm.
- It is a mistake to forget that most of the fluid comes from the seminal glands and prostate.
- It is a mistake to think the fluids play no role in reproduction.

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Describing the composition and functions aloud is the strongest test.

- Describe how semen is produced from memory.
- Describe its composition.

- Explain what the gland fluids do for the sperm.
- Relate semen to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the production, composition, and functions of semen. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- Semen is a mixture of sperm from the testes and fluids from the accessory glands.
- Most of its volume is fluid, chiefly from the seminal glands and prostate.
- The sperm make up only a small part.
- The fluid nourishes the sperm, protects them from acidity, and carries them.
- Semen carries and supports the sperm on their journey.
