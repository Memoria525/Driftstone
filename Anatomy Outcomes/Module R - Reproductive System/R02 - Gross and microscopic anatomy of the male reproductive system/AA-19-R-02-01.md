## Identify and describe the gross anatomy, microscopic anatomy, and functions of the testes

# The Testes

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Identify and describe the gross anatomy, microscopic anatomy, and functions of the testes.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The testes are the male gonads, producing sperm in coiled tubules and hormones in the tissue between them. Understanding their structure and functions frames the male reproductive system. Today we identify and describe the gross anatomy, microscopic anatomy, and functions of the testes.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe the gross anatomy of the testes.
- You will be able to describe their microscopic anatomy.
- You will be able to state their functions.
- You will be able to connect the testes to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Coiled Tubules That Make Sperm and Cells Between Them That Make Hormones

The organising idea is that the testes are made of coiled seminiferous tubules that produce sperm, with cells between the tubules that produce hormones. Tubules make sperm, cells between make hormones. This idea of two functions in one organ frames the topic.

- The testes are the male gonads.
- They contain coiled seminiferous tubules that produce sperm.
- Between the tubules are cells that produce male hormones.
- The testes produce both sperm and hormones.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Factory of Coiled Production Lines With Hormone Dispensers Between Them

To keep the testes concrete, picture a factory packed with coiled production lines that make a product, with dispensers between the lines that release a signalling substance. The seminiferous tubules make sperm and the cells between make hormones, and the image keeps the two functions in view.

- The coiled production lines correspond to the seminiferous tubules making sperm.
- The dispensers between the lines correspond to the cells making hormones.
- The two outputs correspond to sperm and hormones.
- The analogy breaks down in that a factory is machinery, whereas the testis is living tissue.

---

## The Gross Anatomy

Begin with the gross anatomy, since the testes are paired oval organs. They lie in the scrotum.

- The testes are two oval organs.
- They lie in the scrotum, outside the main body cavity.
- Their position in the scrotum keeps them slightly cooler than the body, which sperm production needs.
- Each testis is divided internally into lobules.

---

## The Microscopic Anatomy

Next the microscopic anatomy, which shows the tubules and cells. These carry out the two functions.

- Each lobule contains coiled seminiferous tubules.
- The seminiferous tubules are where sperm are produced.
- Between the tubules are interstitial cells that produce hormones.
- Cells within the tubules support the developing sperm.

---

## The Functions

Finally the functions, which combine sperm and hormone production. The testes do both.

- The testes produce sperm in the seminiferous tubules.
- They produce male hormones, chiefly testosterone, in the interstitial cells.
- The hormones drive male development and support sperm production.
- The testes are therefore both a sperm-making and a hormone-making organ.

---

## Why the Testes Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The testes underlie reproduction and male hormones relevant to your patients. Each point below connects them to practice.

- The testes produce testosterone, which influences muscle and bone, relevant to tissue adaptation.
- Their position in the scrotum makes them vulnerable to injury.
- Understanding the testes clarifies the male reproductive system.
- Male hormones are relevant to muscle and bone health.
- Understanding the testes grounds your reasoning about the reproductive system.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a factory of coiled production lines with hormone dispensers.

- It is a mistake to think the testes only make sperm, since they also make hormones.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the tubules make sperm and the cells between make hormones.
- It is a mistake to forget that the scrotal position keeps the testes cooler.
- It is a mistake to think sperm are made in the tissue between the tubules.

## Consolidation and Self-Check

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Describing the two functions aloud is the strongest test.

- Describe the gross anatomy of the testes from memory.
- Describe their microscopic anatomy.
- State their functions.
- Explain why the testes lie in the scrotum.
- Relate the testes to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the testes. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The testes are the male gonads, lying in the scrotum outside the body cavity.
- Their scrotal position keeps them slightly cooler, which sperm production needs.
- They contain coiled seminiferous tubules that produce sperm.
- Between the tubules, interstitial cells produce testosterone.
- The testes produce both sperm and hormones.
