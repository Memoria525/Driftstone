## Describe a typical ovarian cycle and explain how the process of folliculogenesis spans multiple ovarian cycles

# The Ovarian Cycle

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe a typical ovarian cycle and explain how the process of folliculogenesis spans multiple ovarian cycles.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The ovarian cycle is the monthly sequence of follicle growth, ovulation, and the follicle's aftermath, but the development of a follicle actually spans several cycles before it is ready. Understanding this frames how the ovary works over time. Today we describe a typical ovarian cycle and explain how folliculogenesis spans multiple cycles.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe the phases of the ovarian cycle.
- You will be able to explain what happens in each phase.
- You will be able to explain how follicle development spans several cycles.
- You will be able to connect the cycle to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Monthly Cycle of Growth, Release, and Aftermath, Built on Development Over Several Cycles

The organising idea is that the ovarian cycle has a phase of follicle growth, ovulation, and a phase after ovulation, but the follicle that ovulates has been developing over several previous cycles. A monthly cycle built on longer development. This idea of a cycle built on longer development frames the topic.

- The ovarian cycle has a phase before ovulation and a phase after.
- In the first phase, a follicle grows.
- Ovulation releases the egg.
- In the second phase, the empty follicle becomes a hormone gland.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Monthly Harvest From Crops Planted Over Several Seasons

To keep the cycle concrete, picture a monthly harvest of one ripe crop, drawn from crops that have been growing over several previous seasons before they were ready. The follicle that ovulates each cycle has developed over several cycles, and the image keeps the monthly harvest built on longer growth in view.

- The monthly harvest corresponds to the monthly ovarian cycle.
- The ripe crop corresponds to the follicle that ovulates.
- The growth over several seasons corresponds to the follicle developing over several cycles.
- The analogy breaks down in that crops are planted by choice, whereas follicle development is driven by hormones.

---

## The First Phase of the Cycle

Begin with the first phase, before ovulation. A follicle grows and produces hormone.

- In the first phase, a group of follicles grows.
- Usually one becomes dominant.
- The growing follicle produces oestrogen.
- This phase ends with ovulation.

---

## The Second Phase of the Cycle

Next the second phase, after ovulation. The empty follicle becomes a gland.

- After ovulation, the empty follicle becomes a temporary hormone gland.
- This gland produces progesterone and some oestrogen.
- These hormones prepare the body for a possible pregnancy.
- If pregnancy does not occur, the gland breaks down and the cycle repeats.

---

## How Folliculogenesis Spans Several Cycles

Finally how folliculogenesis spans several cycles, since this is the objective's focus. Development takes longer than one cycle.

- The follicle that ovulates in a cycle did not begin growing in that cycle.
- Follicles begin developing several cycles before they are ready.
- They pass through early stages over these cycles.
- Only in its final cycle does a follicle grow rapidly, become dominant, and ovulate.

---

## Why the Ovarian Cycle Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The ovarian cycle underlies the female hormonal cycle relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The cycle's hormones affect the body throughout the month, relevant to symptoms across the cycle.
- The hormones influence bone, relevant to bone health.
- Understanding the cycle clarifies how the ovary works over time.
- Female hormones are relevant to bone and tissue health.
- Understanding the cycle grounds your reasoning about the female reproductive system.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a monthly harvest from crops planted over several seasons.

- It is a mistake to think the ovulating follicle began growing in the same cycle, since it developed over several cycles.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the empty follicle becomes a hormone gland.
- It is a mistake to muddle the two phases of the cycle.
- It is a mistake to forget that the cycle repeats if pregnancy does not occur.

- Describe the phases of the ovarian cycle from memory.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the ovarian cycle. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The ovarian cycle has a first phase of follicle growth and a second phase after ovulation.
- In the first phase, a follicle grows and produces oestrogen, ending with ovulation.
- In the second phase, the empty follicle becomes a hormone gland producing progesterone.
- The follicle that ovulates has been developing over several previous cycles.
- If pregnancy does not occur, the gland breaks down and the cycle repeats.
