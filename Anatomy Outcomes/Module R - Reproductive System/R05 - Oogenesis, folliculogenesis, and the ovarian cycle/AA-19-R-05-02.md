# The Stages of Folliculogenesis

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the stages of folliculogenesis (ovarian follicle development) during a typical ovarian cycle.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

As an egg develops, the follicle around it grows through a series of stages, ending with the release of the egg. Understanding these stages frames the ovarian cycle. Today we describe the stages of folliculogenesis during a typical ovarian cycle.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe the stages of follicle development.
- You will be able to describe how the follicle changes.
- You will be able to explain what happens to the follicle after ovulation.
- You will be able to connect folliculogenesis to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Follicle That Grows, Releases Its Egg, and Then Becomes a Hormone Gland

The organising idea is that the follicle around the egg grows through stages, releases its egg at ovulation, and then becomes a temporary hormone gland. Grow, release, then become a gland. This idea of a growing, releasing follicle frames the topic.

- The follicle around the egg grows through stages.
- It enlarges and fills with fluid.
- It releases its egg at ovulation.
- The empty follicle then becomes a temporary hormone gland.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Bud That Swells, Opens to Release a Seed, and Then Becomes a Fruit

To keep folliculogenesis concrete, picture a bud that swells, opens to release a seed, and then develops into a fruit that produces something useful. The follicle grows, releases the egg, and becomes a hormone gland, and the image keeps the swelling, releasing, and transforming in view.

- The swelling bud corresponds to the growing follicle.
- Opening to release a seed corresponds to ovulation releasing the egg.
- Becoming a fruit corresponds to the follicle becoming a hormone gland.
- The analogy breaks down in that a bud is a plant, whereas the follicle is animal tissue driven by hormones.

---

## The Early Stages

Begin with the early stages, when the follicle is small. It starts as a resting follicle.

- The follicle begins as a small resting follicle with the egg inside.
- It has a single layer of supporting cells around the egg.
- Several follicles begin to grow at the start of a cycle.
- The supporting cells multiply and the follicle enlarges.

---

## The Growing Stages

Next the growing stages, when the follicle enlarges and fills with fluid. One follicle becomes dominant.

- As the follicle grows, a fluid-filled space forms within it.
- The follicle enlarges further.
- Usually one follicle becomes dominant while the others stop growing.
- The dominant follicle becomes large and ready to release its egg.

---

## Ovulation and After

Finally ovulation and the follicle's later fate. It releases the egg and becomes a gland.

- At ovulation, the dominant follicle releases its egg.
- The empty follicle then transforms into a temporary hormone gland.
- This gland produces hormones that prepare the body for a possible pregnancy.
- If pregnancy does not occur, the gland breaks down.

---

## Why Folliculogenesis Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

Folliculogenesis underlies the ovarian cycle and hormones relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The follicle produces hormones that drive the cycle and influence bone, relevant to bone health.
- The cycle of hormones affects the body throughout the menstrual cycle.
- Understanding folliculogenesis clarifies the ovarian cycle.
- Female hormones are relevant to bone and tissue health.
- Understanding it grounds your reasoning about the female reproductive system.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a bud that swells, opens, and becomes a fruit.

- It is a mistake to think the follicle disappears after ovulation, since it becomes a hormone gland.
- It is a mistake to overlook that usually one follicle becomes dominant.
- It is a mistake to forget that the follicle fills with fluid as it grows.
- It is a mistake to think all the growing follicles release eggs.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the stages of folliculogenesis. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The follicle begins as a small resting follicle with the egg inside.
- It grows, its supporting cells multiply, and a fluid-filled space forms.
- Usually one follicle becomes dominant and releases its egg at ovulation.
- The empty follicle becomes a temporary hormone gland.
- If pregnancy does not occur, the gland breaks down.
