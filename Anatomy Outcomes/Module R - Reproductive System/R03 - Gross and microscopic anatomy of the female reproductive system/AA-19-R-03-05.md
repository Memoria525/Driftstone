# The Female External Genitalia

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Identify and describe the structure and functions of the external genitalia (e.g., mons pubis, labia majora, labia minora, clitoris, greater vestibular glands).

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The female external genitalia protect the openings of the urinary and reproductive tracts and serve in reproduction. Understanding them frames the external part of the female system. Today we identify and describe the structure and functions of the female external genitalia.

- By the end of this session you will be able to name the parts of the female external genitalia.
- You will be able to describe each part.
- You will be able to state their functions.
- You will be able to connect them to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Structures That Protect the Openings and Serve in Reproduction

The organising idea is that the female external genitalia are a set of structures that protect the openings of the urinary and reproductive tracts and take part in reproduction. Protecting folds and structures. This idea of protecting structures frames the topic.

- The female external genitalia surround the openings of the urinary and reproductive tracts.
- They include folds of skin that protect the openings.
- They include the clitoris and glands.
- They protect the openings and serve in reproduction.

---

## Our Master Analogy: Protective Covers Around Two Entrances

To keep the external genitalia concrete, picture protective covers and folds arranged around two entrances, shielding them and keeping them moist. The female external genitalia protect the urinary and reproductive openings, and the image keeps the protecting covers in view.

- The protective covers correspond to the labia protecting the openings.
- The two entrances correspond to the urinary and reproductive openings.
- Keeping them moist corresponds to the glands lubricating the area.
- The analogy breaks down in that covers are inert, whereas the external genitalia are living tissue.

---

## The Mons Pubis and Labia

Begin with the mons pubis and labia, the protecting folds. These shield the openings.

- The mons pubis is a fatty pad over the pubic bone.
- The labia majora are the outer folds of skin.
- The labia minora are the inner folds.
- Together they protect the openings of the urinary and reproductive tracts.

---

## The Clitoris

Next the clitoris, which contains erectile tissue. It is the homologue of the penis.

- The clitoris is a small structure at the front of the external genitalia.
- It contains erectile tissue.
- It is highly sensitive.
- It is the female homologue of the penis.

---

## The Vestibular Glands

Finally the vestibular glands, which lubricate. These moisten the area.

- The greater vestibular glands lie near the vaginal opening.
- They produce a lubricating fluid.
- The fluid moistens the area.
- They help lubricate during reproduction.

---

## Why the External Genitalia Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The external genitalia are relevant to pelvic-health practice. Each point below connects them to practice.

- The external genitalia are near the pelvic floor, relevant to pelvic-health practice.
- Childbirth affects the external genitalia, relevant to recovery afterward.
- Understanding them clarifies the external female system.
- Pelvic-health function is relevant to some patients you treat.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about the female reproductive system.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to protective covers around two entrances.

- It is a mistake to overlook that the external genitalia protect the openings.
- It is a mistake to confuse the labia majora and minora.
- It is a mistake to forget that the clitoris is the homologue of the penis.
- It is a mistake to overlook the vestibular glands' lubricating role.
