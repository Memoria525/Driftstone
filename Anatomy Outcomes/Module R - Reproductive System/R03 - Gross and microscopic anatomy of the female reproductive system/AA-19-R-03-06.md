## Identify and describe the structure and functions of the mammary glands

# The Mammary Glands

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Identify and describe the structure and functions of the mammary glands.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The mammary glands produce milk to nourish a newborn, developing their milk-making ability during pregnancy. Understanding them frames the nourishment of the newborn. Today we identify and describe the structure and functions of the mammary glands.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe the structure of the mammary glands.
- You will be able to state their function.
- You will be able to explain how they produce milk.
- You will be able to connect the mammary glands to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Glands That Produce Milk to Nourish a Newborn

The organising idea is that the mammary glands are modified glands that produce milk to nourish a newborn, made of milk-producing units that drain through ducts to the nipple. Milk-making glands. This idea of milk-producing glands frames the topic.

- The mammary glands are modified glands in the breast.
- They produce milk to nourish a newborn.
- They contain milk-producing units.
- The units drain through ducts to the nipple.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Cluster of Production Units Draining to a Single Outlet

To keep the mammary glands concrete, picture a cluster of production units that make a product, draining through pipes to a single outlet. The milk-producing units drain through ducts to the nipple, and the image keeps the production and drainage in view.

- The production units correspond to the milk-producing units.
- The pipes correspond to the ducts.
- The single outlet corresponds to the nipple.
- The analogy breaks down in that production units are machinery, whereas the mammary glands are living tissue that responds to hormones.

---

## The Structure of the Mammary Glands

Begin with the structure, since they are glands within the breast. They drain to the nipple.

- The mammary glands lie within the breast.
- They contain lobes of milk-producing tissue.
- The lobes drain through ducts that lead to the nipple.
- The glands are surrounded by fat that gives the breast its shape.

---

## The Function of the Mammary Glands

Next the function, which is to make milk. They nourish the newborn.

- The mammary glands produce milk after childbirth.
- The milk nourishes the newborn.
- Milk production is triggered and maintained by hormones.
- The milk is released through the nipple during feeding.

---

## How the Glands Produce Milk

Finally how they produce milk, since this is driven by hormones. Pregnancy prepares them.

- During pregnancy, hormones prepare the mammary glands to make milk.
- After childbirth, hormones trigger milk production.
- The baby's feeding stimulates the release of the milk.
- Continued feeding maintains the milk supply.

---

## Why the Mammary Glands Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The mammary glands underlie the nourishment of the newborn relevant to your patients. Each point below connects them to practice.

- The mammary glands nourish the newborn, relevant to the postnatal period.
- Their changes are relevant to postnatal recovery in obstetric physiotherapy.
- Understanding them clarifies the nourishment of the newborn.
- The postnatal period is relevant to some patients you treat.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about the female reproductive system.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a cluster of production units draining to a single outlet.

- It is a mistake to overlook that the glands drain through ducts to the nipple.
- It is a mistake to forget that hormones drive milk production.
- It is a mistake to think the glands make milk without the influence of pregnancy hormones.
- It is a mistake to overlook that feeding stimulates milk release.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the mammary glands. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The mammary glands are modified glands in the breast that produce milk.
- They contain lobes of milk-producing tissue draining through ducts to the nipple.
- Hormones prepare them in pregnancy and trigger milk production after childbirth.
- The baby's feeding stimulates milk release and maintains the supply.
