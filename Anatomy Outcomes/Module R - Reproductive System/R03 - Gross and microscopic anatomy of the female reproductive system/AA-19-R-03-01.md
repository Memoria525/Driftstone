## Identify and describe the gross anatomy, microscopic anatomy and functions of the ovaries

# The Ovaries

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Identify and describe the gross anatomy, microscopic anatomy and functions of the ovaries.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The ovaries are the female gonads, producing eggs and hormones in a monthly cycle. Understanding their structure and functions frames the female reproductive system. Today we identify and describe the gross anatomy, microscopic anatomy, and functions of the ovaries.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe the gross anatomy of the ovaries.
- You will be able to describe their microscopic anatomy.
- You will be able to state their functions.
- You will be able to connect the ovaries to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Organs That Make Eggs in Follicles and Hormones Around Them

The organising idea is that the ovaries produce eggs, each developing in a follicle, and hormones that drive the female cycle. Follicles make eggs, and the ovary makes hormones. This idea of egg and hormone production frames the topic.

- The ovaries are the female gonads.
- They contain follicles, each holding a developing egg.
- They produce eggs and release one each cycle.
- They produce female hormones that drive the reproductive cycle.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Nursery of Developing Units That Also Broadcasts Signals

To keep the ovaries concrete, picture a nursery where many units develop, one being released each cycle, while the nursery also broadcasts signals to coordinate the process. The ovaries develop eggs in follicles and release hormones, and the image keeps the egg and hormone roles in view.

- The developing units correspond to the eggs in their follicles.
- Releasing one each cycle corresponds to releasing an egg each cycle.
- Broadcasting signals corresponds to producing hormones.
- The analogy breaks down in that a nursery is machinery, whereas the ovary is living tissue.

---

## The Gross Anatomy

Begin with the gross anatomy, since the ovaries are paired oval organs. They lie in the pelvis.

- The ovaries are two small oval organs.
- They lie in the pelvis, one on each side of the uterus.
- They are held in place by ligaments.
- They are near the open ends of the uterine tubes.

---

## The Microscopic Anatomy

Next the microscopic anatomy, which shows the follicles. These hold the developing eggs.

- The ovary contains many follicles.
- Each follicle holds a developing egg surrounded by supporting cells.
- The follicles are at different stages of development.
- The supporting cells produce hormones.

---

## The Functions

Finally the functions, which combine egg and hormone production. The ovaries do both.

- The ovaries produce eggs and release one each cycle.
- They produce female hormones, chiefly oestrogen and progesterone.
- The hormones drive the changes of the reproductive cycle.
- The ovaries are therefore both an egg-making and a hormone-making organ.

---

## Why the Ovaries Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The ovaries underlie reproduction and female hormones relevant to your patients. Each point below connects them to practice.

- The ovaries produce hormones that influence bone, relevant to bone health, especially after menopause.
- The loss of ovarian hormones at menopause affects bone density, relevant to fracture risk.
- Understanding the ovaries clarifies the female reproductive system.
- Female hormones are relevant to bone and tissue health.
- Understanding the ovaries grounds your reasoning about the reproductive system.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a nursery of developing units that also broadcasts signals.

- It is a mistake to think the ovaries only make eggs, since they also make hormones.
- It is a mistake to overlook that each egg develops in a follicle.
- It is a mistake to forget that the ovaries produce oestrogen and progesterone.
- It is a mistake to place the ovaries outside the pelvis.

## Consolidation and Self-Check

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Describing the two functions aloud is the strongest test.

- Describe the gross anatomy of the ovaries from memory.
- Describe their microscopic anatomy.
- State their functions.
- Explain the role of the follicles.
- Relate the ovaries to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the ovaries. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The ovaries are the female gonads, lying in the pelvis on each side of the uterus.
- They contain many follicles, each holding a developing egg.
- They release one egg each cycle.
- They produce oestrogen and progesterone, which drive the reproductive cycle.
- The loss of ovarian hormones at menopause affects bone density.
