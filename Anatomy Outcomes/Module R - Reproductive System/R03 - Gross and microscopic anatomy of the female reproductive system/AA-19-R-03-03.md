# The Ligaments of the Female Reproductive System

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Identify and describe the ligaments of the female reproductive system (e.g., broad ligament, ovarian ligament, suspensory ligament of the ovary, round ligament of the uterus).

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The female reproductive organs are held in place by a set of ligaments that suspend and anchor them within the pelvis. Understanding these ligaments frames how the organs are supported. Today we identify and describe the ligaments of the female reproductive system.

- By the end of this session you will be able to name the major ligaments.
- You will be able to describe what each supports.
- You will be able to describe their arrangement.
- You will be able to connect the ligaments to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Ligaments That Suspend and Anchor the Reproductive Organs

The organising idea is that a set of ligaments suspends and anchors the ovaries and uterus within the pelvis, holding them in place while allowing some movement. Ligaments that hold the organs in place. This idea of supporting ligaments frames the topic.

- Ligaments hold the female reproductive organs in place.
- The broad ligament is a large sheet supporting the uterus and tubes.
- Other ligaments anchor the ovary.
- The round ligament helps hold the uterus.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Sheet and Straps Suspending Items in a Space

To keep the ligaments concrete, picture a large sheet with attached straps suspending items within a space, holding them in place while allowing some movement. The broad ligament is the sheet and the other ligaments the straps, and the image keeps the suspending arrangement in view.

- The large sheet corresponds to the broad ligament.
- The straps corresponds to the ovarian, suspensory, and round ligaments.
- Holding the items in place corresponds to anchoring the organs.
- The analogy breaks down in that a sheet and straps are inert, whereas the ligaments are living tissue.

---

## The Broad Ligament

Begin with the broad ligament, the large supporting sheet. It drapes over the organs.

- The broad ligament is a large sheet of tissue.
- It extends from the sides of the uterus to the walls of the pelvis.
- It drapes over the uterus and the uterine tubes.
- It supports the uterus, tubes, and ovaries.

---

## The Ovarian Ligaments

Next the ligaments of the ovary, which anchor it. Two hold the ovary in place.

- The ovarian ligament attaches the ovary to the uterus.
- The suspensory ligament of the ovary attaches the ovary to the pelvic wall.
- The suspensory ligament also carries the vessels to the ovary.
- Together they hold the ovary in position.

---

## The Round Ligament

Finally the round ligament, which helps hold the uterus. It runs to the front.

- The round ligament of the uterus runs from the uterus forward to the front of the pelvis.
- It helps hold the uterus in its usual forward-tilted position.
- It passes through the wall of the abdomen.
- It is one of several ligaments supporting the uterus.

---

## Why the Ligaments Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The ligaments underlie the support of the reproductive organs relevant to pelvic-health practice. Each point below connects them to practice.

- The ligaments and the pelvic floor together support the uterus, relevant to pelvic-health practice.
- Weakness of the support can allow the organs to descend, a prolapse you may help manage.
- The round ligament can be a source of pain in pregnancy.
- Understanding the ligaments clarifies how the organs are supported.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about pelvic support.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a sheet and straps suspending items.

- It is a mistake to think one ligament holds all the organs, since several share the support.
- It is a mistake to confuse the ovarian ligament with the suspensory ligament.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the broad ligament drapes over the organs.
- It is a mistake to forget that the round ligament helps hold the uterus forward.

## Consolidation and Self-Check

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Naming each ligament and what it supports aloud is the strongest test.

- Name the major ligaments from memory.
- Describe what each supports.
- Describe their arrangement.
- Explain what the broad ligament supports.
- Relate the ligaments to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

---

## Key Takeaways

We have described the ligaments of the female reproductive system. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The broad ligament is a large sheet supporting the uterus, tubes, and ovaries.
- The ovarian ligament attaches the ovary to the uterus, and the suspensory ligament attaches it to the pelvic wall and carries its vessels.
- The round ligament runs from the uterus to the front and helps hold it forward.
- Together the ligaments suspend and anchor the reproductive organs.
- The ligaments and pelvic floor support the uterus, relevant to prolapse.
