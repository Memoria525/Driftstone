## Identify and describe the gross anatomy, microscopic anatomy and functions of the uterus and uterine (fallopian) tubes

# The Uterus and Uterine Tubes

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Identify and describe the gross anatomy, microscopic anatomy and functions of the uterus and uterine (fallopian) tubes.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The uterine tubes carry the egg toward the uterus and are where fertilisation usually occurs, and the uterus is the muscular organ that carries a pregnancy. Understanding them frames the central female reproductive organs. Today we identify and describe the gross anatomy, microscopic anatomy, and functions of the uterus and uterine tubes.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe the uterine tubes.
- You will be able to describe the uterus.
- You will be able to state their functions.
- You will be able to connect them to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Tubes That Carry the Egg and a Muscular Organ That Carries a Pregnancy

The organising idea is that the uterine tubes carry the egg from the ovary toward the uterus and are the usual site of fertilisation, while the uterus is a muscular organ with a lining that supports a pregnancy. Tubes carry the egg, the uterus carries a pregnancy. This idea of carrying and nurturing frames the topic.

- The uterine tubes carry the egg from the ovary toward the uterus.
- They are the usual site of fertilisation.
- The uterus is a muscular organ that carries a pregnancy.
- Its lining thickens each cycle to support a possible pregnancy.

---

## Our Master Analogy: Delivery Tubes Leading to a Nurturing Chamber

To keep the organs concrete, picture delivery tubes that carry an item toward a nurturing chamber prepared to receive it. The uterine tubes carry the egg to the uterus, which is prepared to nurture a pregnancy, and the image keeps the carrying tubes and nurturing chamber in view.

- The delivery tubes correspond to the uterine tubes.
- The nurturing chamber corresponds to the uterus.
- Preparing to receive corresponds to the uterine lining thickening.
- The analogy breaks down in that tubes and a chamber are machinery, whereas these organs are living tissue.

---

## The Uterine Tubes

Begin with the uterine tubes, which carry the egg. They open near the ovary.

- The uterine tubes extend from near each ovary to the uterus.
- Their open ends have finger-like projections that sweep the released egg in.
- Their lining has cilia and muscle that move the egg toward the uterus.
- Fertilisation usually occurs within the uterine tube.

---

## The Uterus

Next the uterus, which carries a pregnancy. It is a muscular organ.

- The uterus is a muscular, pear-shaped organ in the pelvis.
- It has a thick muscular wall.
- Its inner lining thickens each cycle to support a possible pregnancy.
- If pregnancy does not occur, the lining is shed as the menstrual period.

---

## The Functions

Finally the functions, which combine carrying and nurturing. The tubes and uterus serve reproduction.

- The uterine tubes carry the egg and are the site of fertilisation.
- The uterus receives the fertilised egg and supports its development.
- Its lining nourishes the early pregnancy.
- Its muscular wall contracts during childbirth.

---

## Why the Uterus and Tubes Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The uterus and tubes underlie pregnancy relevant to obstetric physiotherapy. Each point below connects them to practice.

- The uterus carries a pregnancy, central to obstetric physiotherapy.
- The uterus's muscular wall contracts during childbirth, relevant to labour.
- The pelvic floor supports the uterus, relevant to pelvic-health practice.
- Understanding these organs clarifies the female reproductive system.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about pregnancy and the pelvic floor.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to delivery tubes leading to a nurturing chamber.

- It is a mistake to think fertilisation usually occurs in the uterus, since it usually occurs in the uterine tube.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the uterine lining thickens each cycle.
- It is a mistake to forget that the uterus is a muscular organ.
- It is a mistake to think the tubes play no role in moving the egg.

## Consolidation and Self-Check

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Describing the organs and functions aloud is the strongest test.

- Describe the uterine tubes from memory.
- Describe the uterus.
- State their functions.
- Explain where fertilisation usually occurs.
- Relate them to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the uterus and uterine tubes. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The uterine tubes carry the egg from near the ovary toward the uterus.
- They sweep the egg in and move it with cilia and muscle, and are the usual site of fertilisation.
- The uterus is a muscular, pear-shaped organ that carries a pregnancy.
- Its lining thickens each cycle and is shed as the menstrual period if pregnancy does not occur.
- The uterus's muscular wall contracts during childbirth.
