## Explain the role of the hypothalamus in the production and release of posterior pituitary hormones

# The Hypothalamus and Posterior Pituitary Hormones

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Explain the role of the hypothalamus in the production and release of posterior pituitary hormones.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The posterior pituitary releases hormones, but unlike the anterior pituitary, it does not make them; the hypothalamus makes them and sends them down nerve fibres for storage and release. Understanding this explains why the posterior pituitary is really an extension of the brain. Today we explain the role of the hypothalamus in the production and release of posterior pituitary hormones.

- By the end of this session you will be able to explain where posterior pituitary hormones are made.
- You will be able to describe how they reach the posterior pituitary.
- You will be able to explain how they are stored and released.
- You will be able to connect this to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: The Hypothalamus Makes the Hormones the Posterior Pituitary Only Stores and Releases

The organising idea is that the hypothalamus produces the posterior pituitary hormones and sends them down nerve fibres to the posterior pituitary, which only stores and releases them. Made in the brain, stored and released below. This idea of production above and release below frames the topic.

- The posterior pituitary hormones are made in the hypothalamus, not in the pituitary.
- They travel down nerve fibres from the hypothalamus to the posterior pituitary.
- The posterior pituitary stores them until they are needed.
- It then releases them into the blood on a signal from the hypothalamus.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Factory That Sends Goods to a Warehouse for Dispatch

To keep the control concrete, picture a factory that manufactures goods and sends them down a conveyor to a warehouse, which stores them and dispatches them when ordered. The hypothalamus is the factory and the posterior pituitary is the warehouse, and the image keeps production above and dispatch below in view.

- The factory making goods corresponds to the hypothalamus making the hormones.
- The conveyor sending goods corresponds to the nerve fibres carrying the hormones.
- The warehouse storing and dispatching corresponds to the posterior pituitary storing and releasing.
- The analogy breaks down in that a conveyor is mechanical, whereas the hormones travel down living nerve fibres.

---

## Where the Hormones Are Made

Begin with production, which happens in the hypothalamus. The hormones are made by neurons.

- The posterior pituitary hormones are made by neurons in the hypothalamus.
- These neurons produce the hormones in their cell bodies.
- The hypothalamus, not the posterior pituitary, is the site of production.
- This is why the posterior pituitary is an extension of the hypothalamus.

---

## How the Hormones Reach the Posterior Pituitary

Next transport, which is down the nerve fibres. The hormones travel along the axons.

- The neurons that make the hormones have long axons running down into the posterior pituitary.
- The hormones travel down these axons to the posterior pituitary.
- They are carried inside the nerve fibres rather than through the blood.
- They arrive at the endings of the fibres in the posterior pituitary.

---

## Storage and Release

Finally storage and release, which the posterior pituitary handles. It holds and dispatches the hormones.

- The hormones are stored at the nerve endings in the posterior pituitary.
- They remain stored until a signal calls for their release.
- When the hypothalamic neurons are stimulated, they trigger release of the stored hormones into the blood.
- The two main hormones released regulate water balance and functions such as childbirth and milk release.

---

## Why This Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The posterior pituitary hormones underlie water balance relevant to exercise and hydration. Each point below connects them to practice.

- One posterior pituitary hormone regulates water balance, relevant to hydration during exercise.
- Understanding the production and release clarifies how the brain controls these hormones.
- The link between the hypothalamus and posterior pituitary shows the nervous-endocrine connection.
- Water balance is relevant to a patient's response to activity and recovery.
- Understanding this grounds your reasoning about hormonal control of water balance.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a factory sending goods to a warehouse.

- It is a mistake to think the posterior pituitary makes its own hormones, since the hypothalamus makes them.
- It is a mistake to believe the hormones travel to the posterior pituitary through the blood, since they travel down nerve fibres.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the posterior pituitary only stores and releases the hormones.
- It is a mistake to confuse the posterior pituitary control with the blood-link control of the anterior pituitary.

## Key Takeaways

We have explained the control of the posterior pituitary. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The posterior pituitary hormones are made by neurons in the hypothalamus.
- They travel down nerve fibres to the posterior pituitary, not through the blood.
- The posterior pituitary stores them and releases them into the blood on a hypothalamic signal.
- The posterior pituitary is an extension of the hypothalamus.
- Its hormones regulate water balance and functions such as childbirth and milk release.
