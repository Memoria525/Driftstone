# The Adrenal Cortex

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the anatomy of the adrenal cortex, its location, and the major hormones secreted.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The adrenal cortex is the outer part of the adrenal gland, releasing hormones that regulate salt and water balance, metabolism, and the response to long-term stress. Understanding it explains how the body sustains itself under demand. Today we describe the anatomy of the adrenal cortex, its location, and its major hormones.

- By the end of this session you will be able to locate the adrenal cortex.
- You will be able to describe its anatomy.
- You will be able to name its major hormones and their effects.
- You will be able to connect the adrenal cortex to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: The Outer Adrenal Layer That Sustains the Body Under Demand

The organising idea is that the adrenal cortex releases hormones that regulate salt and water balance, metabolism, and the sustained response to stress, keeping the body running under long-term demand. Sustained regulation under demand. This idea of a sustaining outer layer frames the topic.

- The adrenal cortex is the outer part of the adrenal gland.
- It sits atop the kidney.
- It releases hormones regulating salt and water, metabolism, and stress.
- These hormones support the body's sustained functioning.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Building's Long-Term Resource Manager

To keep the adrenal cortex concrete, picture a resource manager who regulates a building's water and power balance, manages its energy stores, and keeps it running through prolonged demand. The adrenal cortex regulates salt, water, and metabolism this way, and the image keeps its sustaining role in view.

- Regulating water and power balance corresponds to regulating salt and water.
- Managing energy stores corresponds to regulating metabolism.
- Keeping the building running under demand corresponds to the sustained stress response.
- The analogy breaks down in that a manager acts by decision, whereas the cortex responds to hormonal and chemical signals.

---

## The Location and Anatomy

Begin with where the adrenal cortex lies and its structure. It caps the kidney as the outer layer.

- The adrenal glands sit one on top of each kidney.
- The adrenal cortex is the outer part of each adrenal gland.
- It surrounds the inner adrenal medulla.
- It is organised in layers, each producing particular hormones.

---

## The Salt and Metabolic Hormones

Next its hormones regulating salt and metabolism, which sustain the body. These act on balance and energy.

- The adrenal cortex releases a hormone that regulates salt and water balance by acting on the kidneys.
- This hormone helps control blood volume and blood pressure.
- It releases a hormone that regulates metabolism and helps the body respond to stress.
- This stress hormone raises blood sugar and influences how the body uses energy.

---

## The Sex Hormones

Finally its small production of sex hormones. This is a minor role.

- The adrenal cortex releases small amounts of sex hormones.
- These add to the larger amounts produced by the reproductive glands.
- They contribute to some secondary characteristics.
- This is a minor role compared with the cortex's salt and metabolic hormones.

---

## Why the Adrenal Cortex Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The adrenal cortex underlies the stress response and salt balance relevant to exercise. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The cortex's stress hormone helps the body sustain the demands of prolonged activity.
- Its regulation of salt and water affects blood volume and pressure relevant to exercise.
- Conditions of the adrenal cortex affect energy, salt balance, and the stress response.
- Long-term use of related medications can affect tissue and bone, relevant to your patients.
- Understanding the adrenal cortex grounds your reasoning about the sustained stress response.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a long-term resource manager.

- It is a mistake to confuse the adrenal cortex with the adrenal medulla, since they are different layers with different hormones.
- It is a mistake to think the cortex handles only stress, since it also regulates salt and metabolism.
- It is a mistake to place the adrenal glands anywhere but atop the kidneys.
- It is a mistake to overlook the cortex's small production of sex hormones.
