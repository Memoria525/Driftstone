# The Adrenal Medulla

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the anatomy of the adrenal medulla, its location, and the major hormones secreted.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The adrenal medulla is the inner part of the adrenal gland, releasing the hormones of the rapid fight-or-flight response. Understanding it explains the surge of energy and alertness we feel under sudden stress. Today we describe the anatomy of the adrenal medulla, its location, and its major hormones.

- By the end of this session you will be able to locate the adrenal medulla.
- You will be able to describe its anatomy.
- You will be able to name its major hormones and their effects.
- You will be able to connect the adrenal medulla to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: The Inner Adrenal Core That Drives the Rapid Stress Response

The organising idea is that the adrenal medulla releases the hormones adrenaline and noradrenaline that drive the rapid fight-or-flight response, reinforcing the sympathetic nervous system. A rapid stress core. This idea of a fight-or-flight core frames the topic.

- The adrenal medulla is the inner part of the adrenal gland.
- It releases adrenaline and noradrenaline.
- These hormones drive the rapid fight-or-flight response.
- It works closely with the sympathetic nervous system.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Rapid-Response Booster Wired to the Alarm

To keep the medulla concrete, picture a booster unit wired directly to a building's alarm, that releases a surge of power the instant the alarm sounds to prepare the building for action. The adrenal medulla releases its hormones the instant the sympathetic nervous system signals danger, and the image keeps the rapid boost in view.

- The booster unit corresponds to the adrenal medulla.
- The surge of power corresponds to adrenaline and noradrenaline.
- The wiring to the alarm corresponds to its close link to the sympathetic nervous system.
- The analogy breaks down in that a booster is mechanical, whereas the medulla releases hormones into the blood.

---

## The Location and Anatomy

Begin with where the medulla lies and its nature. It is the inner core of the adrenal gland.

- The adrenal medulla is the inner part of each adrenal gland.
- It is surrounded by the adrenal cortex.
- It is closely connected to the sympathetic nervous system.
- It acts almost like a specialised part of that system.

---

## The Medullary Hormones

Next its hormones, which drive the rapid stress response. These are adrenaline and noradrenaline.

- The adrenal medulla releases adrenaline and noradrenaline into the blood.
- These hormones raise heart rate and the force of the heartbeat.
- They raise blood sugar and divert blood to the muscles.
- They prepare the body for rapid, vigorous action.

---

## The Link to the Sympathetic Nervous System

Finally its link to the sympathetic system, which explains its rapid action. It reinforces the nervous response.

- The adrenal medulla is stimulated directly by the sympathetic nervous system.
- When the sympathetic system is activated, the medulla releases its hormones.
- These hormones prolong and reinforce the fight-or-flight response.
- The medulla thus adds a hormonal boost to the rapid nervous response.

---

## Why the Adrenal Medulla Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The adrenal medulla underlies the rapid response to exertion and stress relevant to exercise. Each point below connects it to practice.

- Adrenaline raises heart rate and diverts blood to muscle during exercise, which you monitor.
- The fight-or-flight response prepares the body for vigorous activity.
- The medulla's hormones reinforce the sympathetic response during exertion.
- Understanding the medulla clarifies the body's rapid response to demand.
- Understanding it grounds your reasoning about the response to exercise and stress.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a rapid-response booster wired to the alarm.

- It is a mistake to confuse the adrenal medulla with the adrenal cortex, since one drives rapid stress and the other sustained regulation.
- It is a mistake to think the medulla acts slowly, since it drives the rapid response.
- It is a mistake to overlook its close link to the sympathetic nervous system.
- It is a mistake to place the medulla outside the adrenal gland.
