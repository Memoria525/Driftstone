## Identify and define the general terms gyrus, sulcus, and fissure

# Gyrus, Sulcus, and Fissure

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Identify and define the general terms gyrus, sulcus, and fissure.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The surface of the cerebrum is folded into ridges and grooves, and three basic terms name them. Learning these terms lets you read the brain's surface and locate the landmarks used to divide it. Today we identify and define the terms gyrus, sulcus, and fissure.

- By the end of this session you will be able to define gyrus, sulcus, and fissure.
- You will be able to distinguish a sulcus from a fissure.
- You will be able to explain why the brain's surface is folded.
- You will be able to connect these terms to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Folds That Pack a Large Surface Into a Small Space

The organising idea is that the cerebrum is folded into ridges and grooves so that a large surface area of cortex fits within the skull, and the three terms simply name the ridges, the shallow grooves, and the deep grooves. Folds that pack in more cortex. This idea of folding for surface area frames the terms.

- The cerebrum is folded into ridges and grooves.
- A gyrus is a raised ridge, and a sulcus is a shallow groove.
- A fissure is a deep groove.
- The folding packs a large area of cortex into the limited space of the skull.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Crumpled Sheet Fitted Into a Box

To keep the terms concrete, picture a large sheet of paper crumpled to fit into a small box: the raised folds are ridges, the shallow creases are minor grooves, and the deep folds are major grooves. The brain's surface is crumpled this way to fit the skull, and the image keeps ridges and grooves in view.

- The raised folds correspond to the gyri.
- The shallow creases correspond to the sulci.
- The deep folds correspond to the fissures.
- The analogy breaks down in that a crumpled sheet is folded randomly, whereas the brain's folds follow a consistent pattern that names its regions.

---

## Defining a Gyrus

Begin with the ridges, the raised folds. These are the gyri.

- A gyrus is a raised ridge or fold on the surface of the cerebrum.
- Gyri increase the surface area of the cortex.
- Specific named gyri, such as the precentral gyrus, carry particular functions.
- The plural of gyrus is gyri.

---

## Defining a Sulcus and a Fissure

Next the grooves, distinguished by depth. These are the sulci and fissures.

- A sulcus is a shallow groove between two gyri.
- A fissure is a deeper groove, dividing larger regions of the brain.
- The difference between them is mainly one of depth, a fissure being deeper.
- Named sulci and fissures, such as the central sulcus and longitudinal fissure, serve as landmarks.

---

## Why the Brain Is Folded

Finally why the folding exists, since it explains the terms. Folding maximises cortex.

- The folding greatly increases the surface area of the cortex.
- This allows far more cortical tissue to fit within the fixed space of the skull.
- More cortex allows greater processing capacity.
- The pattern of folds is consistent enough to name the brain's regions.

---

## Why These Terms Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The terms let you read the brain's surface and locate functional regions. Each point below connects them to practice.

- Named gyri, such as the precentral gyrus, carry motor functions central to movement you assess.
- Named sulci and fissures are landmarks used to locate functional regions on imaging.
- Reading the brain's surface underlies interpreting the location of a stroke or lesion.
- The folding explains how much cortex, and therefore function, is packed into the brain.
- Understanding these terms grounds your reading of brain anatomy.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors blur these terms, and naming them keeps them distinct. When unsure, return to a crumpled sheet in a box.

- It is a mistake to confuse a gyrus with a sulcus, since one is a ridge and the other a groove.
- It is a mistake to treat a sulcus and a fissure as identical, since a fissure is deeper.
- It is a mistake to think the folding is random, since it follows a consistent pattern.
- It is a mistake to overlook that folding increases cortical surface area.

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Defining each term aloud is the strongest test.

- Define gyrus, sulcus, and fissure from memory.
- Distinguish a sulcus from a fissure.
- Explain why the brain's surface is folded.
- Give an example of a named gyrus and a named fissure.
- Relate these terms to locating a functional region.

## Key Takeaways

We have defined the terms for the brain's folds. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- A gyrus is a raised ridge on the surface of the cerebrum.
- A sulcus is a shallow groove, and a fissure is a deeper groove.
- The folding packs a large area of cortex into the skull, increasing processing capacity.
- Named gyri, sulci, and fissures serve as landmarks.
- The terms let you read the brain's surface and locate functional regions.
