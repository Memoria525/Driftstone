# The Three Functional Types of Neuron

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Compare and contrast the three functional types of neurons (motor [efferent] neurons, sensory [afferent] neurons, and interneurons [association neurons]) with respect to their structure, location, and function.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

Neurons are classified not only by shape but by the job they do in the flow of information: bringing it in, passing it on, or sending it out. These three functional types map onto the sense-decide-act loop of the nervous system. Today we compare and contrast the three functional types of neuron.

- By the end of this session you will be able to name the three functional types of neuron.
- You will be able to state the role of each in the flow of information.
- You will be able to state where each is located.
- You will be able to connect the types to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Neurons Named by Their Place in the Loop

The organising idea is that the three functional types are defined by their place in the sense-decide-act loop, with sensory neurons bringing information in, interneurons processing it, and motor neurons sending commands out. Input, processing, output, as cell types. This idea of naming by role in the loop frames the comparison.

- The functional types are defined by their role in the flow of information.
- Sensory neurons carry information toward the central nervous system.
- Interneurons process information within the central nervous system.
- Motor neurons carry commands away to effectors.

---

## Our Master Analogy: Reporters, Analysts, and Dispatchers

To keep the types concrete, picture a news operation with reporters who gather information, analysts who process it in the office, and dispatchers who send out instructions. The three functional neuron types are these roles, defined by their place in handling information. The image keeps input, processing, and output as roles in view.

- The reporters gathering information correspond to the sensory neurons.
- The office analysts processing it correspond to the interneurons.
- The dispatchers sending instructions correspond to the motor neurons.
- The analogy breaks down in that people can change roles, whereas each neuron type has a fixed function set by its connections.

---

## The Sensory Neurons

Begin with the input type, which brings information in. These are the sensory, or afferent, neurons.

- Sensory neurons carry information from receptors toward the central nervous system.
- They are typically unipolar, with cell bodies in the dorsal root ganglia.
- They detect stimuli such as touch, pain, temperature, and position.
- They form the afferent, or input, side of the nervous system.

---

## The Interneurons

Next the processing type, which lies within the central nervous system. These are the interneurons.

- Interneurons lie entirely within the central nervous system.
- They connect sensory neurons to motor neurons and to one another.
- They are typically multipolar and are the most numerous type.
- They process and integrate information, forming the basis of decision, memory, and reflexes.

---

## The Motor Neurons

Finally the output type, which sends commands out. These are the motor, or efferent, neurons.

- Motor neurons carry commands away from the central nervous system to effectors.
- They are typically multipolar, with cell bodies in the central nervous system.
- They act on muscles and glands to produce responses.
- They form the efferent, or output, side of the nervous system.

---

## Why These Types Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The functional types underlie how sensation, processing, and movement are produced. Each point below connects a type to practice.

- Motor neurons produce the movement you assess, strengthen, and retrain.
- Sensory neurons carry the sensation you test in examination.
- Interneurons underlie the reflexes and motor control central to rehabilitation.
- A problem in any type, from sensation to processing to output, produces a deficit you must localise.
- Understanding the types grounds your reasoning about neurological presentations.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors blur the types, and naming them keeps them distinct. When unsure, return to reporters, analysts, and dispatchers.

- It is a mistake to confuse sensory and motor neurons, since one carries in and the other out.
- It is a mistake to place interneurons in the peripheral nervous system, since they lie within the central nervous system.
- It is a mistake to overlook interneurons, which are the most numerous type.
- It is a mistake to equate functional types with structural types, since the two classifications differ.

- Name the three functional types of neuron from memory.

- Explain how the three types form a loop.

## Key Takeaways

We have compared the three functional types of neuron. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- Functional types are defined by their role in the flow of information.
- Sensory neurons carry information toward the central nervous system.
- Interneurons process information within the central nervous system and are most numerous.
- Motor neurons carry commands away to muscles and glands.
- Together the three form the sense-decide-act loop of the nervous system.
