# The Components and Ganglia of the Autonomic Divisions

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the major components of the sympathetic and parasympathetic divisions and the major ganglia of each division.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

Each autonomic division is built from characteristic nerves and ganglia that carry its fibres to the organs. Learning these components completes your picture of how automatic commands reach their targets. Today we describe the major components and ganglia of the sympathetic and parasympathetic divisions.

- By the end of this session you will be able to name the major components of the sympathetic division.
- You will be able to name the major components of the parasympathetic division.
- You will be able to describe the major ganglia of each division.
- You will be able to connect these components to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Each Division Has Its Own Nerves and Ganglion Sites

The organising idea is that the sympathetic division carries its fibres through a chain of ganglia beside the spine and their connecting nerves, while the parasympathetic division carries its fibres through specific cranial nerves and pelvic nerves to ganglia near the organs. Each division has its own wiring and relay sites. This idea of division-specific components frames the topic.

- The sympathetic division uses a chain of ganglia beside the spine and their connecting nerves.
- The parasympathetic division uses specific cranial nerves and pelvic nerves.
- Each division relays in ganglia located characteristically for that division.
- The components carry automatic commands from the cord to the organs.

---

## Our Master Analogy: Two Delivery Networks With Different Depots

To keep the components concrete, picture two delivery networks, one with a line of depots close to the central warehouse and one with depots far out near the customers. The sympathetic depots lie near the spine and the parasympathetic depots lie near the organs, and the image keeps the differing relay sites in view.

- The line of depots near the warehouse corresponds to the sympathetic trunk ganglia near the spine.
- The depots far out near customers correspond to the parasympathetic ganglia near the organs.
- The connecting delivery routes correspond to the rami and splanchnic nerves.
- The analogy breaks down in that depots are buildings, whereas the ganglia are clusters of living neuron cell bodies.

---

## The Components of the Sympathetic Division

Begin with the sympathetic components, centred on the chain beside the spine. These carry its fibres.

- The sympathetic trunk, or chain, is a line of ganglia running beside the vertebral column.
- The white rami communicantes carry preganglionic fibres from the cord into the trunk.
- The gray rami communicantes carry postganglionic fibres from the trunk back to the spinal nerves.
- The splanchnic nerves carry sympathetic fibres to ganglia in front of the spine that serve the abdominal organs.

---

## The Components of the Parasympathetic Division

Next the parasympathetic components, centred on cranial and pelvic nerves. These carry its fibres.

- The parasympathetic division uses four cranial nerves: the oculomotor, facial, glossopharyngeal, and vagus.
- The vagus nerve carries parasympathetic fibres to the heart, lungs, and much of the digestive tract.
- The pelvic splanchnic nerves carry parasympathetic fibres from the sacral cord to the pelvic organs.
- These nerves carry the division's fibres toward ganglia near or within the organs.

---

## The Ganglia of Each Division

Finally the ganglia, where each division relays. Their locations differ.

- The sympathetic trunk ganglia lie beside the spine, and the prevertebral, or collateral, ganglia lie in front of it.
- The sympathetic ganglia are therefore near the cord and far from the organs.
- The parasympathetic terminal ganglia lie near the organs, and intramural ganglia lie within the walls of the organs.
- The parasympathetic ganglia are therefore near or within their target organs.

---

## Why These Components Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The components underlie how automatic commands reach the organs relevant to exercise and function. Each point below connects them to practice.

- The vagus nerve's parasympathetic supply to the heart is relevant to heart rate control during and after exercise.
- The sympathetic chain's supply underlies the fight-or-flight responses during exertion.
- Understanding the components clarifies how automatic commands reach the organs.
- The pelvic splanchnic nerves' supply is relevant to pelvic organ function.
- Understanding these components grounds your reasoning about autonomic control.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to two delivery networks with different depots.

- It is a mistake to place the parasympathetic ganglia near the spine, since they lie near or within the organs.
- It is a mistake to confuse the white and gray rami, since one carries preganglionic and the other postganglionic fibres.
- It is a mistake to overlook that four cranial nerves carry parasympathetic fibres.
- It is a mistake to forget the pelvic splanchnic nerves serving the pelvic organs.

## Consolidation and Self-Check

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Naming the components and ganglia aloud is the strongest test.

- Name the major components of the sympathetic division from memory.
- Name the major components of the parasympathetic division.
- Describe the major ganglia of each division.
- Explain where each division's ganglia lie relative to the organs.
- Relate one component to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

---

## Key Takeaways

We have described the components and ganglia of the autonomic divisions. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The sympathetic division uses the sympathetic trunk, the white and gray rami communicantes, and the splanchnic nerves.
- Its ganglia, the trunk and prevertebral ganglia, lie near the spine.
- The parasympathetic division uses four cranial nerves, including the vagus, and the pelvic splanchnic nerves.
- Its ganglia, terminal and intramural, lie near or within the organs.
- The components carry automatic commands from the cord to the organs.
