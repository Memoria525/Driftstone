# The Layers of the Heart Wall

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the structure and function of each layer of the heart wall (epicardium, myocardium, endocardium).

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The heart wall is built in three layers, an outer covering, a thick muscular middle, and a smooth inner lining. Understanding them explains how the heart pumps and how its surface and lining are kept smooth. Today we describe the structure and function of each layer of the heart wall.

- By the end of this session you will be able to name the three layers of the heart wall.
- You will be able to describe the structure of each.
- You will be able to state the function of each.
- You will be able to connect the layers to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Muscular Middle Between a Smooth Lining and an Outer Covering

The organising idea is that the heart wall has a thick muscular middle layer that does the pumping, a smooth inner lining that lets blood flow without clotting, and an outer covering that protects the surface. A pumping middle between two smooth surfaces. This idea of a three-layered wall frames the topic.

- The heart wall has three layers.
- The outer epicardium covers the surface.
- The middle myocardium is the thick muscle that pumps.
- The inner endocardium is the smooth lining.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Pump With an Outer Coat, a Muscular Body, and a Smooth Liner

To keep the layers concrete, picture a pump with an outer protective coat, a muscular body that does the work, and a smooth inner liner so fluid flows freely. The heart wall has these three layers, and the image keeps covering, pumping, and lining in view.

- The outer protective coat corresponds to the epicardium.
- The muscular body corresponds to the myocardium.
- The smooth inner liner corresponds to the endocardium.
- The analogy breaks down in that a pump's parts are manufactured, whereas the heart's layers are living tissue.

---

## The Epicardium

Begin with the outer layer, which covers the surface. This is the epicardium.

- The epicardium is the outer layer of the heart wall.
- It is the same as the visceral layer of the serous pericardium.
- It covers and protects the surface of the heart.
- It contains the coronary vessels and some fat.

---

## The Myocardium

Next the middle layer, which does the pumping. This is the myocardium.

- The myocardium is the thick middle layer of cardiac muscle.
- It is the bulk of the heart wall.
- Its contraction pumps blood out of the heart.
- It is thickest in the left ventricle, which pumps to the whole body.

---

## The Endocardium

Finally the inner layer, which lines the chambers. This is the endocardium.

- The endocardium is the smooth inner lining of the heart.
- It lines the chambers and covers the valves.
- Its smooth surface lets blood flow without clotting.
- It is continuous with the lining of the blood vessels.

---

## Why the Heart Wall Layers Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The heart wall layers underlie the heart's pumping and conditions relevant to your patients. Each point below connects a layer to practice.

- The myocardium does the pumping, and its damage, as in a heart attack, impairs the heart's function.
- The smooth endocardium prevents clotting, and its damage can allow clots to form.
- The epicardium is the layer involved in pericarditis, causing chest pain.
- The heart's pumping ability underlies exercise tolerance in patients you rehabilitate.
- Understanding the layers grounds your reasoning about heart function.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a pump with a coat, body, and liner.

- It is a mistake to confuse the three layers, since one covers, one pumps, and one lines.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the epicardium is the visceral serous pericardium.
- It is a mistake to think the myocardium is thin, since it is the thick pumping layer.
- It is a mistake to forget that the endocardium's smoothness prevents clotting.

---

## Consolidation and Self-Check

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Naming each layer and its role aloud is the strongest test.

- Name the three layers of the heart wall from memory.
- Describe the structure of each.
- State the function of each.
- Explain why the endocardium must be smooth.
- Relate one layer to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

---

## Key Takeaways

We have described the heart wall layers. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The heart wall has three layers: epicardium, myocardium, and endocardium.
- The epicardium covers the surface and is the visceral serous pericardium.
- The myocardium is the thick cardiac muscle that pumps blood.
- The endocardium is the smooth inner lining that prevents clotting.
- The myocardium's pumping underlies heart function and exercise tolerance.
