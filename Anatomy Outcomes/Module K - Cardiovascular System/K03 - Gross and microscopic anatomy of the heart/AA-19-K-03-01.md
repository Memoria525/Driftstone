# The Position of the Heart in the Thoracic Cavity

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the position of the heart in the thoracic cavity.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The heart sits in a specific place within the chest, between the lungs and behind the sternum, tilted so that its point lies toward the left. Understanding its position frames how it is examined and why chest compressions are placed where they are. Today we describe the position of the heart in the thoracic cavity.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe where the heart lies in the chest.
- You will be able to describe its orientation.
- You will be able to identify its base and apex.
- You will be able to connect the heart's position to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Tilted Pump Between the Lungs Behind the Sternum

The organising idea is that the heart sits in the central chest between the two lungs, behind the sternum, tilted so its pointed apex lies toward the lower left. A tilted central pump. This idea of a tilted central position frames the topic.

- The heart lies in the central part of the chest between the lungs.
- It sits behind the sternum.
- It is tilted so its pointed end lies toward the lower left.
- Most of its mass lies to the left of the midline.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Pump Set Slightly Off-Centre Behind a Shield

To keep the position concrete, picture a pump set in the central compartment of a machine, behind a protective shield, tilted so its nozzle points toward the lower left corner. The heart sits behind the sternum this way, and the image keeps its central, tilted position in view.

- The central compartment corresponds to the space between the lungs.
- The protective shield corresponds to the sternum in front.
- The nozzle pointing to the lower left corresponds to the apex of the heart.
- The analogy breaks down in that a pump is fixed, whereas the heart moves with each beat and with breathing.

---

## The Location in the Chest

Begin with where the heart lies, since its central position is key. It sits in the mediastinum.

- The heart lies in the mediastinum, the central region of the chest between the lungs.
- It sits behind the sternum and in front of the vertebral column.
- It rests on the diaphragm below.
- It is enclosed in a protective sac, the pericardium.

---

## The Orientation

Next its orientation, since it is tilted rather than upright. It leans toward the left.

- The heart is tilted so that it points toward the lower left.
- About two-thirds of its mass lies to the left of the midline.
- Its broad upper part is the base, and its pointed lower part is the apex.
- The apex points toward the lower left, where the heartbeat can be felt.

---

## The Base and Apex

Finally its base and apex, which orient the heart. These are its upper and lower ends.

- The base is the broad upper part of the heart, where the great vessels attach.
- The base faces upward and toward the right.
- The apex is the pointed lower tip of the heart.
- The apex lies toward the lower left, resting near the diaphragm.

---

## Why the Heart's Position Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The heart's position underlies its examination and emergency care relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The apex is where the heartbeat is felt and heard, relevant to assessing the heart.
- The heart's position behind the sternum underlies where chest compressions are applied in resuscitation.
- Its relationship to the lungs is relevant to cardiorespiratory assessment.
- Understanding its position clarifies how the heart is examined.
- Understanding it grounds your reasoning about the heart in the chest.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a pump set off-centre behind a shield.

- It is a mistake to think the heart lies on the far left, since it lies centrally but tilted to the left.
- It is a mistake to place the apex at the top, since it is the pointed lower tip.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the heart sits behind the sternum.
- It is a mistake to forget that the base, not the apex, is where the great vessels attach.

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Describing the position aloud is the strongest test.

- Describe where the heart lies in the chest from memory.
- Describe its orientation.
- Identify its base and apex.
- Explain where the heartbeat is felt.
- Relate the heart's position to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the heart's position. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The heart lies in the mediastinum, between the lungs and behind the sternum.
- It is tilted so its pointed apex lies toward the lower left.
- About two-thirds of its mass lies to the left of the midline.
- The base is the broad upper part where the great vessels attach, and the apex is the pointed lower tip.
- Its position underlies its examination and where chest compressions are applied.
