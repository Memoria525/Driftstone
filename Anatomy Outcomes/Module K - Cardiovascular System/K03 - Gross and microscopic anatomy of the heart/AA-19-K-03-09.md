## Define systole and diastole

# Systole and Diastole

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Define systole and diastole.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The heartbeat is a rhythm of two phases, contraction and relaxation, named systole and diastole. Defining them frames how the heart fills and empties and underlies blood pressure. Today we define systole and diastole.

- By the end of this session you will be able to define systole and diastole.
- You will be able to explain what happens in each phase.
- You will be able to relate the two phases to the heartbeat and blood pressure.
- You will be able to connect them to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: The Heartbeat Alternates Between Contraction and Relaxation

The organising idea is that each heartbeat alternates between systole, when a chamber contracts and ejects blood, and diastole, when it relaxes and fills, so the heart cycles between pumping and filling. Contract then relax. This idea of an alternating cycle frames the definitions.

- The heartbeat is a cycle of contraction and relaxation.
- Systole is the phase of contraction, when a chamber ejects blood.
- Diastole is the phase of relaxation, when a chamber fills with blood.
- The two phases alternate with every heartbeat.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Hand Squeezing and Releasing a Bulb

To keep the phases concrete, picture a hand rhythmically squeezing a fluid-filled bulb to push fluid out, then releasing it to let the bulb refill. Squeezing is systole and releasing is diastole, and the image keeps the alternating cycle in view.

- The squeeze pushing fluid out corresponds to systole ejecting blood.
- The release letting the bulb refill corresponds to diastole filling with blood.
- The rhythmic alternation corresponds to the heartbeat cycling between the two.
- The analogy breaks down in that a hand squeezes by choice, whereas the heart contracts automatically in a set rhythm.

---

## Defining Systole

Begin with systole, the contraction phase. It ejects blood.

- Systole is the phase of the heartbeat when the heart muscle contracts.
- During ventricular systole, the ventricles contract and eject blood into the arteries.
- The pressure in the arteries rises during systole.
- Systole is the pumping phase of the cycle.

---

## Defining Diastole

Next diastole, the relaxation phase. It allows filling.

- Diastole is the phase of the heartbeat when the heart muscle relaxes.
- During ventricular diastole, the ventricles relax and fill with blood.
- The pressure in the arteries falls during diastole.
- Diastole is the filling phase of the cycle.

---

## The Two Phases and Blood Pressure

Finally how the phases relate to blood pressure, since this is where the terms are most used. Blood pressure has two numbers.

- Blood pressure is often given as two numbers.
- The higher number reflects the pressure during systole.
- The lower number reflects the pressure during diastole.
- The two together describe the range of pressure with each heartbeat.

---

## Why Systole and Diastole Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

Systole and diastole underlie blood pressure and heart function relevant to your patients. Each point below connects them to practice.

- Blood pressure, given as systolic over diastolic, is monitored during exercise you supervise.
- The heart's filling in diastole and pumping in systole underlie its output during exercise.
- Changes in blood pressure with exercise are relevant to safety in cardiac rehabilitation.
- Understanding the phases clarifies how the heart pumps and how blood pressure is measured.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about the heartbeat and blood pressure.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors blur the two phases, and naming them keeps them distinct. When unsure, return to a hand squeezing and releasing a bulb.

- It is a mistake to swap the two, since systole is contraction and diastole is relaxation.
- It is a mistake to think the heart only pumps, since filling in diastole is equally essential.
- It is a mistake to confuse the systolic and diastolic blood pressure numbers.
- It is a mistake to forget that pressure rises in systole and falls in diastole.

## Key Takeaways

We have defined systole and diastole. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The heartbeat is a cycle of contraction and relaxation.
- Systole is the contraction phase, when a chamber ejects blood and arterial pressure rises.
- Diastole is the relaxation phase, when a chamber fills and arterial pressure falls.
- Blood pressure is given as systolic over diastolic.
- The two phases alternate with every heartbeat, pumping then filling.
