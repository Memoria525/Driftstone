## Compare and contrast pulmonary circulation and systemic circulation

# Pulmonary and Systemic Circulation Compared

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Compare and contrast pulmonary circulation and systemic circulation.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The circulation is really two loops in series, one to the lungs to pick up oxygen and one to the body to deliver it. Understanding the two loops explains how blood is oxygenated and distributed. Today we compare and contrast pulmonary and systemic circulation.

- By the end of this session you will be able to define pulmonary and systemic circulation.
- You will be able to contrast the route of each.
- You will be able to contrast the blood each carries.
- You will be able to connect the two loops to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Two Loops, One to the Lungs and One to the Body

The organising idea is that the circulation has two loops in series, the pulmonary loop carrying blood to the lungs to be oxygenated and the systemic loop carrying oxygenated blood to the body. A lung loop and a body loop. This idea of two loops frames the comparison.

- The circulation consists of two loops driven by the heart.
- The pulmonary circulation carries blood to and from the lungs.
- The systemic circulation carries blood to and from the rest of the body.
- The two loops are connected in series through the heart.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Refuelling Loop and a Delivery Loop

To keep the two loops concrete, picture a fleet that runs a short refuelling loop to a filling station and a long delivery loop to customers, the two loops linked at a central depot. The pulmonary loop refuels the blood with oxygen and the systemic loop delivers it, and the image keeps the two loops in view.

- The short refuelling loop corresponds to the pulmonary circulation to the lungs.
- The long delivery loop corresponds to the systemic circulation to the body.
- The central depot corresponds to the heart linking the two loops.
- The analogy breaks down in that a fleet uses one set of vehicles, whereas the two loops move the same blood through different vessels.

---

## The Pulmonary Circulation

Begin with the loop to the lungs, which oxygenates the blood. It is the shorter loop.

- The pulmonary circulation carries blood from the right side of the heart to the lungs and back to the left side.
- In the lungs, the blood picks up oxygen and releases carbon dioxide.
- It carries deoxygenated blood to the lungs and returns oxygenated blood.
- It is the shorter, lower-pressure loop.

---

## The Systemic Circulation

Next the loop to the body, which delivers the oxygen. It is the longer loop.

- The systemic circulation carries blood from the left side of the heart to the body and back to the right side.
- In the body, the blood delivers oxygen and picks up carbon dioxide.
- It carries oxygenated blood to the body and returns deoxygenated blood.
- It is the longer, higher-pressure loop.

---

## Comparing the Two Loops

Holding the two together lets us state the comparison cleanly, which the objective asks. They differ in route, pressure, and the blood carried.

- The pulmonary loop goes to the lungs, while the systemic loop goes to the body.
- The pulmonary loop oxygenates the blood, while the systemic loop delivers the oxygen.
- The pulmonary loop is shorter and lower-pressure, while the systemic loop is longer and higher-pressure.
- In the pulmonary loop the arteries carry deoxygenated blood, the reverse of the usual systemic pattern.

---

## Why This Comparison Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The two loops underlie oxygen delivery and exercise tolerance central to your patients. Each point below connects the comparison to practice.

- The pulmonary loop oxygenates the blood, central to the breathing and gas exchange in cardiorespiratory care.
- The systemic loop delivers oxygen to the muscles, underlying exercise tolerance.
- Understanding the two loops clarifies how blood is oxygenated and distributed.
- Heart and lung conditions affect these loops, relevant to exercise capacity.
- Understanding the comparison grounds your reasoning about circulation and exercise.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors blur the two loops, and naming them keeps them distinct. When unsure, return to a refuelling loop and a delivery loop.

- It is a mistake to think arteries always carry oxygenated blood, since pulmonary arteries carry deoxygenated blood.
- It is a mistake to confuse which side of the heart drives each loop, since the right drives the pulmonary and the left the systemic.
- It is a mistake to treat the two loops as separate, since they are connected in series.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the systemic loop is higher-pressure.

## Key Takeaways

We have compared the two circulatory loops. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The circulation has a pulmonary loop to the lungs and a systemic loop to the body.
- The pulmonary loop oxygenates the blood, carrying deoxygenated blood to the lungs and returning oxygenated blood.
- The systemic loop delivers oxygen, carrying oxygenated blood to the body and returning deoxygenated blood.
- The right heart drives the pulmonary loop and the left heart the systemic loop.
- In the pulmonary loop, the arteries carry deoxygenated blood, reversing the usual pattern.
