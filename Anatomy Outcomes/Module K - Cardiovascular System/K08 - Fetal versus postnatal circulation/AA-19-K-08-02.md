## Trace the pathway of blood flow from the placenta, through the fetal heart and body, and back to the placenta

# Tracing Fetal Blood Flow

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Trace the pathway of blood flow from the placenta, through the fetal heart and body, and back to the placenta.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

Fetal blood follows a route quite different from the adult, using the placenta for oxygen and shortcuts that bypass the liver and lungs. Tracing it ties the special fetal structures into one continuous circuit. Today we trace the pathway of blood from the placenta, through the fetal heart and body, and back.

- By the end of this session you will be able to trace fetal blood from the placenta to the heart.
- You will be able to trace it through the heart and to the body.
- You will be able to trace it back to the placenta.
- You will be able to connect the pathway to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Circuit That Loads Oxygen at the Placenta and Bypasses the Lungs

The organising idea is that fetal blood picks up oxygen at the placenta, travels to the heart while bypassing the liver, is routed past the lungs to the body, and returns to the placenta to reload. Placenta to body, bypassing the lungs. This idea of a placental circuit frames the topic.

- Fetal blood picks up oxygen at the placenta.
- It travels to the heart, bypassing much of the liver.
- It is routed past the lungs to reach the body.
- It returns to the placenta to reload with oxygen.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Delivery Loop Serving a Building from an Outside Depot

To keep the pathway concrete, picture a delivery loop that loads at an outside depot, distributes to a building while bypassing its idle internal plant, and returns to the depot to reload. The fetal circuit loads at the placenta, bypasses the lungs, serves the body, and returns, and the image keeps the loop in view.

- Loading at the outside depot corresponds to picking up oxygen at the placenta.
- Bypassing the idle plant corresponds to bypassing the lungs.
- Distributing to the building corresponds to supplying the fetal body.
- The analogy breaks down in that a delivery loop is planned, whereas the fetal circuit is a fixed arrangement of vessels.

---

## From the Placenta to the Heart

Begin with the path from the placenta to the heart. Oxygen-rich blood travels inward.

- Oxygen-rich blood leaves the placenta in the umbilical vein.
- Much of it passes through the ductus venosus, bypassing the liver.
- It joins the blood in the inferior vena cava heading to the heart.
- It enters the right atrium of the fetal heart.

---

## Through the Heart, Bypassing the Lungs

Next the path through the heart, using the lung bypasses. Blood is routed past the lungs.

- From the right atrium, much of the blood passes through the foramen ovale into the left atrium, bypassing the lungs.
- This blood passes into the left ventricle and out into the aorta to the body.
- Blood that does enter the right ventricle and pulmonary artery mostly passes through the ductus arteriosus into the aorta, again bypassing the lungs.
- In both ways, blood is routed to the body rather than the non-functioning lungs.

---

## Back to the Placenta

Finally the return to the placenta, to reload with oxygen. The umbilical arteries carry blood back.

- The blood supplies the fetal body, delivering oxygen and nutrients.
- Oxygen-poor blood collects and travels toward the umbilical arteries.
- The umbilical arteries carry this blood back to the placenta.
- At the placenta, the blood reloads with oxygen, and the circuit repeats.

---

## Why This Pathway Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The fetal pathway underlies development and congenital conditions relevant to paediatric practice. Each point below connects it to practice.

- Understanding the pathway grounds the study of newborn and congenital circulation.
- The changes at birth follow from this pathway, essential for understanding newborn circulation.
- Congenital conditions arise when the pathway or its structures do not change normally.
- These conditions affect children you may treat.
- Understanding the pathway grounds your reasoning about development.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the pathway, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a delivery loop serving a building from an outside depot.

- It is a mistake to think fetal blood passes through the lungs normally, since it mostly bypasses them.
- It is a mistake to confuse the umbilical vein and arteries in the circuit.
- It is a mistake to overlook the ductus venosus bypassing the liver.
- It is a mistake to forget that the circuit returns to the placenta to reload.

- Trace fetal blood from the placenta to the heart from memory.

---

## Key Takeaways

We have traced the fetal blood pathway. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- Oxygen-rich blood leaves the placenta in the umbilical vein and bypasses the liver via the ductus venosus.
- It enters the right atrium and mostly passes through the foramen ovale to the left side and out to the body.
- Blood in the pulmonary artery mostly passes through the ductus arteriosus to the aorta, bypassing the lungs.
- The umbilical arteries carry oxygen-poor blood back to the placenta to reload.
- The circuit loads oxygen at the placenta and bypasses the non-functioning lungs.
