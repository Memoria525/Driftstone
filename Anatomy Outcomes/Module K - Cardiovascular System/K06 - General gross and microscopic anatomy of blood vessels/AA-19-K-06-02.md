## Compare and contrast which blood vessels transport oxygenated versus deoxygenated blood within the pulmonary and systemic circulations

# Oxygenated and Deoxygenated Blood in the Circulations

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Compare and contrast which blood vessels transport oxygenated versus deoxygenated blood within the pulmonary and systemic circulations.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

A common rule says arteries carry oxygenated blood and veins carry deoxygenated blood, but the pulmonary circulation reverses this. Understanding the exception explains how the two loops handle oxygen. Today we compare and contrast which vessels carry oxygenated versus deoxygenated blood in the pulmonary and systemic circulations.

- By the end of this session you will be able to state which vessels carry oxygenated blood in each circulation.
- You will be able to explain why the pulmonary circulation reverses the usual pattern.
- You will be able to state the correct definition of arteries and veins.
- You will be able to connect this to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Direction, Not Oxygen, Defines Arteries and Veins

The organising idea is that arteries and veins are defined by the direction they carry blood, not by whether it is oxygenated, so the pulmonary circulation reverses the usual oxygenation pattern. Direction defines the vessel. This idea of direction over oxygen frames the comparison.

- Arteries carry blood away from the heart, and veins carry it back, regardless of oxygenation.
- In the systemic circulation, arteries carry oxygenated blood and veins carry deoxygenated blood.
- In the pulmonary circulation, the arteries carry deoxygenated blood and the veins carry oxygenated blood.
- The pulmonary circulation reverses the usual oxygenation pattern.

---

## Our Master Analogy: Delivery Trucks Defined by Direction, Not Cargo

To keep the idea concrete, picture trucks defined as outbound or inbound by their direction, not by what they carry, so an outbound truck could carry either full or empty loads depending on the route. Arteries and veins are defined by direction, and the pulmonary route carries the opposite oxygen load. The image keeps direction over cargo in view.

- The outbound truck corresponds to the artery carrying blood away from the heart.
- The inbound truck corresponds to the vein carrying blood back.
- The load differing by route corresponds to the pulmonary vessels carrying the opposite oxygen state.
- The analogy breaks down in that a truck's cargo is chosen, whereas the blood's oxygen state depends on where it has just been.

---

## The Systemic Circulation

Begin with the systemic circulation, which follows the usual rule. Arteries carry oxygenated blood here.

- In the systemic circulation, arteries carry oxygenated blood from the heart to the body.
- The body's tissues take up the oxygen.
- The veins then carry deoxygenated blood back to the heart.
- This is the pattern most people first learn.

---

## The Pulmonary Circulation

Next the pulmonary circulation, which reverses the rule. Arteries carry deoxygenated blood here.

- In the pulmonary circulation, the pulmonary arteries carry deoxygenated blood from the heart to the lungs.
- The blood picks up oxygen in the lungs.
- The pulmonary veins then carry oxygenated blood back to the heart.
- This reverses the usual oxygenation pattern.

---

## Why the Pattern Reverses

Finally why the reversal happens, since it follows from the definitions. Direction defines the vessel.

- Arteries are defined by carrying blood away from the heart, whatever its oxygen state.
- The pulmonary arteries carry blood away from the heart to the lungs, so they are arteries even though the blood is deoxygenated.
- The pulmonary veins carry blood back to the heart, so they are veins even though the blood is oxygenated.
- The reversal shows that direction, not oxygen, defines arteries and veins.

## Why This Comparison Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The comparison underlies a correct understanding of circulation relevant to cardiorespiratory care. Each point below connects it to practice.

- Understanding the pulmonary reversal clarifies how blood is oxygenated in the lungs.
- The correct definition of arteries and veins prevents errors in reasoning about circulation.
- The oxygenation of blood underlies exercise tolerance you assess.
- Cardiorespiratory conditions affect these loops, relevant to your patients.
- Understanding the comparison grounds your reasoning about circulation.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to trucks defined by direction, not cargo.

- It is a mistake to define arteries as carrying oxygenated blood, since the pulmonary arteries carry deoxygenated blood.
- It is a mistake to think veins always carry deoxygenated blood, since the pulmonary veins carry oxygenated blood.
- It is a mistake to forget that direction, not oxygen, defines the vessel.
- It is a mistake to overlook the pulmonary reversal.
