# The Segments of the Small Intestine

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Identify the specific segments of the small intestine (duodenum, jejunum, ileum), including their relative length.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The small intestine has three named segments in order, from the short first part that receives secretions to the long final part that joins the large intestine. Learning them lets you locate where digestion and absorption occur along its length. Today we identify the segments of the small intestine and their relative length.

- By the end of this session you will be able to name the three segments in order.
- You will be able to describe each segment.
- You will be able to state their relative lengths.
- You will be able to connect the segments to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Short Duodenum, Then the Longer Jejunum and Ileum

The organising idea is that the small intestine has three segments in order, the short duodenum that receives secretions, the middle jejunum, and the long ileum that joins the large intestine. A short start, then two longer parts. This idea of three ordered segments frames the topic.

- The small intestine has three segments in order.
- The duodenum is the short first segment.
- The jejunum is the middle segment.
- The ileum is the long final segment that joins the large intestine.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Pipeline in Three Named Sections

To keep the segments concrete, picture a pipeline in three named sections, a short first section where inputs join, a middle section, and a long final section. The small intestine's segments run this way, and the image keeps the three sections in view.

- The short first section where inputs join corresponds to the duodenum receiving secretions.
- The middle section corresponds to the jejunum.
- The long final section corresponds to the ileum.
- The analogy breaks down in that a pipeline is rigid, whereas the small intestine is soft and coiled.

---

## The Duodenum

Begin with the duodenum, the short first segment. It receives secretions.

- The duodenum is the first and shortest segment of the small intestine.
- It receives the food from the stomach.
- It receives bile from the liver and enzymes from the pancreas.
- Much of the mixing with these secretions occurs here.

---

## The Jejunum

Next the jejunum, the middle segment. It is a main site of absorption.

- The jejunum is the middle segment of the small intestine.
- It follows the duodenum.
- It is a main site of digestion and absorption.
- Its lining has abundant folds and projections for absorption.

---

## The Ileum

Finally the ileum, the long final segment. It joins the large intestine.

- The ileum is the last and longest segment of the small intestine.
- It continues absorption.
- It contains clusters of immune tissue in its wall.
- It joins the large intestine at its end.

---

## Why the Segments Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The segments underlie digestion and absorption along the small intestine relevant to your patients. Each point below connects a segment to practice.

- The duodenum receives the secretions that complete digestion, relevant to nutrition.
- The jejunum and ileum absorb most nutrients, relevant to nutrition and recovery.
- Conditions affecting particular segments affect absorption differently.
- Understanding the segments clarifies where digestion and absorption occur.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about the small intestine.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a pipeline in three named sections.

- It is a mistake to muddle the order of the segments.
- It is a mistake to think the duodenum is long, since it is the shortest segment.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the duodenum receives bile and pancreatic enzymes.
- It is a mistake to forget that the ileum joins the large intestine.

## Consolidation and Self-Check

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Naming the segments in order aloud is the strongest test.

- Name the three segments in order from memory.
- Describe each segment.
- State their relative lengths.
- Explain what the duodenum receives.
- Relate one segment to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the segments of the small intestine. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The small intestine has three segments in order: duodenum, jejunum, and ileum.
- The duodenum is the short first segment that receives bile and pancreatic enzymes.
- The jejunum is the middle segment and a main site of absorption.
- The ileum is the long final segment that joins the large intestine.
- Together they complete digestion and absorb most nutrients.
