## Compare and contrast the number, function and first general appearance of deciduous teeth versus permanent teeth

# Deciduous and Permanent Teeth Compared

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Compare and contrast the number, function and first general appearance of deciduous teeth versus permanent teeth.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

Humans have two sets of teeth in a lifetime, the baby teeth that appear in childhood and the adult teeth that replace them. Understanding their number, function, and timing frames the development of the teeth. Today we compare and contrast the deciduous and permanent teeth.

- By the end of this session you will be able to state the number of each set of teeth.
- You will be able to state their function.
- You will be able to describe when each first appears.
- You will be able to connect the comparison to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A First Set of Twenty Baby Teeth Replaced by Thirty-Two Adult Teeth

The organising idea is that humans first grow a set of twenty deciduous, or baby, teeth in childhood, which are later replaced by a set of thirty-two permanent, or adult, teeth. Twenty baby teeth, thirty-two adult teeth. This idea of two successive sets frames the comparison.

- Humans have two sets of teeth in a lifetime.
- The deciduous, or baby, teeth are the first set, numbering twenty.
- The permanent, or adult, teeth are the second set, numbering thirty-two.
- The permanent teeth replace the deciduous teeth.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Temporary Set of Tools Replaced by a Full Set

To keep the comparison concrete, picture a starter set of twenty tools for a growing workshop, later replaced by a full set of thirty-two as the workshop matures. The deciduous teeth are the starter set and the permanent teeth the full set, and the image keeps the two successive sets in view.

- The starter set of tools corresponds to the twenty deciduous teeth.
- The full set corresponds to the thirty-two permanent teeth.
- Replacing the starter set corresponds to the permanent teeth replacing the deciduous.
- The analogy breaks down in that tools are swapped by hand, whereas the teeth are replaced through natural development.

---

## The Number of Each Set

Begin with the number, the clearest difference. The two sets differ in count.

- The deciduous teeth number twenty in a full set.
- The permanent teeth number thirty-two in a full set.
- The permanent set has more teeth, including the molars at the back.
- The extra permanent teeth appear as the jaw grows larger.

---

## The Function of the Teeth

Next their function, which is shared by both sets. Teeth break down food.

- Both sets of teeth break food down by chewing.
- Different teeth have different shapes for different tasks.
- The front teeth cut, and the back teeth grind.
- Chewing is a form of mechanical digestion.

---

## The First Appearance of Each Set

Finally when each set first appears, since the timing differs. The two sets emerge at different ages.

- The deciduous teeth first appear in infancy, beginning at around six months of age.
- They are gradually replaced from around six years of age.
- The permanent teeth appear from around six years, replacing the deciduous teeth.
- The last permanent teeth, the wisdom teeth, appear in early adulthood.

---

## Why This Comparison Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The comparison underlies dental development relevant to some patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The teeth break down food, relevant to nutrition across the lifespan.
- Problems with the teeth affect chewing and nutrition in some patients.
- The timing of the teeth is relevant to child development.
- Understanding the comparison clarifies the development of the teeth.
- Understanding it grounds your reasoning about chewing and nutrition.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the comparison, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a temporary set of tools replaced by a full set.

- It is a mistake to give both sets the same number, since the deciduous set has twenty and the permanent thirty-two.
- It is a mistake to think the deciduous teeth appear at birth, since they begin at around six months.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the permanent teeth replace the deciduous.
- It is a mistake to forget that the wisdom teeth appear in early adulthood.

---

## Consolidation and Self-Check

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Contrasting the two sets aloud is the strongest test.

- State the number of each set of teeth from memory.
- State their function.
- Describe when each first appears.
- Explain why the permanent set has more teeth.
- Relate the comparison to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

---

## Key Takeaways

We have compared the deciduous and permanent teeth. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- Humans have two sets of teeth in a lifetime.
- The deciduous teeth number twenty, and the permanent teeth number thirty-two.
- Both sets break food down by chewing, with front teeth cutting and back teeth grinding.
- The deciduous teeth appear from around six months, and the permanent teeth from around six years.
- The permanent teeth replace the deciduous, with the wisdom teeth appearing in early adulthood.
