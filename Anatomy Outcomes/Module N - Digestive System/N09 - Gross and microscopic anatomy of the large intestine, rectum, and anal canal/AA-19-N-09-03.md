# The Functions of the Large Intestine, Rectum, and Anal Canal

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the general functions of the large intestine, rectum, and anal canal.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The large intestine absorbs water from the remaining material, forms it into faeces, and stores and eliminates it. Understanding these functions frames the end of digestion. Today we describe the functions of the large intestine, rectum, and anal canal.

- By the end of this session you will be able to list the functions of the large intestine.

## The Big Idea: Absorb Water, Form Faeces, and Store and Eliminate Them

The organising idea is that the large intestine absorbs water from the remaining material, forms it into faeces, and the rectum and anal canal store and eliminate them. Absorb water, form, store, eliminate. This idea of the final steps frames the functions.

- The large intestine absorbs water from the remaining material.
- It forms the material into faeces.
- The rectum stores the faeces.
- The anal canal eliminates them.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Drying and Disposal Stage

To keep the functions concrete, picture a final stage that dries out the remaining material, compacts it into a form, and holds it until disposal. The large intestine dries and forms the faeces, and the rectum and anal canal store and dispose of them, and the image keeps the drying and disposal in view.

- Drying out the material corresponds to absorbing water.
- Compacting it corresponds to forming faeces.
- Holding it until disposal corresponds to storing and eliminating.
- The analogy breaks down in that a disposal stage is machinery, whereas the large intestine is living tissue that also hosts helpful bacteria.

---

## The Water Absorption and Faeces Formation Functions

Begin with water absorption and faeces formation, the main colon functions. The colon dries and forms the material.

- The large intestine absorbs water from the remaining material.
- This dries out the material and reduces its volume.
- It forms the material into faeces.
- Bacteria in the colon also help break down some remaining material and produce some vitamins.

---

## The Storage Function

Next storage, the role of the rectum. It holds the faeces before elimination.

- The rectum stores the faeces after they are formed.
- It holds them until it is convenient to eliminate them.
- As the rectum fills, it signals the need to eliminate.
- This lets elimination be controlled and delayed.

---

## The Elimination Function

Finally elimination, the role of the anal canal. It removes the faeces from the body.

- The anal canal eliminates the faeces.
- Sphincters guard the anal canal and control elimination.
- Relaxing the sphincters allows the faeces to pass.
- This completes the passage of material through the digestive system.

---

## Why These Functions Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The functions underlie bowel function and continence relevant to your patients. Each point below connects a function to practice.

- Water absorption affects the consistency of the faeces, relevant to constipation and diarrhoea.
- The storage and controlled elimination underlie continence, relevant to pelvic-health practice.
- The sphincters control elimination, relevant to bowel continence.
- Understanding the functions clarifies the end of digestion.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about bowel function.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors narrow the functions, and naming them keeps them complete. When unsure, return to a drying and disposal stage.

- It is a mistake to think the large intestine absorbs many nutrients, since it mainly absorbs water.
- It is a mistake to overlook the role of the colon's bacteria.
- It is a mistake to forget that the rectum stores the faeces.
- It is a mistake to overlook the sphincters controlling elimination.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the functions of the large intestine, rectum, and anal canal. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The large intestine absorbs water and forms the remaining material into faeces.
- Bacteria in the colon help break down some material and produce some vitamins.
