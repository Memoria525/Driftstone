# The Segments and Flexures of the Large Intestine

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Identify the specific segments and related flexures of the large intestine.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The large intestine is divided into named segments connected by bends called flexures as it frames the abdomen. Learning them lets you follow the path of the bowel and locate its parts. Today we identify the segments and flexures of the large intestine.

- By the end of this session you will be able to name the segments of the large intestine in order.
- You will be able to name the flexures.
- You will be able to describe the path around the abdomen.
- You will be able to connect the segments to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Named Segments Connected by Bends Framing the Abdomen

The organising idea is that the large intestine has named segments connected by bends, or flexures, running up the right side, across the top, and down the left. Segments joined by bends around the abdomen. This idea of a segmented framing path frames the topic.

- The large intestine has named segments in order.
- The segments are connected by bends called flexures.
- It runs up the right side, across the top, and down the left.
- It ends at the rectum.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Route With Named Stretches and Corners

To keep the segments concrete, picture a route around the edge of a square, with named stretches connected by corners. The large intestine's segments are the stretches and its flexures the corners, and the image keeps the framing path in view.

- The named stretches correspond to the segments of the colon.
- The corners correspond to the flexures.
- Running around the edge of the square corresponds to framing the abdomen.
- The analogy breaks down in that a route is fixed, whereas parts of the colon are mobile.

---

## The First Segments

Begin with the first segments, from the start up the right side. These lead up to the first flexure.

- The large intestine begins with the caecum in the lower right abdomen, where the small intestine joins.
- The appendix hangs from the caecum.
- The ascending colon runs up the right side of the abdomen.
- It bends at the right colic flexure, near the liver, to become the next segment.

---

## The Middle Segments

Next the middle segments, across the top and down the left. These connect the two flexures.

- The transverse colon runs across the upper abdomen.
- It bends at the left colic flexure, near the spleen.
- The descending colon runs down the left side of the abdomen.
- These segments connect the right side to the lower left.

---

## The Final Segments

Finally the final segments, leading to the rectum. These lie in the lower left and pelvis.

- The sigmoid colon is the S-shaped segment in the lower left abdomen.
- It leads to the rectum.
- The rectum stores faeces in the pelvis.
- The anal canal is the final passage to the anus.

---

## Why the Segments Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The segments underlie the path of the bowel relevant to your patients. Each point below connects a segment to practice.

- Knowing the segments helps you locate where a bowel condition affects the colon.
- The position of pain along the colon can point to a particular segment.
- The appendix, at the caecum, is the site of appendicitis, relevant to recognising abdominal pain.
- Understanding the segments clarifies the path of the bowel.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about the large intestine.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a route with named stretches and corners.

- It is a mistake to muddle the order of the segments.
- It is a mistake to overlook the flexures connecting the segments.
- It is a mistake to place the appendix anywhere but at the caecum.
- It is a mistake to forget that the sigmoid colon leads to the rectum.

## Consolidation and Self-Check

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Naming the segments in order aloud is the strongest test.

- Name the segments of the large intestine in order from memory.
- Name the flexures.
- Describe the path around the abdomen.
- State where the appendix is located.
- Relate one segment to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

---

## Key Takeaways

We have described the segments and flexures of the large intestine. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The large intestine begins with the caecum, from which the appendix hangs.
- The ascending colon runs up the right, bending at the right colic flexure.
- The transverse colon crosses the top, bending at the left colic flexure.
- The descending colon runs down the left, leading to the sigmoid colon and the rectum.
- The segments are connected by flexures as the colon frames the abdomen.
