## Compare and contrast the location, composition, and innervation (somatic versus autonomic) of the internal and external anal sphincters

# The Internal and External Anal Sphincters

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Compare and contrast the location, composition, and innervation (somatic versus autonomic) of the internal and external anal sphincters.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The anal canal is guarded by two sphincters, one automatic and one under voluntary control, that together control the elimination of faeces. Understanding them explains how continence is maintained. Today we compare and contrast the internal and external anal sphincters.

- By the end of this session you will be able to locate the two sphincters.
- You will be able to describe their composition.
- You will be able to contrast their innervation and control.
- You will be able to connect them to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: An Automatic Inner Sphincter and a Voluntary Outer Sphincter

The organising idea is that the anal canal has an inner sphincter of smooth muscle under automatic control and an outer sphincter of skeletal muscle under voluntary control, together maintaining continence. Automatic inner, voluntary outer. This idea of two complementary sphincters frames the comparison.

- The anal canal is guarded by two sphincters.
- The internal anal sphincter is smooth muscle under automatic control.
- The external anal sphincter is skeletal muscle under voluntary control.
- Together they control the elimination of faeces.

---

## Our Master Analogy: An Automatic Gate Backed by a Manually Held Gate

To keep the sphincters concrete, picture an automatic gate that closes by itself, backed by a second gate you can hold shut by hand. The internal sphincter closes automatically and the external sphincter is held voluntarily, and the image keeps the automatic and voluntary control in view.

- The automatic gate corresponds to the internal anal sphincter.
- The manually held gate corresponds to the external anal sphincter.
- Holding the second gate shut corresponds to voluntary control of the external sphincter.
- The analogy breaks down in that gates are mechanical, whereas the sphincters are living muscle controlled by nerves.

---

## The Location and Composition

Begin with location and composition, since the two differ. One is inner and smooth, the other outer and skeletal.

- The internal anal sphincter is the inner ring, made of smooth muscle.
- It is a thickening of the smooth muscle of the anal canal wall.
- The external anal sphincter is the outer ring, made of skeletal muscle.
- It surrounds the internal sphincter.

---

## The Internal Sphincter's Innervation

Next the internal sphincter's control, which is automatic. It is controlled by the autonomic nervous system.

- The internal anal sphincter is controlled by the autonomic nervous system.
- It is not under voluntary control.
- It relaxes automatically when the rectum fills.
- Its automatic relaxation signals the need to eliminate.

---

## The External Sphincter's Innervation

Finally the external sphincter's control, which is voluntary. It is controlled by the somatic nervous system.

- The external anal sphincter is controlled by the somatic nervous system.
- It is under voluntary control.
- It can be held closed to delay elimination even when the internal sphincter relaxes.
- Relaxing it voluntarily allows elimination.

---

## Why This Comparison Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The sphincters underlie bowel continence relevant to pelvic-health practice. Each point below connects the comparison to practice.

- The external sphincter's voluntary control lets elimination be delayed, central to continence.
- Weakness of the external sphincter can cause incontinence you may help manage.
- Pelvic-floor exercises strengthen the voluntary external sphincter.
- Understanding the two sphincters clarifies how continence is maintained.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about bowel continence.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors blur the two, and naming them keeps them distinct. When unsure, return to an automatic gate backed by a manually held gate.

- It is a mistake to think both sphincters are under voluntary control, since only the external is.
- It is a mistake to give the internal sphincter skeletal muscle, since it is smooth muscle.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the internal sphincter relaxes automatically when the rectum fills.
- It is a mistake to forget that the external sphincter can delay elimination.

## Consolidation and Self-Check

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Contrasting the two aloud is the strongest test.

- Locate the two sphincters from memory.
- Describe their composition.
- Contrast their innervation and control.
- Explain how the external sphincter allows elimination to be delayed.
- Relate the comparison to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

## Key Takeaways

We have compared the internal and external anal sphincters. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The anal canal is guarded by an internal and an external sphincter.
- The internal sphincter is smooth muscle under automatic control, relaxing when the rectum fills.
- The external sphincter is skeletal muscle under voluntary control.
- The external sphincter can be held closed to delay elimination.
- Pelvic-floor exercises strengthen the voluntary external sphincter, central to continence.
