# The Sphincters of the Stomach

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the compositions, locations and functions of the inferior esophageal (cardiac, lower esophageal) sphincter and the pyloric sphincter.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The stomach has a sphincter at each end, one guarding the entrance from the oesophagus and one controlling the exit into the small intestine. Understanding them explains how the stomach holds its contents and empties them gradually. Today we describe the two sphincters of the stomach.

- By the end of this session you will be able to locate the two sphincters.
- You will be able to describe their composition.
- You will be able to state their functions.
- You will be able to connect the sphincters to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Ring of Muscle at Each End Controlling Entry and Exit

The organising idea is that the stomach has a ring of muscle at each end, the lower oesophageal sphincter guarding the entrance from the oesophagus and the pyloric sphincter controlling the exit to the small intestine. A guarding ring at each end. This idea of two controlling rings frames the topic.

- The stomach has a sphincter at each end.
- Each sphincter is a ring of muscle.
- The lower oesophageal sphincter guards the entrance from the oesophagus.
- The pyloric sphincter controls the exit into the small intestine.

---

## Our Master Analogy: Valves at the Inlet and Outlet of a Tank

To keep the sphincters concrete, picture a tank with a valve at its inlet and a valve at its outlet, each opening to let contents through and closing to hold them. The stomach's sphincters are these valves at its entrance and exit, and the image keeps the two controlling valves in view.

- The inlet valve corresponds to the lower oesophageal sphincter.
- The outlet valve corresponds to the pyloric sphincter.
- Opening and closing to control flow corresponds to the sphincters controlling entry and exit.
- The analogy breaks down in that valves are mechanical, whereas the sphincters are rings of living muscle.

---

## The Lower Oesophageal Sphincter

Begin with the entrance sphincter, the lower oesophageal sphincter. It guards against reflux.

- The lower oesophageal sphincter is at the junction of the oesophagus and stomach.
- It is a ring of smooth muscle.
- It opens to let swallowed food pass into the stomach.
- It closes afterward to prevent stomach contents from rising into the oesophagus.

---

## The Pyloric Sphincter

Next the exit sphincter, the pyloric sphincter. It controls emptying.

- The pyloric sphincter is at the junction of the stomach and small intestine.
- It is a ring of smooth muscle, thicker than the wall around it.
- It opens to let small amounts of stomach contents into the small intestine.
- It controls the gradual emptying of the stomach.

---

## The Functions of the Sphincters

Finally the functions together, since the two sphincters control the stomach's ends. They hold and release its contents.

- The lower oesophageal sphincter keeps the stomach contents in the stomach.
- The pyloric sphincter keeps the contents in the stomach until they are ready to move on.
- Together they let the stomach hold and churn its contents.
- The pyloric sphincter then releases the contents gradually.

---

## Why the Sphincters Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The sphincters underlie the stomach's control and reflux relevant to your patients. Each point below connects them to practice.

- Failure of the lower oesophageal sphincter causes reflux, where stomach acid rises and irritates the oesophagus.
- Reflux can cause chest discomfort you may need to recognise.
- The pyloric sphincter controls emptying, relevant to how meals are handled.
- Understanding the sphincters clarifies how the stomach holds and releases its contents.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about the stomach and reflux.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors blur the two, and naming them keeps them distinct. When unsure, return to valves at the inlet and outlet of a tank.

- It is a mistake to swap the two sphincters, since one guards the entrance and the other the exit.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the lower oesophageal sphincter prevents reflux.
- It is a mistake to think the pyloric sphincter empties the stomach all at once, since it releases contents gradually.
- It is a mistake to forget that both are rings of muscle.

- Locate the two sphincters from memory.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the two sphincters of the stomach. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The stomach has a sphincter at each end, each a ring of muscle.
- The lower oesophageal sphincter guards the entrance and prevents reflux.
- The pyloric sphincter controls the exit and empties the stomach gradually.
- Together they let the stomach hold and churn its contents before release.
- Failure of the lower oesophageal sphincter causes reflux.
