## Identify and describe the gross anatomy of the stomach, including its location relative to other body structures

# The Gross Anatomy of the Stomach

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Identify and describe the gross anatomy of the stomach, including its location relative to other body structures.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The stomach is a muscular, expandable bag that sits in the upper left abdomen, receiving food from the oesophagus and churning it before passing it to the small intestine. Understanding its shape and location frames its role. Today we identify and describe the gross anatomy of the stomach and its location.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe the shape of the stomach.
- You will be able to describe its location.
- You will be able to describe its relationship to other structures.
- You will be able to connect the stomach's anatomy to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: An Expandable J-Shaped Bag in the Upper Left Abdomen

The organising idea is that the stomach is an expandable, J-shaped muscular bag in the upper left abdomen, connecting the oesophagus above to the small intestine below. An expandable J-shaped bag. This idea of an expandable bag frames the topic.

- The stomach is an expandable, muscular bag.
- It is roughly J-shaped.
- It lies in the upper left part of the abdomen.
- It connects the oesophagus above to the small intestine below.

---

## Our Master Analogy: An Expandable Mixing Chamber Between Two Tubes

To keep the stomach concrete, picture an expandable mixing chamber connected between an inlet tube and an outlet tube, that fills, churns its contents, and empties them onward. The stomach is this mixing chamber between the oesophagus and small intestine, and the image keeps the expandable bag in view.

- The expandable mixing chamber corresponds to the stomach.
- The inlet tube corresponds to the oesophagus.
- The outlet tube corresponds to the small intestine.
- The analogy breaks down in that a chamber is rigid machinery, whereas the stomach is living muscle that expands and secretes.

---

## The Shape of the Stomach

Begin with the shape, since the stomach is a distinctive bag. It is J-shaped and expandable.

- The stomach is a J-shaped bag with a curved outline.
- Its inner curve is the lesser curvature and its outer curve is the greater curvature.
- It can expand greatly to hold a meal.
- Its wall is muscular, allowing it to churn its contents.

---

## The Location of the Stomach

Next its location, in the upper left abdomen. It sits below the diaphragm.

- The stomach lies in the upper left part of the abdomen.
- It sits just below the diaphragm.
- Most of it lies to the left of the midline.
- Its position shifts somewhat as it fills and empties.

---

## The Relationship to Other Structures

Finally its relationship to other structures, since these matter. It lies near several organs.

- The oesophagus enters the stomach from above, through the diaphragm.
- The small intestine leaves the stomach below.
- The liver lies to the right of the stomach, and the spleen to its left.
- The pancreas lies behind the stomach.

---

## Why the Stomach's Anatomy Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The stomach's anatomy underlies digestion and related conditions relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The stomach churns and begins digesting food, relevant to digestion and nutrition.
- Its position below the diaphragm is relevant to reflux, where its contents rise into the oesophagus.
- Its expandability lets it hold a meal, relevant to eating and comfort.
- Understanding its anatomy clarifies its role in digestion.
- Understanding it grounds your reasoning about the stomach.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to an expandable mixing chamber between two tubes.

- It is a mistake to place the stomach in the lower abdomen, since it lies in the upper left.
- It is a mistake to think the stomach is a fixed size, since it expands to hold a meal.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the pancreas lies behind it.
- It is a mistake to forget that it connects the oesophagus to the small intestine.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the gross anatomy of the stomach. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The stomach is an expandable, J-shaped muscular bag.
- It lies in the upper left abdomen, below the diaphragm.
- Its inner curve is the lesser curvature and its outer curve is the greater curvature.
- It connects the oesophagus above to the small intestine below.
- The liver, spleen, and pancreas lie near it.
