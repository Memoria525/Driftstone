# The Gastric Folds (Rugae)

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Identify gastric folds (rugae) and discuss their functional significance.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The empty stomach's lining is thrown into folds called rugae, which flatten as the stomach fills. Understanding them explains how the stomach can expand so much. Today we identify the gastric folds and discuss their functional significance.

- By the end of this session you will be able to identify the gastric folds.
- You will be able to describe how they change as the stomach fills.
- You will be able to explain their functional significance.
- You will be able to connect the rugae to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Folds That Let the Stomach Expand to Hold a Meal

The organising idea is that the rugae are folds in the empty stomach's lining that flatten out as the stomach fills, letting the stomach expand greatly to hold a meal. Folds that unfold to expand. This idea of expandable folds frames the topic.

- The rugae are folds in the lining of the empty stomach.
- They flatten out as the stomach fills.
- This lets the stomach expand greatly.
- Their significance is to allow the stomach to hold a large meal.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Pleated Bag That Unfolds to Hold More

To keep the rugae concrete, picture a pleated bag whose folds open out as it fills, letting it hold far more than its empty size suggests. The stomach's rugae unfold as it fills, and the image keeps the expandable folds in view.

- The pleats of the bag correspond to the rugae of the empty stomach.
- The pleats opening out corresponds to the rugae flattening as the stomach fills.
- Holding more than its empty size corresponds to the stomach expanding to hold a meal.
- The analogy breaks down in that a bag is inert, whereas the stomach lining is living tissue that also secretes.

---

## Identifying the Gastric Folds

Begin with what the rugae are, since they must be identified. They are folds in the lining.

- The rugae are folds in the mucosa and submucosa of the stomach.
- They are prominent when the stomach is empty.
- They run mostly lengthwise along the stomach.
- They give the empty stomach's lining a ridged appearance.

---

## How the Folds Change as the Stomach Fills

Next how they change, since this is their key behaviour. They flatten as the stomach fills.

- When the stomach is empty, the rugae are prominent folds.
- As the stomach fills with a meal, the folds flatten out.
- This lets the lining stretch and the stomach expand.
- When the stomach empties again, the folds reform.

---

## The Functional Significance

Finally their significance, since this is the objective's focus. They allow expansion.

- The rugae let the stomach expand greatly to hold a meal.
- Without them, the lining could not stretch as much.
- They let the body eat a large meal at once.
- They also increase the surface area of the lining when the stomach is empty.

---

## Why the Rugae Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The rugae underlie the stomach's expandability relevant to your patients. Each point below connects them to practice.

- The rugae let the stomach hold a meal, relevant to eating and comfort.
- The expandability of the stomach is relevant to how meals are handled.
- Understanding the rugae clarifies how the stomach expands.
- The folds are a feature seen on examination of the stomach's lining.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about the stomach.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a pleated bag that unfolds to hold more.

- It is a mistake to think the rugae are permanent ridges, since they flatten as the stomach fills.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the rugae let the stomach expand.
- It is a mistake to confuse the rugae with the folds of the small intestine.
- It is a mistake to forget that the folds reform when the stomach empties.
