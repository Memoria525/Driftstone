## Identify and describe the different regions of the pharynx with respect to the passage of air and/or food

# The Regions of the Pharynx

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Identify and describe the different regions of the pharynx with respect to the passage of air and/or food.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The pharynx, or throat, is a shared passage for air and food, divided into three regions that differ in what passes through them. Understanding these regions frames how air and food are routed. Today we identify and describe the regions of the pharynx with respect to the passage of air and food.

- By the end of this session you will be able to name the three regions of the pharynx.
- You will be able to state what passes through each.
- You will be able to explain where air and food routes cross.
- You will be able to connect the pharynx to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Three-Region Throat Where the Air and Food Routes Cross

The organising idea is that the pharynx has three regions, an upper region for air only, a middle region shared by air and food, and a lower region for food only, so the air and food routes cross in the throat. A shared crossing throat. This idea of a crossing passage frames the topic.

- The pharynx is divided into three regions.
- The upper region carries only air.
- The middle region carries both air and food.
- The lower region carries only food, so the routes cross in the throat.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Junction Where Two Routes Share a Stretch of Road

To keep the regions concrete, picture two routes that run separately, share a stretch of road in the middle, then separate again. Air and food share the middle pharynx and then part, and the image keeps the crossing in view.

- The route running alone at the top corresponds to the upper pharynx for air only.
- The shared stretch corresponds to the middle pharynx for air and food.
- The routes parting corresponds to air and food separating below.
- The analogy breaks down in that roads are fixed, whereas the pharynx actively directs air and food during swallowing.

---

## The Upper Region

Begin with the upper region, which carries only air. It lies behind the nose.

- The upper region of the pharynx is the nasopharynx.
- It lies behind the nasal cavity.
- It carries only air, from the nose toward the lower airway.
- Food does not normally enter it.

---

## The Middle Region

Next the middle region, which carries both air and food. It lies behind the mouth.

- The middle region of the pharynx is the oropharynx.
- It lies behind the mouth.
- It carries both air and food.
- It is where the air and food routes overlap.

---

## The Lower Region

Finally the lower region, which carries only food. It leads to the oesophagus.

- The lower region of the pharynx is the laryngopharynx.
- It lies above the oesophagus and the larynx.
- It carries food toward the oesophagus.
- Here the food route separates from the air route, which goes to the larynx.

---

## Why the Pharynx Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The pharynx underlies swallowing and airway protection relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- Because air and food routes cross in the pharynx, swallowing must protect the airway.
- Problems with swallowing can allow food to enter the airway, relevant to the risk of choking.
- Understanding the regions clarifies how air and food are routed.
- Swallowing safety is central to some patients you treat.
- Understanding the pharynx grounds your reasoning about swallowing.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a junction where two routes share a stretch of road.

- It is a mistake to think the whole pharynx carries both air and food, since the upper region carries only air.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the air and food routes cross in the throat.
- It is a mistake to muddle the three regions.
- It is a mistake to forget that the lower region leads to the oesophagus.

---

## Consolidation and Self-Check

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Naming the regions and what passes through aloud is the strongest test.

- Name the three regions of the pharynx from memory.
- State what passes through each.
- Explain where the air and food routes cross.
- State which region leads to the oesophagus.
- Relate the pharynx to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

---

## Key Takeaways

We have described the regions of the pharynx. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The pharynx has three regions.
- The nasopharynx carries only air.
- The oropharynx carries both air and food.
- The laryngopharynx carries food toward the oesophagus.
- The air and food routes cross in the pharynx, so swallowing must protect the airway.
