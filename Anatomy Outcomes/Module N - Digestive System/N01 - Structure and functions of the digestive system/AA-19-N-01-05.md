## Define peristalsis

# Defining Peristalsis

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Define peristalsis.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

Food is moved along the digestive tract not by gravity but by a wave of muscular contraction called peristalsis. Understanding it explains how food travels even against gravity. Today we define peristalsis.

- By the end of this session you will be able to define peristalsis.
- You will be able to describe how the wave of contraction works.
- You will be able to explain where peristalsis occurs.
- You will be able to connect peristalsis to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Wave of Muscle Contraction That Pushes Food Along

The organising idea is that peristalsis is a wave of muscular contraction that squeezes behind the food and relaxes ahead of it, pushing the food along the tract. A pushing muscle wave. This idea of a propelling wave frames the definition.

- Peristalsis is a wave of muscular contraction in the wall of the tract.
- The muscle contracts behind the food and relaxes ahead of it.
- This pushes the food along the tract.
- It moves food even against gravity.

---

## Our Master Analogy: Squeezing Toothpaste Along a Tube

To keep peristalsis concrete, picture squeezing a tube of toothpaste from behind, so the contents are pushed forward toward the opening. Peristalsis squeezes behind the food to push it along, and the image keeps the propelling squeeze in view.

- The squeeze from behind corresponds to the muscle contracting behind the food.
- The contents pushed forward corresponds to the food being propelled along.
- The opening ahead corresponds to the relaxed tract ahead of the food.
- The analogy breaks down in that a tube is squeezed by hand, whereas peristalsis is produced by the tract's own muscle in a moving wave.

---

## Defining Peristalsis

Begin with the definition itself, stated precisely. It is a wave of contraction.

- Peristalsis is a series of wave-like muscular contractions.
- It occurs in the muscular wall of the digestive tract.
- The contractions move in one direction along the tract.
- They propel the food and other contents along.

---

## How the Wave Works

Next how the wave works, since this is the mechanism. Muscle contracts behind and relaxes ahead.

- The circular muscle of the tract wall contracts behind the food.
- This squeezes the food forward.
- The muscle ahead of the food relaxes to receive it.
- The wave moves along, pushing the food ahead of it.

---

## Where Peristalsis Occurs

Finally where peristalsis occurs, since it operates throughout the tract. It moves food from the oesophagus onward.

- Peristalsis occurs throughout the digestive tract.
- It moves food down the oesophagus to the stomach.
- It moves the contents along the intestines.
- Because it is muscular, it can move food even upward against gravity.

---

## Why Peristalsis Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

Peristalsis underlies the movement of food and normal digestion relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- Peristalsis moves food through the tract, essential to digestion and nutrition.
- Because it works against gravity, food moves even when lying down, relevant to positioning.
- Reduced peristalsis, as in some conditions, slows digestion and can cause discomfort.
- Understanding peristalsis clarifies how food travels through the tract.
- Understanding it grounds your reasoning about digestion.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors cloud the definition, and naming them keeps it precise. When unsure, return to squeezing toothpaste along a tube.

- It is a mistake to think food moves by gravity, since peristalsis pushes it along.
- It is a mistake to believe peristalsis is a single contraction, since it is a moving wave.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the muscle relaxes ahead of the food.
- It is a mistake to think peristalsis occurs only in the oesophagus, since it occurs throughout the tract.

## Key Takeaways

We have defined peristalsis. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- Peristalsis is a wave of muscular contraction in the wall of the digestive tract.
- The muscle contracts behind the food and relaxes ahead of it.
- This pushes the food along the tract.
- It occurs throughout the tract and moves food even against gravity.
- It is essential to the movement of food through the digestive system.
