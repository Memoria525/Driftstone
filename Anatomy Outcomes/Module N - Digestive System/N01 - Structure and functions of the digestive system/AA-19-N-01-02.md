## Describe the major functions of the digestive system

# The Major Functions of the Digestive System

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the major functions of the digestive system.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The digestive system breaks food down, absorbs the nutrients the body needs, and eliminates what is left. Understanding its functions frames how the body is fuelled and nourished. Today we describe the major functions of the digestive system.

- By the end of this session you will be able to list the major functions of the digestive system.
- You will be able to describe how food is broken down.
- You will be able to describe absorption and elimination.
- You will be able to connect these functions to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Take In, Break Down, Absorb, and Eliminate

The organising idea is that the digestive system takes in food, breaks it down into small molecules, absorbs those molecules into the blood, and eliminates the waste. Take in, break down, absorb, eliminate. This idea of a four-step process frames the functions.

- The digestive system takes in food.
- It breaks the food down into small molecules.
- It absorbs these molecules into the blood.
- It eliminates the waste that is left.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Processing Plant That Takes In, Refines, Extracts, and Disposes

To keep the functions concrete, picture a processing plant that takes in raw material, refines it into usable parts, extracts what is useful into storage, and disposes of the waste. The digestive system takes in, breaks down, absorbs, and eliminates this way, and the image keeps the four steps in view.

- Taking in raw material corresponds to ingesting food.
- Refining it corresponds to breaking food down.
- Extracting the useful parts corresponds to absorbing nutrients.
- Disposing of the waste corresponds to eliminating what is left.
- The analogy breaks down in that a plant is machinery, whereas the digestive system is living tissue that also defends against pathogens in food.

---

## Ingestion and Digestion

Begin with taking in and breaking down food. These are ingestion and digestion.

- Ingestion is taking food into the mouth.
- Digestion is the breakdown of food into small molecules.
- Digestion is both mechanical, physically breaking food up, and chemical, breaking it down with enzymes.
- The system also moves the food along by muscular contractions.

---

## Absorption

Next absorption, which takes the nutrients into the body. This mostly happens in the small intestine.

- Absorption is the passage of the small molecules from the tract into the blood.
- It occurs mainly in the small intestine.
- The absorbed nutrients are carried to the body's cells.
- Water is also absorbed, mainly in the large intestine.

---

## Elimination

Finally elimination, which removes the waste. This is the last function.

- Elimination is the removal of undigested waste from the body.
- The waste that cannot be absorbed is formed into faeces.
- The faeces are eliminated through the anus.
- This completes the passage of material through the system.

---

## Why These Functions Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The digestive functions underlie nutrition and health relevant to your patients. Each point below connects a function to practice.

- Absorption of nutrients provides the fuel and building blocks for recovery and adaptation.
- Nutrition is relevant to healing and to a patient's energy for rehabilitation.
- Digestive problems affect nutrition and can produce abdominal pain you may need to recognise.
- Understanding the functions clarifies how the body is fuelled.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about nutrition and recovery.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors narrow the functions, and naming them keeps them complete. When unsure, return to a processing plant.

- It is a mistake to see digestion as only chemical, since it is also mechanical.
- It is a mistake to overlook that absorption mainly occurs in the small intestine.
- It is a mistake to forget elimination as a function.
- It is a mistake to think the system only breaks food down, since it also absorbs and eliminates.

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Listing the functions aloud is the strongest test.

- List the major functions of the digestive system from memory.
- Describe how food is broken down.
- Describe absorption and elimination.
- Explain where absorption mainly occurs.
- Relate one function to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

---

## Key Takeaways

We have described the digestive functions. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The digestive system takes in food, breaks it down, absorbs nutrients, and eliminates waste.
- Digestion is both mechanical and chemical.
- Absorption mainly occurs in the small intestine, and water absorption in the large intestine.
- Elimination removes undigested waste as faeces.
- The functions provide the fuel and building blocks for recovery and adaptation.
