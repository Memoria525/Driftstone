# The Location of the Gallbladder

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the location of the gallbladder relative to other body structures.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The gallbladder sits tucked against the underside of the liver in the upper right abdomen, near the duodenum it delivers bile to. Understanding its location frames its relationships. Today we describe the location of the gallbladder relative to other structures.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe where the gallbladder lies.
- You will be able to describe its relationship to the liver.
- You will be able to describe its relationship to nearby organs.
- You will be able to connect its location to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Tucked Under the Liver in the Upper Right Abdomen

The organising idea is that the gallbladder lies tucked against the underside of the liver in the upper right abdomen, near the duodenum to which it delivers bile. Under the liver, upper right. This idea of a position under the liver frames the topic.

- The gallbladder lies on the underside of the liver.
- It is in the upper right part of the abdomen.
- It lies near the duodenum, the first part of the small intestine.
- It connects to the bile ducts that lead to the duodenum.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Pouch Attached Beneath a Large Unit Near an Outlet

To keep the location concrete, picture a pouch attached to the underside of a large unit, positioned near the outlet it feeds. The gallbladder is tucked under the liver near the duodenum, and the image keeps its position and connection in view.

- The pouch under the large unit corresponds to the gallbladder under the liver.
- The outlet it feeds corresponds to the duodenum it delivers bile to.
- The upper corner corresponds to the upper right abdomen.
- The analogy breaks down in that a pouch is fixed, whereas the gallbladder contracts to release its contents.

---

## The General Location

Begin with the general location, in the upper right abdomen. It sits under the liver.

- The gallbladder lies in the upper right part of the abdomen.
- It sits on the underside of the liver.
- It is partly hidden by the lower edge of the liver.
- Its position places it behind the lower right ribs.

---

## The Relationship to the Liver

Next its relationship to the liver, since it is attached to it. It lies in a hollow on the liver.

- The gallbladder sits in a hollow on the underside of the liver.
- It receives bile from the liver through the bile ducts.
- It is closely attached to the liver.
- Its position under the liver means the liver partly covers it.

---

## The Relationship to Nearby Organs

Finally its relationship to nearby organs, since these matter. It lies near the duodenum and colon.

- The duodenum lies near the gallbladder, and bile is delivered to it.
- The transverse colon lies below the gallbladder.
- The bile ducts run from the gallbladder toward the duodenum.
- The gallbladder's position lets it deliver bile to the duodenum.

---

## Why the Gallbladder's Location Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The gallbladder's location underlies its pain and examination relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- Gallbladder pain is felt in the upper right abdomen, relevant to interpreting a patient's pain.
- Gallbladder pain can refer to the right shoulder, relevant to distinguishing it from musculoskeletal pain.
- Its position behind the lower right ribs is relevant to its examination.
- Understanding its location clarifies its relationships to nearby organs.
- Understanding it grounds your reasoning about the upper abdomen.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a pouch attached beneath a large unit near an outlet.

- It is a mistake to place the gallbladder away from the liver, since it lies on its underside.
- It is a mistake to overlook that it lies near the duodenum.
- It is a mistake to forget that its pain can refer to the shoulder.
- It is a mistake to place the gallbladder in the lower abdomen.
