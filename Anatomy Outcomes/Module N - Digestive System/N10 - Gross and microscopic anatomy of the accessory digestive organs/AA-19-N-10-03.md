# The Location of the Liver

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the location of the liver relative to other body structures.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The liver is a large organ that fills much of the upper right abdomen, tucked under the diaphragm and protected by the ribs. Understanding its location frames its relationships to nearby organs. Today we describe the location of the liver relative to other structures.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe where the liver lies.
- You will be able to describe its protection by the ribs.
- You will be able to describe its relationship to nearby organs.
- You will be able to connect its location to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Large Organ in the Upper Right Abdomen Under the Diaphragm

The organising idea is that the liver is a large organ occupying the upper right abdomen, tucked under the diaphragm and protected by the lower ribs, near the stomach and gallbladder. Upper right, under the diaphragm. This idea of an upper right position frames the topic.

- The liver lies mainly in the upper right part of the abdomen.
- It is tucked under the diaphragm.
- It is protected by the lower ribs.
- It lies near the stomach, gallbladder, and other organs.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Large Unit Tucked Under a Ceiling Behind a Protective Grille

To keep the location concrete, picture a large unit tucked up under a ceiling behind a protective grille, filling the upper corner of a space. The liver is tucked under the diaphragm behind the ribs, filling the upper right abdomen, and the image keeps its position and protection in view.

- The large unit tucked under the ceiling corresponds to the liver under the diaphragm.
- The protective grille corresponds to the ribs protecting the liver.
- The upper corner corresponds to the upper right abdomen.
- The analogy breaks down in that a unit is fixed machinery, whereas the liver moves slightly with breathing.

---

## The General Location

Begin with the general location, in the upper right abdomen. It fills much of this space.

- The liver fills much of the upper right part of the abdomen.
- It extends across the midline into the upper left.
- It is the largest organ in the abdomen.
- It sits just below the diaphragm.

---

## The Protection by the Ribs

Next its protection, by the lower ribs. The ribs shield most of the liver.

- The liver is protected by the lower ribs on the right.
- Most of the liver lies behind the rib cage.
- The ribs shield it from injury.
- Its position behind the ribs means it moves with breathing as the diaphragm moves.

---

## The Relationship to Nearby Organs

Finally its relationship to nearby organs, since these matter. It lies near several organs.

- The diaphragm lies above the liver.
- The stomach lies to the left of the liver.
- The gallbladder lies on the underside of the liver.
- The hepatic portal vein brings blood to the liver from the gut.

---

## Why the Liver's Location Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The liver's location underlies its examination and injury relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The liver's position behind the lower right ribs is relevant to rib injury, which can damage it.
- It moves with breathing, relevant to how it is examined.
- Its position near the diaphragm links it to breathing movement.
- Understanding its location clarifies its relationships to nearby organs.
- Understanding it grounds your reasoning about the upper abdomen.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a large unit tucked under a ceiling behind a grille.

- It is a mistake to place the liver in the lower abdomen, since it lies in the upper right.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the ribs protect it.
- It is a mistake to forget that it moves with breathing.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the gallbladder lies on its underside.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the location of the liver. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The liver lies mainly in the upper right abdomen, extending across the midline.
- It is tucked under the diaphragm and protected by the lower ribs.
- It moves with breathing as the diaphragm moves.
- It lies near the stomach and above the gallbladder.
- Its position behind the ribs is relevant to rib injury and its examination.
