# The Structure and Functions of the Pancreas

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Identify and describe the structure and functions of the pancreas.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The pancreas is a dual organ, secreting digestive enzymes into the gut and hormones into the blood. Understanding its structure and functions frames its central role in both digestion and blood sugar control. Today we identify and describe the structure and functions of the pancreas.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe the structure of the pancreas.
- You will be able to describe its exocrine function.
- You will be able to describe its endocrine function.
- You will be able to connect the pancreas to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Dual Organ That Secretes Digestive Enzymes and Hormones

The organising idea is that the pancreas is both an exocrine and an endocrine organ, secreting digestive enzymes into the gut and hormones that control blood sugar into the blood. Enzymes to the gut, hormones to the blood. This idea of a dual organ frames the topic.

- The pancreas is both an exocrine and an endocrine organ.
- Its exocrine part secretes digestive enzymes into the small intestine.
- Its endocrine part secretes hormones into the blood.
- These hormones control blood sugar.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Factory With a Delivery Line and a Broadcast System

To keep the functions concrete, picture a factory with a delivery line that sends products along a road and a broadcast system that sends signals into a network. The pancreas sends enzymes along a duct and hormones into the blood, and the image keeps the dual output in view.

- The delivery line along a road corresponds to the exocrine enzymes sent along the duct.
- The broadcast into a network corresponds to the endocrine hormones sent into the blood.
- The two outputs corresponds to the dual role of the pancreas.
- The analogy breaks down in that a factory is machinery, whereas the pancreas is living tissue.

---

## The Structure of the Pancreas

Begin with the structure, since it is a soft elongated gland. It lies behind the stomach.

- The pancreas is a soft, elongated gland.
- It lies behind the stomach, across the upper abdomen.
- It has a head near the duodenum, a body, and a tail near the spleen.
- It contains both exocrine and endocrine tissue.

---

## The Exocrine Function

Next the exocrine function, which aids digestion. The pancreas secretes enzymes and fluid.

- The exocrine part of the pancreas secretes digestive enzymes.
- These enzymes break down proteins, fats, and starches.
- It also secretes an alkaline fluid that neutralises stomach acid.
- The enzymes and fluid flow through a duct into the small intestine.

---

## The Endocrine Function

Finally the endocrine function, which controls blood sugar. The pancreas secretes hormones.

- The endocrine part of the pancreas secretes hormones into the blood.
- It secretes insulin, which lowers blood sugar.
- It secretes glucagon, which raises blood sugar.
- These hormones balance the level of sugar in the blood.

---

## Why the Pancreas Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The pancreas underlies digestion and blood sugar control relevant to your patients. Each point below connects a function to practice.

- The pancreas's enzymes complete digestion, relevant to nutrition.
- Its hormones control blood sugar, and their failure causes diabetes in patients you treat.
- Exercise affects blood sugar, relevant to safety in patients with diabetes.
- Understanding the pancreas clarifies its dual role.
- Understanding it grounds your reasoning about digestion and blood sugar.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a factory with a delivery line and a broadcast system.

- It is a mistake to think the pancreas is only an endocrine organ, since it also secretes digestive enzymes.
- It is a mistake to overlook that its enzymes flow through a duct to the gut.
- It is a mistake to forget that its hormones control blood sugar.
- It is a mistake to swap insulin and glucagon.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the structure and functions of the pancreas. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The pancreas is a soft, elongated gland behind the stomach, with a head, body, and tail.
- Its exocrine part secretes digestive enzymes and an alkaline fluid into the small intestine.
- Its endocrine part secretes insulin and glucagon into the blood.
- Insulin lowers blood sugar and glucagon raises it.
- Failure of its hormones causes diabetes, relevant to exercise and safety.
