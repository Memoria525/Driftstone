## Define mesentery and explain its function

# Defining the Mesentery

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Define mesentery and explain its function.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The mobile abdominal organs are held in place and supplied by folds of peritoneum called mesenteries, which suspend them from the back wall. Understanding the mesentery explains how the gut is anchored yet free to move. Today we define the mesentery and explain its function.

- By the end of this session you will be able to define a mesentery.
- You will be able to explain how it suspends the organs.
- You will be able to explain its role in supplying the organs.
- You will be able to connect the mesentery to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Fold of Peritoneum That Suspends and Supplies the Gut

The organising idea is that a mesentery is a double fold of peritoneum that suspends parts of the gut from the back wall of the abdomen and carries their blood vessels and nerves. A suspending, supplying fold. This idea of a suspending fold frames the definition.

- A mesentery is a double fold of peritoneum.
- It suspends parts of the gut from the back wall of the abdomen.
- It carries the blood vessels and nerves to the organs.
- It anchors the organs while allowing them to move.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Sling That Holds and Supplies

To keep the mesentery concrete, picture a sling that suspends an object from a support, holding it in place while carrying its supply lines along the sling. The mesentery suspends the gut and carries its vessels this way, and the image keeps the holding and supplying in view.

- The sling suspending the object corresponds to the mesentery suspending the gut.
- The support corresponds to the back wall of the abdomen.
- The supply lines along the sling correspond to the vessels and nerves in the mesentery.
- The analogy breaks down in that a sling is fixed, whereas the mesentery is living tissue that allows the gut to move.

---

## Defining the Mesentery

Begin with the definition itself, stated precisely. It is a fold of peritoneum.

- A mesentery is a double layer of peritoneum.
- It connects parts of the gut to the back wall of the abdomen.
- It is continuous with the peritoneum covering the organs and lining the wall.
- It forms a suspending fold that holds the organ in place.

---

## The Suspending Function

Next the suspending function, since this holds the gut in place. It anchors yet allows movement.

- The mesentery suspends the mobile parts of the gut from the back wall.
- It holds the organs in their general position.
- It allows the organs to move as they churn and shift.
- Without it, the organs would not be held in place.

---

## The Supplying Function

Finally the supplying function, since the mesentery carries the vessels. It delivers the blood supply.

- The mesentery carries the blood vessels to and from the organs.
- It carries the nerves and lymphatic vessels as well.
- These run within the fold of the mesentery to reach the organs.
- The mesentery thereby supplies the organs it suspends.

---

## Why the Mesentery Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The mesentery underlies the support and supply of the gut relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The mesentery holds the organs in place, relevant to their position and movement.
- It carries the blood supply, essential to the gut's function.
- Twisting of the gut on its mesentery can cut off the blood supply, an abdominal emergency.
- Understanding the mesentery clarifies how the gut is anchored and supplied.
- Understanding it grounds your reasoning about the abdomen.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors cloud the definition, and naming them keeps it precise. When unsure, return to a sling that holds and supplies.

- It is a mistake to think the mesentery only holds the gut, since it also carries its vessels.
- It is a mistake to believe the mesentery is a single layer, since it is a double fold.
- It is a mistake to overlook that it allows the gut to move.
- It is a mistake to forget that it connects the gut to the back wall.

We have defined the mesentery. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- A mesentery is a double fold of peritoneum.
- It suspends parts of the gut from the back wall of the abdomen.
- It carries the blood vessels, nerves, and lymphatic vessels to the organs.
