# The Locations of the Mesenteries

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Compare and contrast the locations of the mesenteries (mesentery proper, mesocolon, lesser omentum, greater omentum).

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The peritoneum forms several named mesenteries, each suspending a particular part of the gut. Learning their locations lets you place each fold and the organ it holds. Today we compare and contrast the locations of the named mesenteries.

- By the end of this session you will be able to name the major mesenteries.
- You will be able to state which organ each suspends.
- You will be able to describe the location of each.
- You will be able to connect the mesenteries to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Named Folds Each Suspending a Particular Part of the Gut

The organising idea is that the named mesenteries each suspend a particular part of the gut, with the mesentery proper holding the small intestine, the mesocolon holding the colon, and the omenta connecting the stomach. Each fold for a particular organ. This idea of named folds for named organs frames the topic.

- The peritoneum forms several named mesenteries.
- The mesentery proper suspends the small intestine.
- The mesocolon suspends the colon.
- The lesser and greater omenta connect the stomach to other structures.

---

## Our Master Analogy: Labelled Slings Each Holding a Different Item

To keep the mesenteries concrete, picture several labelled slings, each holding a different item in place. The named mesenteries are these slings, each suspending a particular part of the gut, and the image keeps the named folds in view.

- The sling for one item corresponds to the mesentery proper for the small intestine.
- The sling for another item corresponds to the mesocolon for the colon.
- The connecting straps corresponds to the omenta connecting the stomach.
- The analogy breaks down in that slings are separate objects, whereas the mesenteries are continuous folds of the one peritoneum.

---

## The Mesentery Proper and Mesocolon

Begin with the two folds that suspend the intestines. These are the mesentery proper and the mesocolon.

- The mesentery proper suspends the small intestine from the back wall.
- It is a fan-shaped fold that lets the small intestine hang in loops.
- The mesocolon suspends parts of the colon from the back wall.
- Different parts of the mesocolon suspend different parts of the colon.

---

## The Lesser Omentum

Next the lesser omentum, which connects the stomach to the liver. It lies above the stomach.

- The lesser omentum connects the upper part of the stomach to the liver.
- It runs between the liver and the lesser curve of the stomach.
- It carries vessels between these organs.
- It lies above and behind the stomach.

---

## The Greater Omentum

Finally the greater omentum, which hangs down over the intestines. It is a large apron of peritoneum.

- The greater omentum hangs down from the lower part of the stomach.
- It drapes like an apron over the intestines.
- It contains fat and immune tissue.
- It helps wall off and contain infection in the abdomen.

---

## Why the Mesenteries Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The mesenteries underlie the support of the gut relevant to your patients. Each point below connects them to practice.

- The mesenteries hold the organs in place and carry their vessels, essential to gut function.
- The greater omentum helps contain infection, relevant to abdominal conditions.
- Understanding the mesenteries clarifies how the gut is suspended.
- Twisting of the gut on its mesentery is an abdominal emergency.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about the abdomen.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to labelled slings each holding a different item.

- It is a mistake to mismatch the mesenteries with the organs they suspend.
- It is a mistake to confuse the lesser and greater omenta, since one connects to the liver and the other hangs over the intestines.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the greater omentum helps contain infection.
- It is a mistake to forget that all are folds of the one peritoneum.

## Consolidation and Self-Check

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Naming each mesentery and its organ aloud is the strongest test.

- Name the major mesenteries from memory.
- State which organ each suspends.
- Describe the location of each.
- Explain the role of the greater omentum.
- Relate one mesentery to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

---

## Key Takeaways

We have compared the locations of the mesenteries. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The mesentery proper suspends the small intestine from the back wall.
- The mesocolon suspends the colon.
- The lesser omentum connects the stomach to the liver.
- The greater omentum hangs down over the intestines and helps contain infection.
- All are named folds of the one peritoneum, each suspending a particular part of the gut.
