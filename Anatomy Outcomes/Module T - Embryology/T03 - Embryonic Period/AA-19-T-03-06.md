## Compare and contrast the adult fates of the neural tube and the neural crest cells

# The Fates of the Neural Tube and Neural Crest Cells

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Compare and contrast the adult fates of the neural tube and the neural crest cells.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The neural tube and the neural crest cells, formed together during neurulation, go on to form different parts of the nervous system and beyond. Understanding their different fates frames how the nervous system is built. Today we compare and contrast the adult fates of the neural tube and the neural crest cells.

- By the end of this session you will be able to state the fate of the neural tube.
- You will be able to state the fate of the neural crest cells.
- You will be able to contrast the two.
- You will be able to connect the comparison to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: The Tube Becomes the Central Nervous System, the Crest Becomes Peripheral and Other Structures

The organising idea is that the neural tube becomes the central nervous system, the brain and spinal cord, while the neural crest cells become parts of the peripheral nervous system and other structures. Tube to centre, crest to periphery and beyond. This idea of different fates frames the comparison.

- The neural tube becomes the central nervous system.
- The neural crest cells become parts of the peripheral nervous system.
- The neural crest cells also form some other structures.
- The two therefore have different fates.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Core Cable and a Set of Migrating Workers

To keep the fates concrete, picture a core cable that becomes the central wiring, and a set of workers who set off to build connections and structures elsewhere. The neural tube becomes the central nervous system and the neural crest cells migrate to form peripheral and other structures, and the image keeps the two fates in view.

- The core cable corresponds to the neural tube becoming the central nervous system.
- The migrating workers corresponds to the neural crest cells.
- Building structures elsewhere corresponds to the neural crest forming peripheral and other structures.
- The analogy breaks down in that cables and workers are made by people, whereas these structures develop through the embryo's programme.

---

## The Fate of the Neural Tube

Begin with the neural tube, which becomes the central nervous system. It forms the brain and cord.

- The neural tube becomes the central nervous system.
- Its front part becomes the brain.
- Its rest becomes the spinal cord.
- The neural tube is the origin of the central nervous system.

---

## The Fate of the Neural Crest Cells

Next the neural crest cells, which migrate and form various structures. They form peripheral and other structures.

- The neural crest cells migrate away from the neural tube.
- They form parts of the peripheral nervous system, including some nerve cells and their supporting cells.
- They form some cells of the adrenal gland.
- They also form structures such as some bones of the face and the pigment cells of the skin.

---

## Comparing the Two

Holding the two together lets us state the comparison cleanly, which the objective asks. They differ in their fates.

- The neural tube becomes the central nervous system, while the neural crest cells become peripheral and other structures.
- The neural tube stays in place, while the neural crest cells migrate.
- The neural tube forms one system, while the neural crest cells form varied structures.
- The two together build much of the nervous system and more.

---

## Why This Comparison Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The comparison underlies the formation of the nervous system relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The neural tube becomes the central nervous system central to neurological practice.
- The neural crest forms parts of the peripheral nervous system relevant to peripheral nerve function.
- Understanding the two fates clarifies how the nervous system is built.
- Developmental conditions affect the nervous system in children you may treat.
- Understanding the comparison grounds your reasoning about development.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors blur the two, and naming them keeps them distinct. When unsure, return to a core cable and a set of migrating workers.

- It is a mistake to think the neural crest cells form the central nervous system, since the neural tube does.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the neural crest cells migrate.
- It is a mistake to forget that the neural crest forms structures beyond the nervous system.
- It is a mistake to think the two have the same fate.

## Consolidation and Self-Check

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Contrasting the two aloud is the strongest test.

- State the fate of the neural tube from memory.
- State the fate of the neural crest cells.
- Contrast the two.
- Explain what the neural crest cells form beyond the nervous system.
- Relate the comparison to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

## Key Takeaways

We have compared the fates of the neural tube and neural crest cells. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The neural tube becomes the central nervous system, the brain and spinal cord.
- The neural crest cells migrate to form parts of the peripheral nervous system.
- The neural crest cells also form some cells of the adrenal gland, some facial bones, and the pigment cells of the skin.
- The neural tube stays in place, while the neural crest cells migrate.
- The two together build much of the nervous system and more.
