## Describe the major events of the fetal period

# The Major Events of the Fetal Period

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the major events of the fetal period.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The fetal period is the long final stage of development before birth, when the organs formed earlier grow and mature and the body prepares for life outside the womb. Understanding it frames the end of prenatal development. Today we describe the major events of the fetal period.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe the major events of the fetal period.
- You will be able to explain how it differs from the embryonic period.
- You will be able to describe how the body prepares for birth.
- You will be able to connect the fetal period to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Growth and Maturation of the Already-Formed Organs

The organising idea is that the fetal period is when the organs that formed earlier grow and mature and the body grows greatly, preparing the fetus for life outside the womb. Grow and mature, not form. This idea of growth and maturation frames the topic.

- The fetal period follows the embryonic period.
- The organs that formed earlier grow and mature.
- The body grows greatly in size.
- The fetus prepares for life outside the womb.

---

## Our Master Analogy: Fitting Out and Finishing a Built Structure

To keep the fetal period concrete, picture a structure whose frame and rooms are already built now being fitted out, finished, and made ready for use. The fetal period grows and matures the already-formed organs and prepares the body for birth, and the image keeps the fitting out and finishing in view.

- The already-built frame and rooms correspond to the organs formed in the embryonic period.
- Fitting out and finishing corresponds to the organs growing and maturing.
- Making it ready for use corresponds to preparing the fetus for birth.
- The analogy breaks down in that a structure is finished by workers, whereas the fetus develops through its own programme.

---

## Growth and Maturation

Begin with growth and maturation, the main events. The organs mature and the body grows.

- The organs that formed in the embryonic period continue to grow and mature.
- They develop the ability to function.
- The body grows greatly in size and weight.
- The proportions of the body change as it grows.

---

## The Development of Function

Next the development of function, as the organs become able to work. The systems mature.

- The organ systems mature toward being able to function on their own.
- The lungs develop toward being able to breathe air.
- The nervous system matures, and the fetus begins to move.
- The fetus develops the ability to survive outside the womb as it matures.

---

## Preparing for Birth

Finally preparing for birth, as the fetus readies for life outside. It gains the ability to survive.

- As the fetal period progresses, the fetus becomes more able to survive outside the womb.
- It gains fat and grows toward its birth size.
- Its lungs and other systems mature toward readiness.
- By the end, the fetus is ready to be born.

---

## Why the Fetal Period Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The fetal period underlies the newborn's readiness relevant to paediatric practice. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The fetal period matures the organs toward function, relevant to the newborn's readiness at birth.
- A baby born early may have organs not yet fully matured, relevant to premature care.
- Understanding the fetal period grounds the study of development.
- The newborn's development is relevant to paediatric physiotherapy.
- Understanding it grounds your reasoning about development.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to fitting out and finishing a built structure.

- It is a mistake to think the organs form in the fetal period, since they form in the embryonic period.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the fetal period is mainly growth and maturation.
- It is a mistake to forget that the fetus prepares for life outside the womb.
- It is a mistake to think the fetal period is short, since it is the longest period.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the major events of the fetal period. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The fetal period follows the embryonic period and is the longest period.
- The organs that formed earlier grow and mature.
- The body grows greatly in size and its systems mature toward being able to function.
- The fetus prepares for life outside the womb, gaining the ability to survive.
- A baby born early may have organs not yet fully matured.
