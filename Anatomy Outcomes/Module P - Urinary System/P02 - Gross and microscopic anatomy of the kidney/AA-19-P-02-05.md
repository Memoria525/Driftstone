# The Structure of a Typical Nephron

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Identify and describe the structure of a typical nephron, including the renal corpuscle (glomerular [Bowman's] capsule and glomerulus), renal tubule (proximal convoluted tubule, nephron loop [loop of Henle], distal convoluted tubule).

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The nephron is the kidney's microscopic urine-making unit, made of a filter and a long processing tubule. Understanding its parts frames how urine is made. Today we identify and describe the structure of a typical nephron.

- By the end of this session you will be able to name the parts of the nephron.
- You will be able to describe the renal corpuscle.
- You will be able to describe the renal tubule.
- You will be able to connect the nephron to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Filter Followed by a Long Processing Tubule

The organising idea is that a nephron is a filter, the renal corpuscle, followed by a long processing tubule that adjusts the filtered fluid into urine. A filter and a processing line. This idea of a filter plus a tubule frames the topic.

- A nephron is the microscopic urine-making unit of the kidney.
- It begins with the renal corpuscle, which filters the blood.
- It continues as the renal tubule, which processes the filtered fluid.
- The tubule has several named parts in order.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Filter Feeding a Winding Processing Line

To keep the nephron concrete, picture a filter that produces a stream of fluid feeding a long winding processing line that adjusts the fluid before it leaves. The renal corpuscle is the filter and the tubule the processing line, and the image keeps the filter-plus-line idea in view.

- The filter corresponds to the renal corpuscle.
- The winding processing line corresponds to the renal tubule.
- Adjusting the fluid corresponds to the tubule processing the filtrate.
- The analogy breaks down in that a processing line is machinery, whereas the tubule is living tissue that actively transports substances.

---

## The Renal Corpuscle

Begin with the renal corpuscle, the filter. It has two parts.

- The renal corpuscle is the filtering part of the nephron.
- It contains the glomerulus, a tuft of capillaries.
- It contains the glomerular capsule, a cup that surrounds the glomerulus.
- Fluid filtered from the glomerulus passes into the capsule and on into the tubule.

---

## The First Parts of the Tubule

Next the first parts of the tubule, which process the fluid. These are the proximal tubule and the loop.

- The proximal convoluted tubule is the coiled first part of the tubule.
- It reabsorbs much of the useful substances and water.
- The nephron loop dips down into the medulla and back.
- It helps concentrate the urine by adjusting water and salts.

---

## The Last Part of the Tubule

Finally the last part of the tubule, which fine-tunes the fluid. This is the distal tubule.

- The distal convoluted tubule is the coiled last part of the tubule.
- It fine-tunes the content of the fluid.
- It adjusts the salts and helps regulate the fluid's acidity.
- It empties the processed fluid into a collecting duct.

---

## Why the Nephron Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The nephron underlies urine formation and fluid balance relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The nephron makes urine, regulating the body's fluid, salt, and waste balance relevant to exercise and hydration.
- Damage to the nephrons impairs this regulation, relevant to kidney disease.
- Understanding the nephron clarifies how urine is made.
- Kidney function underlies fluid balance relevant to your patients.
- Understanding the nephron grounds your reasoning about the kidney.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a filter feeding a winding processing line.

- It is a mistake to muddle the order of the tubule parts.
- It is a mistake to confuse the glomerulus with the capsule, since one is the tuft and the other the cup.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the loop dips into the medulla.
- It is a mistake to forget that the tubule processes the filtered fluid into urine.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the structure of a nephron. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The nephron is the kidney's microscopic urine-making unit.
- The renal corpuscle contains the glomerulus, a tuft of capillaries, and the glomerular capsule that surrounds it.
- The proximal convoluted tubule reabsorbs much of the useful substances and water.
- The nephron loop helps concentrate the urine, and the distal convoluted tubule fine-tunes it.
- The tubule empties the processed fluid into a collecting duct.
