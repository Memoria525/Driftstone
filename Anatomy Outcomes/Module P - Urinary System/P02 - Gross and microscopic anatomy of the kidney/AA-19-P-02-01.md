# The External Structure of the Kidney

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Identify and describe the external structure of the kidney, including its coverings, the renal hilum and renal sinus.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The kidney is a bean-shaped organ wrapped in protective coverings, with a notch on its inner border where its vessels and ureter enter and leave. Understanding its external structure frames how it is protected and connected. Today we identify and describe the external structure of the kidney, its coverings, hilum, and sinus.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe the shape of the kidney.
- You will be able to describe its coverings.
- You will be able to describe the renal hilum and sinus.
- You will be able to connect its external structure to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Bean-Shaped Organ Wrapped in Coverings With a Doorway on Its Inner Border

The organising idea is that the kidney is a bean-shaped organ wrapped in protective layers, with a notch, the renal hilum, on its inner border where its vessels and ureter enter and leave, opening into a space called the renal sinus. A wrapped bean with a doorway. This idea of a wrapped organ with a doorway frames the topic.

- The kidney is a bean-shaped organ.
- It is wrapped in protective coverings.
- The renal hilum is a notch on its inner border.
- Vessels and the ureter enter and leave at the hilum, which opens into the renal sinus.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Bean Wrapped in Padding With a Service Notch

To keep the structure concrete, picture a bean wrapped in layers of padding, with a notch on its inner curve where all its supply lines enter, opening into a hollow inside. The kidney's coverings are the padding, its hilum the notch, and its sinus the hollow, and the image keeps the wrapped bean with a doorway in view.

- The bean corresponds to the kidney.
- The padding corresponds to the coverings.
- The service notch corresponds to the renal hilum.
- The hollow inside corresponds to the renal sinus, though the analogy breaks down in that padding is inert while the coverings are living tissue and fat.

---

## The Shape and Coverings

Begin with the shape and coverings, since they define and protect the kidney. It is a wrapped bean.

- The kidney is bean-shaped, with a smooth outer surface and an indented inner border.
- It is wrapped in a fibrous capsule that covers its surface.
- Around this is a layer of fat that cushions it.
- An outer layer anchors the kidney to the back wall of the abdomen.

---

## The Renal Hilum

Next the renal hilum, the doorway. Vessels and the ureter enter and leave here.

- The renal hilum is a notch on the inner border of the kidney.
- The renal artery enters the kidney here.
- The renal vein and the ureter leave the kidney here.
- The hilum is where the kidney's connections enter and leave.

---

## The Renal Sinus

Finally the renal sinus, the space inside the hilum. It holds the collecting structures.

- The renal sinus is a hollow space inside the kidney, opening at the hilum.
- It contains the collecting structures that gather the urine.
- It contains the vessels and the upper part of the ureter.
- It is filled with fat that cushions these structures.

---

## Why the Kidney's External Structure Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The external structure underlies the kidney's protection and connections relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The coverings and fat protect the kidney, relevant to its position against the back wall.
- The hilum is where the kidney's blood supply and ureter connect, relevant to its function.
- The kidney's position against the back is relevant to loin pain from kidney problems.
- Understanding its external structure clarifies how it is protected and connected.
- Understanding it grounds your reasoning about the kidney.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a bean wrapped in padding with a service notch.

- It is a mistake to overlook the protective coverings and fat around the kidney.
- It is a mistake to place the hilum on the outer border, since it is on the inner border.
- It is a mistake to confuse the hilum with the sinus, since one is the notch and the other the space inside.
- It is a mistake to forget that the ureter leaves at the hilum.

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Describing the external structure aloud is the strongest test.

- Describe the shape of the kidney from memory.
- Describe its coverings.
- Describe the renal hilum and sinus.
- State what enters and leaves at the hilum.
- Relate the external structure to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the external structure of the kidney. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The kidney is a bean-shaped organ with an indented inner border.
- It is wrapped in a fibrous capsule, a layer of fat, and an anchoring outer layer.
- The renal hilum is the notch where the renal artery enters and the renal vein and ureter leave.
- The renal sinus is the hollow space inside the hilum holding the collecting structures.
- The coverings protect the kidney against the back wall of the abdomen.
