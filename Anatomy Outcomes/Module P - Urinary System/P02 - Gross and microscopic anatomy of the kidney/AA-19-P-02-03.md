# Distinguishing the Renal Cortex and Medulla

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Distinguish histologically between the renal cortex and the renal medulla.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

Under the microscope, the cortex and medulla of the kidney look different because they contain different parts of the urine-making units. Understanding the difference explains why the two regions have different appearances and roles. Today we distinguish histologically between the renal cortex and the renal medulla.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe what the cortex contains.
- You will be able to describe what the medulla contains.
- You will be able to explain their different appearances.
- You will be able to connect the distinction to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: The Cortex Holds the Filtering Units and the Medulla Holds the Straight Tubules

The organising idea is that the cortex contains the filtering units and the coiled tubules, giving it a granular appearance, while the medulla contains the straight tubules and ducts, giving it a striped appearance. Granular cortex, striped medulla. This idea of different contents frames the distinction.

- The renal cortex contains the filtering units and coiled tubules.
- This gives the cortex a granular appearance.
- The renal medulla contains straight tubules and collecting ducts.
- This gives the medulla a striped appearance.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Region of Tangled Coils and a Region of Straight Pipes

To keep the distinction concrete, picture one region full of tangled coils giving a granular look, and another region full of straight parallel pipes giving a striped look. The cortex is the tangled coils and the medulla the straight pipes, and the image keeps the granular and striped appearances in view.

- The tangled coils corresponds to the filtering units and coiled tubules of the cortex.
- The straight parallel pipes corresponds to the straight tubules and ducts of the medulla.
- The granular look corresponds to the cortex's appearance.
- The analogy breaks down in that coils and pipes are inert, whereas the tubules are living structures making urine.

---

## The Histology of the Cortex

Begin with the cortex, which contains the filtering units. Its structures give it a granular look.

- The cortex contains the renal corpuscles, the round filtering units.
- It contains the coiled parts of the tubules that lead from them.
- These round and coiled structures give the cortex a granular appearance.
- This is where filtration and much of the processing of the fluid occur.

---

## The Histology of the Medulla

Next the medulla, which contains the straight tubules. Its structures give it a striped look.

- The medulla contains the straight parts of the tubules.
- It contains the collecting ducts that carry urine toward the centre.
- These straight, parallel structures give the medulla a striped appearance.
- This is where the concentration of the urine occurs.

---

## Explaining the Different Appearances

Finally the different appearances, since this is the objective's focus. Their contents explain the look.

- The cortex looks granular because of its round corpuscles and coiled tubules.
- The medulla looks striped because of its straight, parallel tubules and ducts.
- The difference in appearance reflects the difference in contents.
- Distinguishing them by appearance lets you tell the two regions apart under the microscope.

---

## Why This Distinction Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The distinction underlies where the kidney's functions occur relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The cortex is where filtration occurs, and the medulla where urine is concentrated, relevant to kidney function.
- The distinction helps you understand where the kidney's processes happen.
- Understanding the two regions clarifies the kidney's structure.
- Kidney function underlies fluid and waste balance relevant to your patients.
- Understanding the distinction grounds your reasoning about the kidney.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors blur the two, and naming them keeps them distinct. When unsure, return to tangled coils versus straight pipes.

- It is a mistake to place the filtering units in the medulla, since they are in the cortex.
- It is a mistake to expect a granular look in the medulla, since it is striped.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the collecting ducts run through the medulla.
- It is a mistake to think the two regions look the same.
