## Compare and contrast cortical nephrons and juxtamedullary nephrons

# Cortical and Juxtamedullary Nephrons Compared

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Compare and contrast cortical nephrons and juxtamedullary nephrons.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The kidney has two kinds of nephron, most lying mainly in the cortex and a few dipping deep into the medulla. Understanding their difference explains how the kidney can concentrate urine. Today we compare and contrast cortical and juxtamedullary nephrons.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe cortical nephrons.
- You will be able to describe juxtamedullary nephrons.
- You will be able to contrast the two.
- You will be able to connect the comparison to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Most Nephrons Stay in the Cortex, While a Few Dip Deep to Concentrate Urine

The organising idea is that most nephrons are cortical, lying mainly in the cortex with short loops, while a few are juxtamedullary, with long loops dipping deep into the medulla to concentrate the urine. Shallow many versus deep few. This idea of shallow versus deep nephrons frames the comparison.

- The kidney has two kinds of nephron.
- Cortical nephrons lie mainly in the cortex and have short loops.
- Juxtamedullary nephrons dip deep into the medulla with long loops.
- The long loops let the kidney concentrate the urine.

---

## Our Master Analogy: Shallow Wells and a Few Deep Wells

To keep the two concrete, picture a field of mostly shallow wells and a few deep wells that reach far down. Cortical nephrons are the shallow wells and juxtamedullary nephrons the deep wells, and the image keeps the shallow versus deep idea in view.

- The shallow wells correspond to the cortical nephrons.
- The deep wells correspond to the juxtamedullary nephrons.
- Reaching far down corresponds to the long loops dipping into the medulla.
- The analogy breaks down in that wells are static, whereas the nephrons actively process fluid.

---

## The Cortical Nephrons

Begin with the cortical nephrons, the majority. They lie mainly in the cortex.

- Cortical nephrons make up most of the nephrons.
- They lie mainly in the cortex.
- Their loops are short and dip only a little into the medulla.
- They carry out most of the routine processing of the fluid.

---

## The Juxtamedullary Nephrons

Next the juxtamedullary nephrons, the minority. They dip deep into the medulla.

- Juxtamedullary nephrons are fewer in number.
- Their corpuscles lie near the medulla.
- Their loops are long and dip deep into the medulla.
- Their long loops let the kidney produce concentrated urine.

---

## Comparing the Two

Holding the two together lets us state the comparison cleanly, which the objective asks. They differ in depth and role.

- Cortical nephrons are more numerous, while juxtamedullary nephrons are fewer.
- Cortical nephrons have short loops, while juxtamedullary nephrons have long loops.
- Cortical nephrons carry out routine processing, while juxtamedullary nephrons concentrate the urine.
- The two kinds together let the kidney both process and concentrate the fluid.

---

## Why This Comparison Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The comparison underlies the kidney's ability to concentrate urine relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The juxtamedullary nephrons let the kidney concentrate urine, relevant to conserving water during exercise.
- The concentration of urine helps the body retain water when needed.
- Understanding the two kinds clarifies how the kidney regulates water.
- Water balance is relevant to exercise and hydration in your patients.
- Understanding the comparison grounds your reasoning about the kidney.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors blur the two, and naming them keeps them distinct. When unsure, return to shallow wells and a few deep wells.

- It is a mistake to think most nephrons are juxtamedullary, since most are cortical.
- It is a mistake to give the cortical nephrons long loops, since their loops are short.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the juxtamedullary nephrons concentrate the urine.
- It is a mistake to think the two kinds are the same.
