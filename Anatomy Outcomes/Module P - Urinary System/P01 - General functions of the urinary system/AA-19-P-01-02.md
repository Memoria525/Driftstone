## Define the terms filtration, reabsorption, and secretion

# Filtration, Reabsorption, and Secretion

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Define the terms filtration, reabsorption, and secretion.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The kidney makes urine through three processes: filtering the blood, taking back what the body needs, and adding extra waste. Defining these three processes frames how urine is formed. Today we define the terms filtration, reabsorption, and secretion.

- By the end of this session you will be able to define filtration.
- You will be able to define reabsorption.
- You will be able to define secretion.
- You will be able to explain how the three form urine.

---

## The Big Idea: Filter Everything, Take Back What Is Needed, Add Extra Waste

The organising idea is that the kidney forms urine by first filtering a large volume of fluid from the blood, then reabsorbing the useful substances back into the blood, and finally secreting extra waste into the fluid. Filter, take back, add. This idea of a three-step process frames the definitions.

- Filtration removes a large volume of fluid from the blood.
- Reabsorption takes the useful substances back into the blood.
- Secretion adds extra waste into the fluid.
- Together these form the urine.

---

## Our Master Analogy: Emptying a Drawer, Returning What Is Useful, and Adding Extra Rubbish

To keep the three processes concrete, picture emptying a whole drawer onto a table, returning the useful items to the drawer, and adding some extra rubbish to the pile to be thrown out. Filtration empties the blood's fluid, reabsorption returns the useful substances, and secretion adds extra waste, and the image keeps the three steps in view.

- Emptying the drawer corresponds to filtration removing fluid from the blood.
- Returning the useful items corresponds to reabsorption taking substances back.
- Adding extra rubbish corresponds to secretion adding waste.
- The analogy breaks down in that emptying a drawer is done by hand, whereas the kidney's processes are carried out by its tubules and vessels.

---

## Defining Filtration

Begin with filtration, the first process. It removes fluid from the blood.

- Filtration is the removal of a large volume of fluid from the blood.
- It occurs at the start of the nephron, where blood is filtered.
- Water and small substances pass out of the blood into the tubule.
- Large substances, such as blood cells and proteins, stay in the blood.

---

## Defining Reabsorption

Next reabsorption, the second process. It takes back what the body needs.

- Reabsorption is the return of useful substances from the tubule back into the blood.
- Most of the filtered water is reabsorbed.
- Useful substances such as nutrients and needed salts are reabsorbed.
- This keeps the body from losing what it needs.

---

## Defining Secretion

Finally secretion, the third process. It adds extra waste to the fluid.

- Secretion is the addition of extra substances from the blood into the tubule.
- It adds extra waste and substances the body needs to remove.
- It helps fine-tune the content of the urine.
- It removes substances that were not filtered out.

---

## Why These Processes Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The three processes underlie urine formation and fluid balance relevant to your patients. Each point below connects a process to practice.

- The processes together regulate the body's fluid, salt, and waste balance, relevant to exercise and hydration.
- Reabsorption of water keeps the body hydrated, relevant to exercise.
- Kidney problems that impair these processes affect fluid balance in your patients.
- Understanding the processes clarifies how urine is formed.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about the kidney.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors blur the three, and naming them keeps them distinct. When unsure, return to emptying a drawer, returning what is useful, and adding rubbish.

- It is a mistake to confuse reabsorption and secretion, since one takes substances back and the other adds them.
- It is a mistake to think filtration removes only waste, since it removes a large volume of fluid and small substances.
- It is a mistake to overlook that most filtered water is reabsorbed.
- It is a mistake to think urine is formed by filtration alone.

## Key Takeaways

We have defined the three processes of urine formation. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- Filtration removes a large volume of fluid and small substances from the blood.
- Reabsorption takes the useful substances, including most of the water, back into the blood.
- Secretion adds extra waste from the blood into the tubule.
- Together these form the urine.
- The processes regulate the body's fluid, salt, and waste balance.
