## Compare and contrast the functions of the kidney with the functions of the urinary tract (ureters, urinary bladder and urethra)

# The Kidney and the Urinary Tract Compared

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Compare and contrast the functions of the kidney with the functions of the urinary tract (ureters, urinary bladder and urethra).

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The urinary system has two kinds of part: the kidneys that make urine by filtering the blood, and the urinary tract that carries, stores, and expels it. Distinguishing them frames the whole of urinary anatomy. Today we compare and contrast the functions of the kidney with the functions of the urinary tract.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe the functions of the kidney.
- You will be able to describe the functions of the urinary tract.
- You will be able to explain the key difference between them.
- You will be able to connect the distinction to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: The Kidney Makes Urine, and the Tract Carries, Stores, and Expels It

The organising idea is that the kidneys make urine by filtering the blood and adjusting its content, while the urinary tract carries, stores, and expels the finished urine. Make versus transport and store. This idea of making versus handling frames the comparison.

- The kidneys make urine by filtering the blood.
- The urinary tract carries, stores, and expels the finished urine.
- The kidneys adjust the body's fluid, salt, and waste levels.
- The tract does not change the urine, only handles it.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Treatment Plant and Its Delivery and Storage Network

To keep the distinction concrete, picture a water treatment plant that produces treated water, and a network of pipes and a storage tank that carry, hold, and deliver it without changing it. The kidney is the treatment plant and the urinary tract the delivery and storage network, and the image keeps making versus handling in view.

- The treatment plant producing water corresponds to the kidney making urine.
- The pipes and storage tank corresponds to the urinary tract carrying and storing urine.
- Delivering without changing corresponds to the tract handling but not altering the urine.
- The analogy breaks down in that a plant is machinery, whereas the kidney is living tissue that finely adjusts the urine's content.

---

## The Functions of the Kidney

Begin with the kidney, which makes urine. It filters and adjusts the blood.

- The kidneys filter the blood to remove waste and excess substances.
- They adjust the amount of water and salts kept or removed.
- They form urine from the removed substances.
- They also help regulate blood pressure and other functions.

---

## The Functions of the Urinary Tract

Next the urinary tract, which handles the urine. It carries, stores, and expels it.

- The ureters carry urine from the kidneys to the bladder.
- The bladder stores the urine until it is convenient to expel it.
- The urethra carries the urine out of the body.
- The tract handles the urine without changing its content.

---

## Comparing the Two

Holding the two together lets us state the comparison cleanly, which the objective asks. They differ in making versus handling.

- The kidney makes urine, while the tract carries, stores, and expels it.
- The kidney changes the content of the fluid, while the tract does not.
- The kidney regulates the body's fluid and waste levels, while the tract controls when urine leaves.
- The two together produce and remove urine.

---

## Why This Comparison Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The comparison underlies urinary function and continence relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The kidneys' filtering underlies fluid and waste balance, relevant to exercise and hydration.
- The tract's storage and expulsion underlie continence, relevant to pelvic-health practice.
- Understanding the two parts clarifies how urine is made and handled.
- Kidney and bladder problems affect your patients differently.
- Understanding the comparison grounds your reasoning about the urinary system.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors blur the two, and naming them keeps them distinct. When unsure, return to a treatment plant and its delivery network.

- It is a mistake to think the urinary tract makes urine, since the kidneys make it.
- It is a mistake to believe the tract changes the urine's content, since it only handles it.
- It is a mistake to overlook the kidney's role in regulating fluid and waste.
- It is a mistake to forget that the bladder stores the urine.

## Key Takeaways

We have compared the kidney and the urinary tract. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The kidneys make urine by filtering the blood and adjusting its content.
- The urinary tract carries, stores, and expels the finished urine.
- The ureters carry, the bladder stores, and the urethra expels the urine.
- The kidney changes the fluid's content, while the tract does not.
- The kidneys regulate fluid and waste, while the tract controls when urine leaves.
