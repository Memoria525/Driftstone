## Compare and contrast the locations, innervation and functions of the internal urethral sphincter and external urethral sphincter

# The Internal and External Urethral Sphincters

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Compare and contrast the locations, innervation and functions of the internal urethral sphincter and external urethral sphincter.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The urethra is guarded by two sphincters, one automatic and one under voluntary control, that together control urination. Understanding them explains how continence is maintained. Today we compare and contrast the internal and external urethral sphincters.

- By the end of this session you will be able to locate the two sphincters.
- You will be able to contrast their innervation.
- You will be able to state their functions.
- You will be able to connect them to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: An Automatic Inner Sphincter and a Voluntary Outer Sphincter

The organising idea is that the urethra has an inner sphincter of smooth muscle under automatic control and an outer sphincter of skeletal muscle under voluntary control, together controlling urination. Automatic inner, voluntary outer. This idea of two complementary sphincters frames the comparison.

- The urethra is guarded by two sphincters.
- The internal urethral sphincter is smooth muscle under automatic control.
- The external urethral sphincter is skeletal muscle under voluntary control.
- Together they control urination.

---

## Our Master Analogy: An Automatic Valve Backed by a Hand-Held Valve

To keep the sphincters concrete, picture an automatic valve that closes by itself, backed by a second valve you can hold shut by hand. The internal sphincter closes automatically and the external sphincter is held voluntarily, and the image keeps the automatic and voluntary control in view.

- The automatic valve corresponds to the internal urethral sphincter.
- The hand-held valve corresponds to the external urethral sphincter.
- Holding the second valve shut corresponds to voluntary control of the external sphincter.
- The analogy breaks down in that valves are mechanical, whereas the sphincters are living muscle controlled by nerves.

---

## The Location and Innervation

Begin with location and innervation, since the two differ. One is inner and automatic, the other outer and voluntary.

- The internal urethral sphincter is at the neck of the bladder, made of smooth muscle.
- It is controlled by the autonomic nervous system and is not under voluntary control.
- The external urethral sphincter is lower down, made of skeletal muscle.
- It is controlled by the somatic nervous system and is under voluntary control.

---

## The Internal Sphincter's Function

Next the internal sphincter's function, which is automatic. It holds urine in the bladder.

- The internal urethral sphincter keeps the bladder neck closed.
- It holds urine in the bladder as it fills.
- It relaxes automatically when the bladder empties.
- Its automatic control keeps urine in without conscious effort.

---

## The External Sphincter's Function

Finally the external sphincter's function, which is voluntary. It lets urination be controlled.

- The external urethral sphincter is under voluntary control.
- It can be held closed to delay urination.
- It can be relaxed voluntarily to allow urination.
- It gives conscious control over when urination occurs.

---

## Why This Comparison Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The sphincters underlie urinary continence relevant to pelvic-health practice. Each point below connects the comparison to practice.

- The external sphincter's voluntary control lets urination be delayed, central to continence.
- Weakness of the external sphincter can cause incontinence you may help manage.
- Pelvic-floor exercises strengthen the voluntary external sphincter.
- Understanding the two sphincters clarifies how continence is maintained.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about urinary continence.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors blur the two, and naming them keeps them distinct. When unsure, return to an automatic valve backed by a hand-held valve.

- It is a mistake to think both sphincters are under voluntary control, since only the external is.
- It is a mistake to give the internal sphincter skeletal muscle, since it is smooth muscle.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the internal sphincter relaxes automatically when the bladder empties.
- It is a mistake to forget that the external sphincter can delay urination.

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Contrasting the two aloud is the strongest test.

- Locate the two sphincters from memory.

- The urethra is guarded by an internal and an external sphincter.
- The internal sphincter, at the bladder neck, is smooth muscle under automatic control.
