# The Gross Anatomy of the Urinary Tract

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Identify and describe the gross anatomy and location of the ureters, urinary bladder and urethra.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The urinary tract carries urine from the kidneys through the ureters to the bladder, where it is stored, and out through the urethra. Understanding its parts and their locations frames how urine is handled. Today we identify and describe the gross anatomy and location of the ureters, bladder, and urethra.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe the ureters.
- You will be able to describe the bladder.
- You will be able to describe the urethra.
- You will be able to connect the urinary tract to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Tubes That Carry, a Bag That Stores, and a Tube That Expels

The organising idea is that the urinary tract is made of the ureters that carry urine down, the bladder that stores it, and the urethra that carries it out. Carry, store, expel. This idea of carrying, storing, and expelling frames the topic.

- The ureters carry urine from the kidneys to the bladder.
- The bladder stores the urine.
- The urethra carries the urine out of the body.
- Together they handle the urine made by the kidneys.

---

## Our Master Analogy: Feed Pipes, a Holding Tank, and an Outlet Pipe

To keep the tract concrete, picture two feed pipes carrying fluid down into a holding tank, and an outlet pipe carrying it out when the tank empties. The ureters are the feed pipes, the bladder the holding tank, and the urethra the outlet pipe, and the image keeps the carry-store-expel arrangement in view.

- The feed pipes correspond to the ureters.
- The holding tank corresponds to the bladder.
- The outlet pipe corresponds to the urethra.
- The analogy breaks down in that pipes and a tank are plumbing, whereas the tract is living tissue that controls its own emptying.

---

## The Ureters

Begin with the ureters, which carry urine down. They run from the kidneys to the bladder.

- The ureters are two narrow tubes.
- Each carries urine from a kidney down to the bladder.
- They run down the back of the abdomen behind the peritoneum.
- They move the urine along by peristalsis.

---

## The Bladder

Next the bladder, which stores urine. It lies in the pelvis.

- The bladder is a muscular, expandable bag.
- It lies in the pelvis, behind the pubic bone.
- It stores the urine until it is convenient to expel it.
- It expands as it fills and contracts to empty.

---

## The Urethra

Finally the urethra, which carries urine out. It leads from the bladder to the outside.

- The urethra is a tube leading from the bladder to the outside of the body.
- It carries the urine out during urination.
- It is guarded by sphincters that control urination.
- Its length differs between the sexes.

---

## Why the Urinary Tract Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The urinary tract underlies urine handling and continence relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The bladder stores urine, and its control underlies continence, relevant to pelvic-health practice.
- The sphincters of the urethra control urination, relevant to continence.
- Understanding the tract clarifies how urine is handled.
- Bladder and continence problems are relevant to some patients you treat.
- Understanding the tract grounds your reasoning about the urinary system.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to feed pipes, a holding tank, and an outlet pipe.

- It is a mistake to think the ureters store urine, since the bladder stores it.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the ureters move urine by peristalsis.
- It is a mistake to forget that the bladder lies in the pelvis.
- It is a mistake to think the urethra is the same length in both sexes.

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Describing each part aloud is the strongest test.

- Describe the ureters from memory.
- Describe the bladder.
- Describe the urethra.
- Explain how urine moves through the tract.
- Relate the urinary tract to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

---

## Key Takeaways

We have described the gross anatomy of the urinary tract. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The ureters are two narrow tubes carrying urine from the kidneys to the bladder by peristalsis.
- The bladder is a muscular, expandable bag in the pelvis that stores urine.
- The urethra carries urine out of the body and is guarded by sphincters.
- The tract carries, stores, and expels the urine made by the kidneys.
- The bladder's control and the urethral sphincters underlie continence.
