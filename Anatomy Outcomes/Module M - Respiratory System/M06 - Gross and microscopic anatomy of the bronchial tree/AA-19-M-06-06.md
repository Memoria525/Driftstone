## Compare and contrast the effects of sympathetic and parasympathetic innervation on the bronchioles

# Autonomic Effects on the Bronchioles

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Compare and contrast the effects of sympathetic and parasympathetic innervation on the bronchioles.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The bronchioles are controlled by the autonomic nervous system, which widens them for exertion and narrows them for rest. Understanding these opposite effects explains how airflow is adjusted and why some medicines widen the airways. Today we compare and contrast the effects of sympathetic and parasympathetic innervation on the bronchioles.

- By the end of this session you will be able to state the sympathetic effect on the bronchioles.
- You will be able to state the parasympathetic effect.
- You will be able to contrast the two.
- You will be able to connect these effects to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: The Sympathetic Widens the Airways and the Parasympathetic Narrows Them

The organising idea is that the sympathetic division widens the bronchioles to increase airflow during exertion, while the parasympathetic division narrows them during rest. Sympathetic widens, parasympathetic narrows. This idea of opposing airway control frames the comparison.

- The bronchioles are controlled by the autonomic nervous system.
- The sympathetic division widens the bronchioles.
- The parasympathetic division narrows the bronchioles.
- The two adjust airflow to the body's needs.

---

## Our Master Analogy: An Accelerator That Opens the Airways and a Brake That Closes Them

To keep the effects concrete, picture an accelerator that opens the airways wide for action and a brake that closes them down for rest. The sympathetic division widens the bronchioles and the parasympathetic narrows them, and the image keeps the opposing control in view.

- The accelerator opening the airways corresponds to the sympathetic widening the bronchioles.
- The brake closing them corresponds to the parasympathetic narrowing them.
- Opening wide for action corresponds to increasing airflow during exertion.
- The analogy breaks down in that an accelerator and brake are used one at a time, whereas the two divisions are usually both active, with one dominating.

---

## The Sympathetic Effect

Begin with the sympathetic effect, which widens the airways. It prepares for exertion.

- The sympathetic division widens the bronchioles, called bronchodilation.
- This increases airflow to the lungs.
- It is part of the fight-or-flight response that prepares the body for exertion.
- Wider airways let more air reach the lungs during exercise.

---

## The Parasympathetic Effect

Next the parasympathetic effect, which narrows the airways. It fits the resting state.

- The parasympathetic division narrows the bronchioles, called bronchoconstriction.
- This decreases airflow to the lungs.
- It is part of the rest-and-digest state.
- Narrower airways fit the lower demand for air at rest.

---

## Comparing the Two

Holding the two together lets us state the comparison cleanly, which the objective asks. They have opposite effects.

- The sympathetic division widens the bronchioles, while the parasympathetic narrows them.
- The sympathetic increases airflow, while the parasympathetic decreases it.
- The sympathetic fits exertion, while the parasympathetic fits rest.
- The two together adjust airflow to the body's changing needs.

---

## Why These Effects Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The autonomic effects underlie airflow control and asthma treatment relevant to your patients. Each point below connects them to practice.

- The sympathetic widening of the airways increases airflow during exercise, relevant to exercise tolerance.
- Medicines that mimic the sympathetic effect widen the airways in asthma.
- Excessive narrowing of the bronchioles obstructs breathing, as in asthma.
- Understanding the effects clarifies how airflow is adjusted.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about airway control.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors blur the two, and naming them keeps them distinct. When unsure, return to an accelerator and a brake.

- It is a mistake to swap the two, since the sympathetic widens and the parasympathetic narrows the airways.
- It is a mistake to think the sympathetic narrows the airways, since it widens them.
- It is a mistake to overlook that airway-widening medicines mimic the sympathetic effect.
- It is a mistake to think only one division is active at a time.
