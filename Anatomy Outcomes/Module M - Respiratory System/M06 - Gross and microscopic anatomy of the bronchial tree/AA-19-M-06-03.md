# Pairing the Bronchi with the Lung Regions They Supply

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Pair each bronchus (main, lobar, segmental) with the general portion of lung it supplies (lung, lobe, bronchopulmonary segment).

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The branching of the airways matches the division of the lungs, so each level of bronchus supplies a corresponding portion of lung. Understanding this pairing explains how the lung is organised into units. Today we pair each bronchus with the portion of lung it supplies.

- By the end of this session you will be able to pair each bronchus with the lung region it supplies.
- You will be able to explain the matching of airways and lung divisions.
- You will be able to describe the bronchopulmonary segment.
- You will be able to connect the pairing to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Each Level of Bronchus Supplies a Matching Portion of Lung

The organising idea is that each level of the bronchial tree supplies a matching division of the lung, with the main bronchus supplying a whole lung, the lobar bronchus a lobe, and the segmental bronchus a bronchopulmonary segment. Airway level matches lung division. This idea of matched divisions frames the topic.

- Each level of bronchus supplies a matching portion of lung.
- The main bronchus supplies a whole lung.
- The lobar bronchus supplies a lobe.
- The segmental bronchus supplies a bronchopulmonary segment.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Supply Network Matching the Districts It Serves

To keep the pairing concrete, picture a supply network where a main line serves a whole city, a branch serves a district, and a sub-branch serves a neighbourhood, each level of pipe matching a level of the area. The bronchi match the lung divisions this way, and the image keeps the matched divisions in view.

- The main line serving the city corresponds to the main bronchus supplying a lung.
- The branch serving a district corresponds to the lobar bronchus supplying a lobe.
- The sub-branch serving a neighbourhood corresponds to the segmental bronchus supplying a segment.
- The analogy breaks down in that a supply network is planned, whereas the airways and lung divisions develop together.

---

## The Main Bronchus and the Lung

Begin with the largest pairing, the main bronchus and a whole lung. Each lung has one main bronchus.

- Each main bronchus supplies one whole lung.
- The right main bronchus supplies the right lung, and the left supplies the left lung.
- The main bronchus is the first branch of the airway into each lung.
- It carries all the air for that lung.

---

## The Lobar Bronchus and the Lobe

Next the lobar bronchus and a lobe. Each lobe has its own lobar bronchus.

- Each lobar bronchus supplies one lobe of a lung.
- The right lung has three lobes and the left has two, each with its own lobar bronchus.
- The lobar bronchus branches from the main bronchus.
- It carries all the air for its lobe.

---

## The Segmental Bronchus and the Bronchopulmonary Segment

Finally the segmental bronchus and a segment. Each segment has its own segmental bronchus.

- Each segmental bronchus supplies one bronchopulmonary segment.
- A bronchopulmonary segment is a defined portion of a lobe.
- The segmental bronchus branches from the lobar bronchus.
- Each segment is a functional unit that can be treated somewhat separately.

---

## Why This Pairing Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The pairing underlies the organisation of the lung relevant to respiratory care. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The bronchopulmonary segments are units used to target airway clearance and drainage to specific parts of the lung.
- Understanding the pairing helps you localise which part of the lung an airway serves.
- Positioning for drainage uses the segmental organisation of the lung.
- Understanding the pairing clarifies how the lung is organised into units.
- Understanding it grounds your reasoning about targeted respiratory care.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the pairing, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a supply network matching its districts.

- It is a mistake to mismatch the levels, since each bronchus supplies a specific portion of lung.
- It is a mistake to think the main bronchus supplies only a lobe, since it supplies a whole lung.
- It is a mistake to overlook the bronchopulmonary segment as a unit.
- It is a mistake to forget that the right and left lungs have different numbers of lobes.
