## Describe the major functions of the larynx

# The Major Functions of the Larynx

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the major functions of the larynx.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The larynx, or voice box, sits at the top of the lower airway and does three jobs: it keeps food out of the airway, it produces the voice, and it channels air. Understanding its functions frames its structure. Today we describe the major functions of the larynx.

- By the end of this session you will be able to list the major functions of the larynx.
- You will be able to describe its role in protecting the airway.
- You will be able to describe its role in producing the voice.
- You will be able to connect the larynx to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Gateway That Guards the Airway, Makes the Voice, and Channels Air

The organising idea is that the larynx guards the entrance to the lower airway, keeping food out, produces the voice, and channels air between the pharynx and the trachea. Guard, voice, and channel. This idea of a guarding, voice-making gateway frames the functions.

- The larynx sits between the pharynx and the trachea.
- It guards the airway, keeping food and drink out.
- It produces the voice through the vocal folds.
- It channels air to and from the lungs.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Gate With a Cover, a Reed, and an Open Passage

To keep the functions concrete, picture a gate fitted with a cover that closes to keep the wrong things out, a reed that vibrates to make sound when air passes, and an open passage for air. The larynx guards, produces the voice, and channels air this way, and the image keeps its three roles in view.

- The cover that closes corresponds to the larynx guarding the airway.
- The vibrating reed corresponds to the vocal folds producing the voice.
- The open passage corresponds to the larynx channelling air.
- The analogy breaks down in that a gate is mechanical, whereas the larynx is living tissue finely controlled by muscles.

---

## The Airway Protection Function

Begin with protection, the larynx's most vital role. It keeps food out of the airway.

- When we swallow, a flap called the epiglottis folds down over the larynx.
- This closes the airway so food and drink pass into the oesophagus, not the trachea.
- This protects the lungs from food and drink entering.
- Coughing, triggered at the larynx, expels anything that does enter.

---

## The Voice Production Function

Next voice production, the larynx's distinctive role. Air vibrates the vocal folds.

- The larynx contains the vocal folds, which produce sound.
- When air passes between them, they vibrate to make sound.
- The pitch and volume of the voice depend on the tension and position of the folds.
- This produces the voice used in speech.

---

## The Air Channelling Function

Finally the channelling of air, the larynx's role in the airway. It carries air between the pharynx and trachea.

- The larynx forms part of the airway between the pharynx and the trachea.
- Air passes through it on its way to and from the lungs.
- It keeps the airway open for breathing.
- It channels the air that also produces the voice.

---

## Why the Larynx Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The larynx underlies airway protection and voice relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The larynx protects the airway during swallowing, relevant to the risk of food entering the airway.
- Voice production is relevant to communication and to voice problems.
- The cough reflex at the larynx clears the airway, relevant to airway clearance.
- Understanding the larynx clarifies how the airway is guarded.
- Understanding it grounds your reasoning about the airway and voice.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors narrow the larynx's roles, and naming them keeps them complete. When unsure, return to a gate with a cover, a reed, and an open passage.

- It is a mistake to see the larynx only as the voice box, since it also protects the airway.
- It is a mistake to overlook the epiglottis's role in swallowing.
- It is a mistake to forget that the larynx channels air for breathing.
- It is a mistake to separate the cough reflex from the larynx.

## Consolidation and Self-Check

Because retrieval consolidates learning, close the slides and attempt these prompts before checking. Listing the functions aloud is the strongest test.

- List the major functions of the larynx from memory.
- Describe its role in protecting the airway.
- Describe its role in producing the voice.
- Explain how the epiglottis protects the airway.
- Relate one function to a physiotherapy-relevant concern.

---

## Key Takeaways

We have described the functions of the larynx. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The larynx sits between the pharynx and trachea.
- It guards the airway, with the epiglottis folding down during swallowing.
- It produces the voice through the vibrating vocal folds.
- It channels air to and from the lungs.
- Its protection of the airway and its cough reflex are relevant to airway care.
