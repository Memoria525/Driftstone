# The Vestibular and Vocal Folds Compared

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Compare and contrast the location, composition, and function of the vestibular folds (false vocal cords) and vocal folds (true vocal cords).

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The larynx contains two pairs of folds, one that produces the voice and one that protects the airway. Distinguishing them explains which folds make sound and which guard the airway. Today we compare and contrast the vestibular folds and the vocal folds.

- By the end of this session you will be able to locate the two pairs of folds.
- You will be able to describe the function of each.
- You will be able to contrast the two.
- You will be able to connect them to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Upper Folds That Protect and Lower Folds That Make the Voice

The organising idea is that the upper vestibular folds protect the airway, while the lower vocal folds produce the voice, so the two pairs have different jobs. Upper protect, lower voice. This idea of two folds with different roles frames the comparison.

- The larynx has two pairs of folds.
- The upper pair are the vestibular folds, or false vocal cords.
- The lower pair are the vocal folds, or true vocal cords.
- The vestibular folds protect the airway, and the vocal folds produce the voice.

---

## Our Master Analogy: An Upper Seal and a Lower Reed

To keep the folds concrete, picture a passage fitted with an upper seal that can close to protect it and a lower reed that vibrates to make sound. The vestibular folds protect and the vocal folds make the voice, and the image keeps the two roles in view.

- The upper seal corresponds to the vestibular folds protecting the airway.
- The lower reed corresponds to the vocal folds producing the voice.
- The closing to protect corresponds to the vestibular folds closing the airway.
- The analogy breaks down in that a seal and reed are separate parts, whereas both folds are part of the one larynx.

---

## The Vestibular Folds

Begin with the upper folds, which protect the airway. These are the false vocal cords.

- The vestibular folds are the upper pair of folds in the larynx.
- They are also called the false vocal cords.
- They can close to help protect the airway.
- They do not normally produce sound.

---

## The Vocal Folds

Next the lower folds, which produce the voice. These are the true vocal cords.

- The vocal folds are the lower pair of folds in the larynx.
- They are also called the true vocal cords.
- When air passes between them, they vibrate to produce sound.
- Their tension and position control the pitch and volume of the voice.

---

## Comparing the Two

Holding the two together lets us state the comparison cleanly, which the objective asks. They differ in location and function.

- The vestibular folds are the upper pair, and the vocal folds are the lower pair.
- The vestibular folds protect the airway, while the vocal folds produce the voice.
- The vocal folds vibrate to make sound, while the vestibular folds do not normally do so.
- Both are folds within the larynx but with different roles.

---

## Why This Comparison Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The folds underlie voice and airway protection relevant to your patients. Each point below connects the comparison to practice.

- The vocal folds produce the voice used in communication, relevant to voice problems.
- The vestibular folds help protect the airway, relevant to airway protection.
- Both close to raise pressure in the chest, as during effort.
- Understanding the folds clarifies how voice and airway protection work.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about the larynx.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors blur the two, and naming them keeps them distinct. When unsure, return to an upper seal and a lower reed.

- It is a mistake to think the vestibular folds produce the voice, since the vocal folds do.
- It is a mistake to place the vocal folds above the vestibular folds, since they are the lower pair.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the vestibular folds protect the airway.
- It is a mistake to confuse the false and true vocal cords.

We have compared the vestibular and vocal folds. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The larynx has an upper pair, the vestibular folds, and a lower pair, the vocal folds.
- The vestibular folds, or false vocal cords, help protect the airway.
- The vocal folds, or true vocal cords, vibrate to produce the voice.
