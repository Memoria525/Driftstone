## Describe the major functions of the respiratory system

# The Major Functions of the Respiratory System

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the major functions of the respiratory system.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The respiratory system supplies the oxygen every cell needs and removes carbon dioxide, and it also filters air, produces speech, and helps regulate acidity. Understanding its functions frames the whole of cardiorespiratory practice. Today we describe the major functions of the respiratory system.

- By the end of this session you will be able to list the major functions of the respiratory system.
- You will be able to describe its role in gas exchange.
- You will be able to describe its other functions.
- You will be able to connect these functions to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Supplying Oxygen and Removing Carbon Dioxide, and More

The organising idea is that the respiratory system's central function is gas exchange, supplying oxygen and removing carbon dioxide, while it also filters air, produces speech, and helps regulate blood acidity. Gas exchange plus support roles. This idea of a gas-exchange system with extra roles frames the functions.

- The respiratory system supplies oxygen to the blood.
- It removes carbon dioxide from the blood.
- It filters, warms, and moistens the incoming air.
- It also produces speech and helps regulate blood acidity.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Building's Ventilation Plant

To keep the functions concrete, picture a building's ventilation plant that draws in fresh air, filters and conditions it, delivers oxygen to where it is needed, and expels stale air. The respiratory system is this ventilation plant, and the image keeps supply, conditioning, and removal in view.

- Drawing in and delivering fresh air corresponds to supplying oxygen.
- Expelling stale air corresponds to removing carbon dioxide.
- Filtering and conditioning the air corresponds to cleaning, warming, and moistening it.
- The analogy breaks down in that a ventilation plant is machinery, whereas the respiratory system is living tissue that also produces speech.

---

## The Gas Exchange Function

Begin with gas exchange, the central function. It supplies oxygen and removes carbon dioxide.

- The respiratory system brings air into the lungs.
- In the lungs, oxygen passes from the air into the blood.
- Carbon dioxide passes from the blood into the air to be exhaled.
- This exchange supplies the oxygen the cells need and removes the waste carbon dioxide.

---

## The Air Conditioning Function

Next the conditioning of the air, which protects the lungs. The airways clean and prepare the air.

- The airways filter dust and particles from the incoming air.
- They warm the air to body temperature.
- They moisten the air so it does not dry the lungs.
- This conditioning protects the delicate lung tissue.

---

## The Other Functions

Finally the other functions, which are easily overlooked. The system also produces speech and regulates acidity.

- Air passing over the vocal folds produces the sound of speech.
- The removal of carbon dioxide helps regulate the acidity of the blood.
- The nose detects smells in the incoming air.
- These add to the system's central role of gas exchange.

---

## Why These Functions Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The respiratory functions underlie breathing and exercise tolerance central to your patients. Each point below connects a function to practice.

- Gas exchange supplies the oxygen that underlies exercise tolerance you assess and build.
- Airway clearance techniques support the filtering and clearing of the airways.
- Breathing retraining improves the efficiency of gas exchange.
- The system's role in acidity regulation is relevant to the body's response to exercise.
- Understanding the functions grounds your reasoning about breathing and exercise.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors narrow the system's roles, and naming them keeps them complete. When unsure, return to a building's ventilation plant.

- It is a mistake to see the respiratory system only as supplying oxygen, since it also removes carbon dioxide and does more.
- It is a mistake to overlook its role in conditioning the air.
- It is a mistake to forget its role in speech and in regulating acidity.
- It is a mistake to separate gas exchange from the breathing that supplies the air.
