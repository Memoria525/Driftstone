## Define the terms respiration, inhalation (inspiration), and exhalation (expiration)

# Defining Respiration, Inhalation, and Exhalation

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Define the terms respiration, inhalation (inspiration), and exhalation (expiration).

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The words for breathing are used loosely, but each has a precise meaning, and distinguishing them frames the whole study of the respiratory system. Understanding them clarifies what breathing in and out actually accomplish. Today we define the terms respiration, inhalation, and exhalation.

- By the end of this session you will be able to define respiration.
- You will be able to define inhalation and exhalation.
- You will be able to distinguish the movement of air from gas exchange.
- You will be able to connect these terms to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: Breathing Moves Air, and Respiration Exchanges Gases

The organising idea is that inhalation and exhalation move air in and out of the lungs, while respiration is the broader term covering the exchange of gases. Moving air versus exchanging gas. This idea of movement versus exchange frames the definitions.

- Inhalation is breathing air into the lungs.
- Exhalation is breathing air out of the lungs.
- Respiration is the broader process of gas exchange.
- Breathing moves the air that makes gas exchange possible.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Bellows Feeding Air to a Furnace

To keep the terms concrete, picture a bellows that draws air in and pushes it out, feeding a furnace where the air's oxygen is actually used. Inhalation and exhalation are the bellows drawing in and pushing out, and respiration is the broader process where the oxygen is used, and the image keeps movement versus use in view.

- The bellows drawing air in corresponds to inhalation.
- The bellows pushing air out corresponds to exhalation.
- The furnace using the oxygen corresponds to gas exchange in respiration.
- The analogy breaks down in that a bellows is worked by hand, whereas breathing is driven by the body's own muscles.

---

## Defining Inhalation and Exhalation

Begin with the two movements of air. These are inhalation and exhalation.

- Inhalation, also called inspiration, is the movement of air into the lungs.
- It occurs when the breathing muscles expand the chest.
- Exhalation, also called expiration, is the movement of air out of the lungs.
- It occurs when the breathing muscles relax and the chest recoils.

---

## Defining Respiration

Next respiration, the broader term. It covers gas exchange.

- Respiration, in its broad sense, is the exchange of gases.
- It includes the exchange of oxygen and carbon dioxide between the air and the blood in the lungs.
- It also includes the exchange between the blood and the tissues.
- It even includes the use of oxygen by the cells to release energy.

---

## Distinguishing Movement From Exchange

Finally the distinction between the terms, since this is the objective's point. Breathing enables respiration.

- Inhalation and exhalation are the physical movement of air.
- Respiration is the exchange and use of gases that the moving air makes possible.
- Breathing supplies the fresh air and removes the used air.
- Respiration is the purpose that breathing serves.

---

## Why These Terms Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

The terms underlie breathing and its assessment central to cardiorespiratory practice. Each point below connects them to practice.

- Inhalation and exhalation are the breathing movements you assess and retrain.
- Gas exchange in respiration is what breathing serves, central to exercise tolerance.
- Breathing retraining targets the movement of air to improve gas exchange.
- Understanding the terms clarifies what breathing accomplishes.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about breathing.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors blur the terms, and naming them keeps them distinct. When unsure, return to a bellows feeding a furnace.

- It is a mistake to use respiration to mean only breathing, since it also covers gas exchange.
- It is a mistake to confuse inhalation and exhalation.
- It is a mistake to think breathing and gas exchange are the same, since one enables the other.
- It is a mistake to overlook that respiration includes the cells' use of oxygen.
