## Describe the major functions of the trachea

# The Major Functions of the Trachea

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the major functions of the trachea.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The trachea is the main airway carrying air between the larynx and the lungs, held open by cartilage and lined by a mucus escalator that cleans the air. Understanding its functions frames its structure. Today we describe the major functions of the trachea.

- By the end of this session you will be able to list the functions of the trachea.
- You will be able to describe its role as an airway.
- You will be able to describe its role in cleaning the air.
- You will be able to connect the trachea to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: An Open Airway That Also Cleans the Air

The organising idea is that the trachea carries air to and from the lungs while keeping the airway open and cleaning the air with a mucus-and-cilia system. An open, self-cleaning airway. This idea of an open cleaning airway frames the functions.

- The trachea carries air between the larynx and the lungs.
- It stays open at all times to keep the airway clear.
- It cleans the air by trapping particles in mucus.
- Cilia sweep the mucus and trapped particles up and out.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Reinforced Duct With a Cleaning Conveyor

To keep the functions concrete, picture a reinforced duct that stays open to carry air, fitted with a sticky conveyor that traps dust and carries it back out. The trachea carries air and cleans it this way, and the image keeps the open airway and cleaning in view.

- The reinforced duct corresponds to the trachea kept open by cartilage.
- Carrying air corresponds to the trachea's airway role.
- The sticky conveyor corresponds to the mucus and cilia cleaning the air.
- The analogy breaks down in that a conveyor is mechanical, whereas the cleaning is done by living cilia and mucus.

---

## The Airway Function

Begin with the airway function, the trachea's main role. It carries air open at all times.

- The trachea carries air from the larynx down toward the lungs.
- It is held open by rings of cartilage so it never collapses.
- This keeps the airway clear for breathing at all times.
- Air passes through it on both inhalation and exhalation.

---

## The Cleaning Function

Next the cleaning function, which protects the lungs. Mucus and cilia clear the air.

- The trachea is lined by cells that produce mucus.
- The mucus traps dust and particles in the incoming air.
- Cilia on the lining cells sweep the mucus upward toward the throat.
- This carries the trapped particles up to be swallowed or coughed out.

---

## The Cough Function

Finally the cough, which clears the airway forcefully. The trachea participates in coughing.

- Irritation of the trachea can trigger a cough.
- A cough forces air out rapidly to clear the airway.
- This expels mucus and particles that need removing.
- The cough protects the lungs from material entering them.

---

## Why the Trachea Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The trachea underlies breathing and airway clearance relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The trachea is the main airway, so its patency is essential to breathing.
- The mucus escalator clears the airway, and airway clearance techniques support it.
- The cough clears the airway, relevant to airway clearance in respiratory care.
- Understanding the trachea clarifies how the airway is kept open and clean.
- Understanding it grounds your reasoning about the airway.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors narrow the trachea's roles, and naming them keeps them complete. When unsure, return to a reinforced duct with a cleaning conveyor.

- It is a mistake to see the trachea only as a passage, since it also cleans the air.
- It is a mistake to overlook the mucus escalator that sweeps particles up.
- It is a mistake to forget that the trachea stays open at all times.
- It is a mistake to separate the cough from the trachea's protective role.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the functions of the trachea. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The trachea carries air between the larynx and the lungs.
- It is held open by cartilage so the airway never collapses.
- It cleans the air by trapping particles in mucus swept up by cilia.
- A cough clears the airway forcefully when needed.
- The mucus escalator and cough are relevant to airway clearance.
