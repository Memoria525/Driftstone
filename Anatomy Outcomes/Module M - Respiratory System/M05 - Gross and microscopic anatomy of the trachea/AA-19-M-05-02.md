# The Gross Anatomy of the Trachea

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the gross anatomical features of the trachea, including its positioning with respect to the esophagus.

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The trachea is a tube running down the front of the neck and chest, lying just in front of the oesophagus, and ending by dividing into the two main bronchi. Understanding its gross features and its relationship to the oesophagus frames its role. Today we describe the gross anatomical features of the trachea and its position relative to the oesophagus.

- By the end of this session you will be able to describe the shape and course of the trachea.
- You will be able to describe its position relative to the oesophagus.
- You will be able to describe where it divides.
- You will be able to connect its anatomy to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: A Tube in Front of the Oesophagus That Divides Into Two Bronchi

The organising idea is that the trachea is a flexible tube running down in front of the oesophagus, held open by cartilage, that divides at its lower end into the two main bronchi. In front of the oesophagus, dividing in two. This idea of a dividing front tube frames the topic.

- The trachea is a tube running down the front of the neck and chest.
- It lies just in front of the oesophagus.
- It is held open by rings of cartilage.
- It divides at its lower end into the two main bronchi.

---

## Our Master Analogy: A Ribbed Hose Running in Front of a Soft Tube

To keep the anatomy concrete, picture a ribbed hose held open by its rings running down in front of a soft collapsible tube, the hose splitting into two at its end. The trachea runs in front of the oesophagus and divides into two bronchi, and the image keeps its position and division in view.

- The ribbed hose corresponds to the trachea held open by cartilage rings.
- The soft collapsible tube behind corresponds to the oesophagus.
- The hose splitting into two corresponds to the trachea dividing into the two main bronchi.
- The analogy breaks down in that a hose is inert, whereas the trachea is living tissue lined by a mucus escalator.

---

## The Shape and Course

Begin with the shape and course of the trachea. It runs down the front from the larynx.

- The trachea begins below the larynx in the neck.
- It runs down the front of the neck into the chest.
- It is held open by C-shaped rings of cartilage along its length.
- It is a flexible tube that moves slightly with breathing and neck movement.

---

## The Position Relative to the Oesophagus

Next its position relative to the oesophagus, which is a key relationship. The trachea lies in front.

- The trachea lies just in front of the oesophagus.
- The open part of its C-shaped cartilage rings faces backward, toward the oesophagus.
- This soft back allows the oesophagus behind to expand when food passes.
- The two tubes run down together, the airway in front and the food tube behind.

---

## Where the Trachea Divides

Finally where the trachea ends, since it divides into the bronchi. This is at its lower end.

- At its lower end, in the chest, the trachea divides into two.
- It forms the right and left main bronchi, one to each lung.
- The point of division is marked by a ridge of cartilage.
- The main bronchi carry air on into the lungs.

---

## Why the Trachea's Anatomy Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The trachea's anatomy underlies the airway relevant to your patients. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The trachea's position in the front of the neck is where it can be felt and where an emergency airway is placed.
- Its relationship to the oesophagus is relevant to swallowing and to the risk of food entering the airway.
- Its division into the bronchi begins the branching airway you reason about in respiratory care.
- Understanding its anatomy clarifies how air reaches the lungs.
- Understanding it grounds your reasoning about the airway.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to a ribbed hose in front of a soft tube.

- It is a mistake to place the trachea behind the oesophagus, since it lies in front.
- It is a mistake to think the cartilage rings are complete circles, since they are C-shaped with an open back.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the trachea divides into two main bronchi.
- It is a mistake to forget that the soft back allows the oesophagus to expand.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the gross anatomy of the trachea. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The trachea runs down the front of the neck and chest from the larynx.
- It lies just in front of the oesophagus.
- It is held open by C-shaped cartilage rings whose open back faces the oesophagus.
- The soft back lets the oesophagus expand when food passes.
- At its lower end the trachea divides into the two main bronchi.
