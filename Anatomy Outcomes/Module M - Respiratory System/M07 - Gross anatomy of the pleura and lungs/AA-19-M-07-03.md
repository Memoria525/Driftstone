## Describe the hilum of the lung, including the relationship of structures at this location

# The Hilum of the Lung

A lecture for masters-level physiotherapy students

Learning objective: Describe the hilum of the lung, including the relationship of structures at this location

---

## Where We Are Going Today

The hilum is the doorway of the lung, where the airway, blood vessels, and other structures enter and leave. Understanding it explains how the lung is connected to the rest of the body. Today we describe the hilum of the lung and the relationship of structures there.

- By the end of this session you will be able to define the hilum of the lung.
- You will be able to name the structures that enter and leave there.
- You will be able to describe their arrangement.
- You will be able to connect the hilum to physiotherapy practice.

---

## The Big Idea: The Doorway Where the Lung's Connections Enter and Leave

The organising idea is that the hilum is the region on the inner surface of each lung where the main bronchus, the blood vessels, and other structures enter and leave, bundled together. The lung's doorway. This idea of a connecting doorway frames the topic.

- The hilum is the region on the inner surface of each lung.
- It is where structures enter and leave the lung.
- These structures include the main bronchus and the blood vessels.
- They are bundled together at the hilum.

---

## Our Master Analogy: The Service Entrance Where All the Connections Meet

To keep the hilum concrete, picture the service entrance of a building where all the pipes, cables, and ducts enter and leave together in one bundle. The hilum is the lung's service entrance where its connections meet, and the image keeps the bundled doorway in view.

- The service entrance corresponds to the hilum.
- The pipes, cables, and ducts corresponds to the bronchus and blood vessels.
- The bundle at the entrance corresponds to the structures gathered at the hilum.
- The analogy breaks down in that a service entrance is built, whereas the hilum forms as the lung develops around its connections.

---

## The Location of the Hilum

Begin with where the hilum lies, since it is on the inner surface. It faces the centre of the chest.

- The hilum is on the inner, or medial, surface of each lung.
- It faces toward the centre of the chest, toward the heart.
- It is the only place where the lung is not covered by the sliding pleura.
- Here the visceral pleura is continuous with the parietal pleura.

---

## The Structures at the Hilum

Next the structures that pass through the hilum. Several enter and leave together.

- The main bronchus enters the lung at the hilum, carrying air.
- The pulmonary artery enters, carrying deoxygenated blood to the lung.
- The pulmonary veins leave, carrying oxygenated blood away from the lung.
- Nerves, lymphatic vessels, and the bronchial vessels also pass through the hilum.

---

## The Arrangement of the Structures

Finally the arrangement of the structures, since they have a consistent relationship. They are bundled in order.

- The structures at the hilum are bundled together.
- The bronchus, artery, and veins have a consistent general arrangement.
- They are surrounded by connective tissue that binds them.
- This bundle connects the lung to the airway and the circulation.

---

## Why the Hilum Matters in Physiotherapy Practice

The hilum underlies the lung's connections relevant to respiratory care. Each point below connects it to practice.

- The hilum is where the airway and blood vessels connect the lung to the body.
- Understanding the hilum clarifies how air and blood reach and leave the lung.
- The hilum is a landmark on chest imaging you may encounter.
- Conditions at the hilum can affect the airway and vessels together.
- Understanding the hilum grounds your reasoning about the lung's connections.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to the service entrance where connections meet.

- It is a mistake to place the hilum on the outer surface of the lung, since it is on the inner surface.
- It is a mistake to think only the bronchus passes through, since the vessels and other structures do too.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the pulmonary veins leave carrying oxygenated blood.
- It is a mistake to forget that the pleura is continuous at the hilum.

## Key Takeaways

We have described the hilum of the lung. If you keep only a few points, keep these.

- The hilum is the region on the inner surface of each lung where structures enter and leave.
- The main bronchus and pulmonary artery enter, and the pulmonary veins leave.
- Nerves, lymphatic vessels, and bronchial vessels also pass through the hilum.
- The structures are bundled together in a consistent arrangement.
- The hilum connects the lung to the airway and the circulation.
