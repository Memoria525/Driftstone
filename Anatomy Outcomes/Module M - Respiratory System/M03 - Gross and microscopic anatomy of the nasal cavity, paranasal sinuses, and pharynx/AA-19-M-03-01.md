## Where We Are Going Today

The first stops on the path of air, the nasal cavity, sinuses, and pharynx, condition the air and share the pharynx with the digestive tract. Understanding them frames how air is prepared before it reaches the lungs. Today we describe the functions, features, and lining of the nasal cavity, paranasal sinuses, and pharynx.

## The Big Idea: Structures That Condition the Air and Share a Passage With Food

The organising idea is that the nasal cavity and sinuses condition the incoming air, and the pharynx is a shared passage for air and food, all lined by epithelium suited to their roles. Conditioning and a shared passage. This idea of air conditioning and a shared route frames the topic.

- The nasal cavity filters, warms, and moistens the air.
- The paranasal sinuses are air-filled spaces that lighten the skull and add resonance.
- The pharynx is a shared passage for air and food.
- These structures are lined by epithelium suited to their functions.

---

## Our Master Analogy: An Air Intake With Filters, Resonating Chambers, and a Shared Junction

To keep the structures concrete, picture an air intake fitted with filters that clean and condition the incoming air, hollow chambers that add resonance, and a junction shared with another line. The nasal cavity conditions the air, the sinuses add resonance, and the pharynx is a shared junction, and the image keeps these roles in view.

- The filters conditioning the air correspond to the nasal cavity.
- The resonating chambers correspond to the paranasal sinuses.
- The shared junction corresponds to the pharynx shared with the digestive tract.
- The analogy breaks down in that an intake is machinery, whereas these structures are living tissue lined by specialised epithelium.

---

## The Nasal Cavity

Begin with the nasal cavity, which conditions the air. It is the main air conditioner.

- The nasal cavity is the space behind the nose through which air enters.
- Its walls have ridges that increase the surface the air contacts.
- It filters dust, warms the air, and moistens it.
- It is lined mostly by a ciliated epithelium that moves mucus and trapped particles.

---

## The Paranasal Sinuses

Next the paranasal sinuses, air-filled spaces in the skull. They lighten and resonate.

- The paranasal sinuses are air-filled spaces in the bones around the nasal cavity.
- They lighten the weight of the skull.
- They add resonance to the voice.
- They are lined by the same kind of ciliated epithelium and drain into the nasal cavity.

---

## The Pharynx

Finally the pharynx, the shared passage. It carries both air and food.

- The pharynx is the throat, a passage behind the nose and mouth.
- It is shared by the respiratory and digestive tracts.
- Air passes through it toward the larynx, and food passes through it toward the oesophagus.
- Its upper part is lined by ciliated respiratory epithelium and its lower part by a tougher epithelium suited to the passage of food.

---

## Why These Structures Matter in Physiotherapy Practice

These structures underlie air conditioning and shared passage relevant to your patients. Each point below connects them to practice.

- The nasal cavity conditions the air, relevant to breathing and to conditions such as congestion.
- The sinuses can become infected, a common upper respiratory condition.
- The shared pharynx is relevant to swallowing and to the risk of food entering the airway.
- Understanding these structures clarifies how air is prepared before the lungs.
- Understanding them grounds your reasoning about the upper airway.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Discard

A few errors distort the picture, and naming them keeps it accurate. When unsure, return to an air intake with filters, chambers, and a junction.

- It is a mistake to think the nasal cavity does no conditioning, since it filters, warms, and moistens the air.
- It is a mistake to overlook that the pharynx is shared with the digestive tract.
- It is a mistake to give the whole pharynx the same lining, since its parts differ.
- It is a mistake to forget that the sinuses drain into the nasal cavity.

- The paranasal sinuses lighten the skull and add resonance, and drain into the nasal cavity.
