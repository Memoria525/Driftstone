## Describe the four-stroke combustion cycle

# The Four-Stroke Combustion Cycle

A lecture for automotive technology students

Learning objective: Describe the four-stroke combustion cycle and the role of each stroke.

---

## Why This Matters

Almost every petrol and diesel car on the road converts fuel into motion using the same repeating sequence of four piston movements. Understand this cycle and you have the scaffold onto which fuel systems, ignition, valve timing, and emissions all hang.

- Each **stroke** is one full travel of the piston from top to bottom or bottom to top.
- Two full crankshaft rotations complete one cycle of all four strokes.
- The same cycle underlies engines from a small city car to a heavy truck.

---

## The Four Strokes

The cycle is often remembered as "suck, squeeze, bang, blow." Each stroke has a precise mechanical job, and the valves and spark are timed to it.

- **Intake:** the piston moves down, the intake valve opens, and an air-fuel mixture is drawn into the cylinder.
- **Compression:** both valves close and the piston rises, squeezing the mixture to raise its temperature and pressure.
- **Power:** the spark plug ignites the compressed mixture, and the expanding gases force the piston down. This is the only stroke that produces useful work.
- **Exhaust:** the piston rises again with the exhaust valve open, pushing spent gases out to the exhaust system.

---

## Key Takeaways

- The four-stroke cycle is intake, compression, power, and exhaust, in that order.
- Only the power stroke produces work; the other three prepare for or clean up after it.
- Valve and spark timing must be matched precisely to piston position for the engine to run.
