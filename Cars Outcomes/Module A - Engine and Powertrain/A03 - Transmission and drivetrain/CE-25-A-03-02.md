# Drivetrain Layouts: FWD, RWD, and AWD

A lecture for automotive technology students

Learning objective: Describe front-, rear-, and all-wheel-drive layouts and their handling effects.

---

## Which Wheels Get the Power

The drivetrain routes engine power to the wheels. Which wheels are driven strongly shapes traction and handling.

- **Front-wheel drive (FWD):** the driven wheels also steer. Compact, efficient, and stable, but can lose traction under hard acceleration.
- **Rear-wheel drive (RWD):** the rear wheels drive while the front wheels steer, balancing the two jobs and favouring performance.
- **All-wheel drive (AWD):** power goes to all four wheels for maximum traction in poor conditions, at the cost of weight and complexity.

---

## Key Takeaways

- FWD, RWD, and AWD describe which wheels receive engine power.
- The layout trades off efficiency, packaging, traction, and handling balance.
