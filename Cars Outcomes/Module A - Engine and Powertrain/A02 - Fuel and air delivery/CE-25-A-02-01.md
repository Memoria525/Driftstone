## Explain fuel injection versus carburetion

# Fuel Injection and Carburetion

A lecture for automotive technology students

Learning objective: Explain how fuel injection differs from carburetion and why it replaced it.

---

## Getting Fuel to the Cylinder

An engine needs the right ratio of air to fuel. Two technologies have historically metered that mixture.

- A **carburettor** uses the vacuum of incoming air to draw fuel through calibrated jets. It is purely mechanical and needs no electronics.
- **Fuel injection** sprays a precisely metered amount of fuel through electronically controlled injectors, guided by sensors.

---

## Why Injection Won

- Injection meters fuel far more precisely across temperatures and altitudes.
- Precise metering lowers emissions and improves fuel economy.
- Electronic control allows the engine to adapt in real time, which a fixed carburettor jet cannot.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Carburettors meter fuel mechanically; injectors meter it electronically.
- Fuel injection offers better economy, lower emissions, and adaptability, which is why modern cars use it.
