## Describe forced induction

# Forced Induction: Turbochargers and Superchargers

A lecture for automotive technology students

Learning objective: Describe how forced induction increases engine power.

---

## More Air, More Power

An engine's power is limited by how much air and fuel it can burn per cycle. Forced induction packs more air into each cylinder than atmospheric pressure alone could.

- A **turbocharger** uses a turbine driven by exhaust gases to spin a compressor that forces air in.
- A **supercharger** drives its compressor mechanically from the crankshaft with a belt.

---

## Trade-offs

- Turbochargers recover otherwise wasted exhaust energy but can suffer lag before boost builds.
- Superchargers respond instantly but consume engine power to drive themselves.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Forced induction increases the air packed into each cylinder, allowing more fuel to be burned.
- Turbos are exhaust-driven; superchargers are crankshaft-driven; each trades response against efficiency.
