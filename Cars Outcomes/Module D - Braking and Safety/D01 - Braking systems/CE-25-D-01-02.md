## Explain anti-lock braking

# Anti-lock Braking Systems (ABS)

A lecture for automotive technology students

Learning objective: Explain how ABS preserves steering control during hard braking.

---

## The Problem with Locked Wheels

If a wheel stops turning under heavy braking, the tyre slides and the driver loses the ability to steer. ABS prevents this.

- Wheel-speed sensors detect a wheel about to lock.
- The system rapidly releases and reapplies pressure to that wheel, many times per second.
- The tyre keeps rolling near the edge of grip, so steering is retained.

---

## Key Takeaways

- ABS stops wheels from locking by pulsing brake pressure automatically.
- Its main benefit is keeping the driver able to steer while braking hard.
