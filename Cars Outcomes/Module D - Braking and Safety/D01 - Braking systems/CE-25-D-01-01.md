## Compare disc and drum brakes

# Disc and Drum Brakes

A lecture for automotive technology students

Learning objective: Compare disc and drum brake designs and their trade-offs.

---

## Turning Motion into Heat

All friction brakes stop a car by converting its kinetic energy into heat. How well they shed that heat is a key difference.

- **Disc brakes** clamp a pair of pads onto a spinning rotor. They are exposed to airflow, so they cool well and resist fade under repeated hard use.
- **Drum brakes** press shoes outward against the inside of a spinning drum. They are enclosed, cheaper, and can double as a parking brake, but trap heat more easily.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Disc brakes clamp a rotor; drum brakes press shoes against a drum.
- Discs cool better and resist fade; drums are cheaper and common on rear axles.
