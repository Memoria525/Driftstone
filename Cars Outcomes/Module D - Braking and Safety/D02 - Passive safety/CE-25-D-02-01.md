# Passive Safety: Crumple Zones and Airbags

A lecture for automotive technology students

Learning objective: Describe how crumple zones and airbags protect occupants in a crash.

---

## Managing Crash Energy

Passive safety features do not prevent a crash but reduce injury when one happens. The goal is to slow occupants down as gently as possible.

- **Crumple zones** are areas of the body designed to fold and absorb energy, extending the time over which the car stops and lowering the forces on occupants.
- A rigid **safety cell** around the occupants resists deforming, preserving survival space.
- **Airbags** inflate in milliseconds to cushion the head and chest, working together with seatbelts.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Crumple zones absorb energy while a rigid cell protects occupants.
- Airbags and seatbelts work together to slow occupants safely.
