## Describe tyre construction and markings

# Tyre Construction and Markings

A lecture for automotive technology students

Learning objective: Describe how a tyre is built and how to read its sidewall markings.

---

## The Only Contact with the Road

Each tyre meets the road over an area roughly the size of a hand, so its construction and correct specification matter enormously for safety.

- The **tread** is the patterned rubber that grips and channels water away.
- Internal **plies and belts** give the tyre its shape and strength.
- The **sidewall** carries markings for size, load rating, and speed rating.

---

## Reading the Sidewall

- A code such as 205/55 R16 gives width in millimetres, aspect ratio, construction type, and rim diameter in inches.
- A load index and speed symbol indicate the maximum weight and speed the tyre is rated for.

---

## Key Takeaways

- A tyre is built from tread, plies, belts, and sidewall working together.
- Sidewall markings specify size, load, and speed limits that must match the vehicle.
