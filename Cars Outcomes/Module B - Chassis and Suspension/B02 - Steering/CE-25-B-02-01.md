## Explain rack-and-pinion steering

# Rack-and-Pinion Steering

A lecture for automotive technology students

Learning objective: Explain how a rack-and-pinion system steers a car.

---

## Turning Rotation into Sideways Motion

Steering must convert the rotation of the wheel in your hands into a sideways push on the road wheels. Rack-and-pinion does this simply and directly.

- A **pinion** gear on the steering column meshes with a flat toothed bar called the **rack**.
- Turning the wheel rotates the pinion, which slides the rack left or right.
- The rack connects through **tie rods** to the wheels, turning them.

---

## Power Assistance

- Hydraulic or electric assistance reduces the effort needed, especially at parking speeds.
- Electric assistance can vary its help with speed and even enable lane-keeping features.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Rack-and-pinion converts steering rotation into linear rack movement that turns the wheels.
- Power assistance lowers effort and enables modern driver aids.
