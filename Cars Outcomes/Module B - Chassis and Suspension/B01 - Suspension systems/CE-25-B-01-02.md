## Compare independent and solid-axle suspension

# Independent and Solid-Axle Suspension

A lecture for automotive technology students

Learning objective: Compare independent and solid-axle suspension designs.

---

## Are the Wheels Linked?

The key distinction is whether the two wheels on an axle move together or separately.

- In a **solid (beam) axle**, both wheels are connected by a rigid beam, so a bump on one side tilts the other. It is strong, simple, and cheap, favoured on trucks and off-roaders.
- In an **independent** suspension, each wheel moves on its own, so a bump on one side barely disturbs the other. It gives a smoother ride and better grip, favoured on most cars.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Solid axles link both wheels; independent suspension lets each move alone.
- Solid axles favour strength and load capacity; independent designs favour ride and handling.
