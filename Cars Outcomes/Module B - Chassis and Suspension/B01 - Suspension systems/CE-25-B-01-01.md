# Main Suspension Components

A lecture for automotive technology students

Learning objective: Identify the main suspension components and their functions.

---

## Isolating the Body from the Road

Suspension keeps the tyres in contact with the road while shielding occupants from bumps. A few core components do most of the work together.

- **Springs** absorb road shocks by compressing, storing energy so the wheel can move without jolting the body.
- **Dampers (shock absorbers)** control how quickly springs release that energy, preventing endless bouncing.
- **Control arms** locate the wheel and allow it to move up and down along a controlled path.
- **Anti-roll bars** link left and right wheels to reduce body lean in corners.

---

## Working as a System

- Springs and dampers must be matched: a stiff spring with a weak damper bounces, and the reverse feels harsh.
- The geometry set by the control arms determines how the wheel stays aligned as it moves.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Springs absorb shocks, dampers control the springs, and control arms locate the wheel.
- Suspension balances ride comfort against handling precision.
