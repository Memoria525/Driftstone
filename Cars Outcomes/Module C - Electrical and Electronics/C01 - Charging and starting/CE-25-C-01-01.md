## Describe the charging and starting system

# The Charging and Starting System

A lecture for automotive technology students

Learning objective: Describe the roles of the battery, starter, and alternator.

---

## Getting Going and Staying Powered

A petrol engine cannot start itself, and once running it needs a constant electrical supply. Three components handle this.

- The **battery** stores electrical energy and delivers the large burst of current needed to crank the engine.
- The **starter motor** uses that current to turn the engine fast enough to begin the combustion cycle.
- The **alternator** is driven by the running engine and generates electricity to power the car and recharge the battery.

---

## The Cycle of Supply

- At rest, the battery supplies everything.
- During starting, the battery feeds the starter motor.
- Once running, the alternator takes over and replenishes the battery.

---

## Key Takeaways

- The battery starts the car, the starter cranks the engine, and the alternator powers the car while running.
- A failure in any one strands the vehicle, so their health is closely linked.
