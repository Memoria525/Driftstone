## Explain the role of the engine control unit

# The Engine Control Unit and Its Sensors

A lecture for automotive technology students

Learning objective: Explain how the ECU uses sensor data to manage the engine.

---

## The Engine's Computer

Modern engines are managed by an **electronic control unit (ECU)**, a computer that reads sensors and adjusts engine operation many times per second.

- Sensors report air flow, engine temperature, throttle position, oxygen in the exhaust, and crankshaft position.
- The ECU uses these readings to decide how much fuel to inject and when to fire the spark.
- Feedback from the **oxygen sensor** lets the ECU fine-tune the mixture to keep emissions low.

---

## Why This Matters

- Continuous adjustment keeps the engine efficient across temperatures, loads, and altitudes.
- Fault codes stored by the ECU help technicians diagnose problems quickly.

---

## Key Takeaways

- The ECU reads many sensors and controls fuelling and ignition in real time.
- Closed-loop feedback from the oxygen sensor keeps the mixture efficient and clean.
