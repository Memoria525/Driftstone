# Content Expansion Prompt

You are helping me build study content for a college-level Anatomy & Physiology flashcard app. I'll give you a chapter outline with numbered sections. Your job is to expand one section at a time into a comprehensive, exhaustive set of bullet points.

## Here is the chapter outline:

Unit 3 — Regulation and Integration
Chapter 13 — The Central Nervous System
	1.	Overview of the central nervous system
	2.	Brief history of the study of the brain and spinal cord
	3.	Functions of the CNS
	4.	Development of the CNS: neural tube formation, primary and secondary brain vesicles, and their adult derivatives
	5.	Protection of the CNS: bony encasement and the meninges (dura mater, arachnoid mater, pia mater)
	6.	Cerebrospinal fluid and the blood-brain barrier: production, circulation, absorption, and CNS fluid homeostasis
	7.	Overview of brain organization: major regions and their relationships
	8.	The cerebrum: surface anatomy, lobes, and fissures
	9.	Cerebral cortex: functional areas (motor, sensory, association)
	10.	Cerebral white matter: association, commissural, and projection tracts
	11.	Lateralization and hemispheric specialization
	12.	Basal nuclei (basal ganglia): structure and function
	13.	The limbic system: components and role in emotion and memory
	14.	The diencephalon: thalamus, hypothalamus, and epithalamus
	15.	The brainstem: midbrain, pons, medulla oblongata, and the reticular formation
	16.	The cerebellum: structure and function
	17.	The spinal cord: gross anatomy, cross-sectional organization, and ascending (sensory) and descending (motor) tracts
	18.	Reflexes and reflex arcs: components, classification, and specific spinal reflexes (stretch, tendon, withdrawal, crossed extensor)
	19.	Higher-order brain functions: consciousness, memory, and learning
	20.	Effects of aging on the central nervous system

## How we'll work

1. I'll tell you which numbered section to expand.
2. You produce a thorough set of bullet points covering everything a college A&P student would need to know for that section.
3. **Use the rest of the chapter outline to make scoping decisions.** If a concept belongs more naturally in another numbered section, leave it there — don't duplicate it here. Call out any scoping decisions you made.
4. I'll review, and we'll go back and forth until I'm happy with the content.
5. Once approved, I'll switch to a card generation prompt to create flashcards from the approved bullet points.
6. After cards are done, we'll return here and I'll ask for the next section.

## Guidelines

- **Comprehensive** — cover everything expected at a college A&P exam level
- **Precise** — correct terminology, chemical symbols where relevant (e.g., Carbon Dioxide (CO₂))
- **College-level depth** — not graduate or medical school
- **Structured** — group related bullets under subtopic headings if the section is large enough to warrant it
- **I have final say** — if I cut something, it's cut; if I add something, it's in

Start with the first numbered section now.
