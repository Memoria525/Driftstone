# Content Expansion Prompt

You are helping me build study content for a college-level Anatomy & Physiology flashcard app. I'll give you a chapter outline with numbered sections. Your job is to expand one section at a time into a comprehensive, exhaustive set of bullet points.

## Here is the chapter outline:

Unit 4 — Maintenance of the Body
Chapter 23 — The Digestive System
	1.	Overview and history of the study of digestion
	2.	Functions of the digestive system
	3.	Anatomy of the digestive system: alimentary canal and accessory organs
	4.	Histology of the alimentary canal wall: mucosa, submucosa, muscularis externa, and serosa/adventitia
	5.	The peritoneum: parietal and visceral layers, peritoneal cavity, mesenteries, and retroperitoneal organs
	6.	The enteric nervous system and motility: peristalsis, segmentation, and migrating motor complexes
	7.	The oral cavity: anatomy, tongue, teeth, salivary glands, and saliva composition and function
	8.	The pharynx and esophagus: anatomy, swallowing (deglutition), and the lower esophageal sphincter
	9.	The stomach: gross anatomy, histology, gastric glands, gastric secretions, and regulation of gastric activity
	10.	The small intestine: duodenum, jejunum, ileum, structural adaptations for absorption (villi, microvilli, plicae circulares), and intestinal secretions
	11.	The liver, gallbladder, and bile: anatomy, histology, bile composition, secretion, and enterohepatic circulation
	12.	The pancreas: exocrine anatomy, pancreatic juice composition, and regulation of secretion
	13.	The large intestine: cecum, colon, rectum, anal canal, histological features, and bacterial flora
	14.	Chemical digestion and absorption: enzymatic breakdown and absorption of carbohydrates, proteins, lipids, nucleic acids, water, electrolytes, and vitamins
	15.	Neural and hormonal control of digestion: gastrin, secretin, cholecystokinin, GIP, and the cephalic, gastric, and intestinal phases
	16.	Defecation: the defecation reflex
	17.	Common digestive disorders
	18.	Effects of aging on the digestive system

## How we'll work

1. I'll tell you which numbered section to expand.
2. You produce a thorough set of bullet points covering everything a college A&P student would need to know for that section.
3. **Use the rest of the chapter outline to make scoping decisions.** If a concept belongs more naturally in another numbered section, leave it there — don't duplicate it here. Call out any scoping decisions you made.
4. I'll review, and we'll go back and forth until I'm happy with the content.
5. Once approved, I'll switch to a card generation prompt to create flashcards from the approved bullet points.
6. After cards are done, we'll return here and I'll ask for the next section.

## Guidelines

- **Comprehensive** — cover everything expected at a college A&P exam level
- **Precise** — correct terminology, chemical symbols where relevant (e.g., Carbon Dioxide (CO₂))
- **College-level depth** — not graduate or medical school
- **Structured** — group related bullets under subtopic headings if the section is large enough to warrant it
- **I have final say** — if I cut something, it's cut; if I add something, it's in
