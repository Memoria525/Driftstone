# Content Expansion Prompt

You are helping me build study content for a college-level Anatomy & Physiology flashcard app. I'll give you a chapter outline with numbered sections. Your job is to expand one section at a time into a comprehensive, exhaustive set of bullet points.

## Here is the chapter outline:

Unit 2 — Support and Movement
Chapter 10 — Muscle Tissue
	1.	Overview of muscle tissue
	2.	Brief history of myology
	3.	Functions of muscle tissue
	4.	Properties of muscle tissue: excitability, contractility, extensibility, and elasticity
	5.	Types of muscle tissue: skeletal, cardiac, and smooth
	6.	Skeletal muscle as an organ: connective tissue coverings, blood supply, and innervation
	7.	The skeletal muscle fiber: sarcolemma, sarcoplasm, myofibrils, sarcomere structure, the sliding filament model, and muscle proteins (contractile, regulatory, and structural)
	8.	The contraction cycle: neuromuscular junction, excitation-contraction coupling, the cross-bridge cycle, muscle relaxation, and types of contraction (isotonic and isometric)
	9.	Neural control of muscle force: motor units, recruitment, muscle twitch, summation, and tetanus
	10.	Muscle metabolism, fatigue, and skeletal muscle fiber types
	11.	Cardiac muscle: structure and functional characteristics
	12.	Smooth muscle: structure and functional characteristics
	13.	Muscle development and regeneration: myoblast fusion, satellite cells, hypertrophy vs. hyperplasia, and comparative regenerative capacity across muscle types
	14.	Effects of aging on muscle tissue
	15.	Muscle disorders: atrophy, dystrophy, and myasthenia gravis

## How we'll work

1. I'll tell you which numbered section to expand.
2. You produce a thorough set of bullet points covering everything a college A&P student would need to know for that section.
3. **Use the rest of the chapter outline to make scoping decisions.** If a concept belongs more naturally in another numbered section, leave it there — don't duplicate it here. Call out any scoping decisions you made.
4. I'll review, and we'll go back and forth until I'm happy with the content.
5. Once approved, I'll switch to a card generation prompt to create flashcards from the approved bullet points.
6. After cards are done, we'll return here and I'll ask for the next section.

## Guidelines

- **Comprehensive** — cover everything expected at a college A&P exam level
- **Precise** — correct terminology, chemical symbols where relevant (e.g., Carbon Dioxide (CO₂))
- **College-level depth** — not graduate or medical school
- **Structured** — group related bullets under subtopic headings if the section is large enough to warrant it
- **I have final say** — if I cut something, it's cut; if I add something, it's in

Start with the first numbered section now.
